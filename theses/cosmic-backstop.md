# Seed: the cosmic backstop

**Status:** tuning harness for v1 — the first real target the system is built against.

## The question as the author posed it
Is there a cosmic backstop for knowledge and/or morality that we can have access to?

By "cosmic backstop" the author means something like a mind-independent ground that
guarantees our knowledge or morality is tracking something real — and the second
clause matters: even if such a ground exists, can we *access* it, or is it sealed
off from us?

## Notes for the framer
This is a topic, not yet a thesis. Several distinct theses live inside it, and the
framer should pick or sharpen one before the council runs. Candidates:

- **Conjunctive vs separable:** knowledge and morality may not stand or fall
  together. There could be a backstop for one and not the other. The framer should
  decide whether to run them as one thesis or split into two runs.
- **Existence vs access:** the realist about a backstop still owes an access story
  (the naturalist's "what's the causal contact?" bites here); the anti-realist owes
  an account of why knowledge and morality seem to need one. These are different
  cruxes.
- **The dichotomy itself:** "either a cosmic backstop or mere subjective
  projection" is exactly the kind of forced choice the phenomenologist and
  contemplative are seated to question. Watch whether the thesis smuggles it in.

## Why it's a good seed
It is genuinely contestable — the four fixed council voices land in four different
places almost by construction:
- **Naturalist:** no backstop outside the natural order; "access" to non-natural
  facts has no causal story. Knowledge and morality are natural phenomena needing
  no cosmic guarantee.
- **Rationalist:** reason's own structure and the bindingness of norms are the
  backstop, accessed a priori, not causally.
- **Phenomenologist:** the question presupposes a subject/object split; meaning and
  norms are constituted in being-in-the-world, so "backstop vs projection" is a
  false choice.
- **Contemplative (Madhyamaka):** the demand for a self-existent ground is the
  error; knowledge and morality function conventionally without an ultimate
  backstop, and that is not nihilism.

A possible **specialist to summon:** a moral realist of the non-naturalist stripe
(e.g. a Parfit- or Enoch-style robust realist) to voice the strongest case that
there is an accessible normative backstop — the position the naturalist most needs
a live opponent for.

## What a good run looks like
The author is a pragmatist with a stake here. A successful run does not tell the
author whether there is a backstop. It locates the single premise the whole dispute
turns on — most likely some version of *whether "access" requires causal contact* —
and tells the author exactly what they'd have to accept to keep their own view.
