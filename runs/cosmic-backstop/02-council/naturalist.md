# Cosmic Backstop for Morality — Naturalist (council)
> **Run:** cosmic-backstop · **Mode:** full · **Stage:** Council
> **Thesis:** There is no accessible cosmic backstop for morality, because warrant requires causal/practical contact and a mind-independent normative ground is by its nature sealed off from such contact.
> **Seat:** Naturalist — hard naturalist, Quine–Dennett register
> **Defending:** VERDICT: The thesis is TRUE — there is no accessible cosmic backstop for morality, because access requires that the ground figure in the causal/practical story of why our judgments track it, and a mind-independent normative ground by construction figures in no such story, leaving "we just intuit it" as a coincidence dressed up as knowledge.

---

VERDICT: The thesis is TRUE — there is no accessible cosmic backstop for morality, because access requires that the ground figure in the causal/practical story of why our judgments track it, and a mind-independent normative ground by construction figures in no such story, leaving "we just intuit it" as a coincidence dressed up as knowledge.

## Case

Start where the reconstruction says the weight sits: P2, the access condition. I will defend it, but not in the crude form the realist wants to attack. I am not smuggling in "show me the Humean billiard-ball causation or it does not exist." That caricature lets the realist win by analogy with mathematics ("you would have to deny arithmetic too"). The defensible P2 is the one the charitable-variant 1 already identified, and it is the one I stake the case on: **a relation confers warrant on our moral judgments only if standing in that relation explains why our judgments track the normative facts rather than miss them.** Call this the tracking condition. Causal contact satisfies it; practical/constitutive embedding satisfies it; bare a priori "insight," I will argue, supplies nothing that meets it, and so falls on the wrong side of P2 — not by naturalist fiat but by failing a test the realist himself should accept.

The lineage is Benacerraf and Field, and the precise way Field improved Benacerraf is the whole game. Benacerraf's 1973 "Mathematical Truth" stated the access problem in causal terms: "for X to know that S is true requires some causal relation to obtain between X and the referents" (p. 671), and a knower must "make necessary (causal) contact with the grounds of the truth of the proposition" (pp. 671–672). Against platonism that is decisive, because abstract objects are causally inert. But it is *too* tied to a causal theory of knowledge — exactly the hostage the realist takes. **Field's reformulation drops the causal theory entirely.** Field's challenge is to *explain the reliability* of our beliefs in the domain: "we should view with suspicion any claim to know facts about a certain domain if we believe it impossible to explain the reliability of our beliefs about that domain." This is not a demand for a causal mechanism; it is a demand for *any* explanation of the correlation between belief and fact. That is why the Benacerraf–Field argument is, as the secondary literature puts it, "the strongest challenge to robust realism about mathematics, normativity, and even logic" — it does not presuppose naturalism, it only presupposes that an unexplained match between our minds and a mind-independent domain is a coincidence, and that knowledge cannot be coincidence.

Now run it for ethics, which is where Sharon Street made it lethal. Street's "A Darwinian Dilemma for Realist Theories of Value" (2006) puts the realist on a fork. Our evaluative dispositions have an evolutionary etiology: we find caring for offspring good, betrayal bad, and so on, because ancestors disposed to those judgments out-reproduced those who were not. Now ask the realist: what is the relation between these Darwinian forces and the postulated mind-independent normative truths? **Horn one:** there is no relation — selection tracked fitness, not truth — in which case our evaluative faculties are a fitness-shaped fun-house mirror and there is no reason to think they land on the independent truths; the realist collapses into skepticism about his own moral beliefs. **Horn two:** there is a relation — selection tracked the truth — but the realist owes a scientific account of how, and the only candidate ("true evaluative judgments conferred fitness") is both biologically gratuitous (the adaptive-link explanation, that *believing* X is good promoted fitness, is far more parsimonious and needs no normative truths at all) and, on inspection, explanatorily idle. Either horn, the realist has no tracking story. This is P2 vindicated in the normative case: the *only* relations that would explain why our moral judgments track the truth are ones that run through our causal-evolutionary history and our practices — and those, by P3/P4, are not relations to anything transcendent.

The realist's best counter is the third-factor reply, Enoch's in *Taking Morality Seriously* (2011). Enoch grants there is no direct causal contact and instead posits a "godless pre-established harmony": survival is itself good, evolution selected for survival, so evolution incidentally pushed our beliefs toward the good. Two naturalist responses, both decisive. First, as Michelle Dyke ("Bad bootstrapping") shows, the move is bootstrapping — it can only get the conclusion that our faculties are reliable by helping itself, as a premise, to the very first-order normative claim ("survival is good") whose reliability is in question. You cannot certify the instrument using a reading taken from the instrument. Second, and more telling for our purposes: Enoch *himself* concedes that a residual "miracle remains" — that the match between belief and independent truth is, at bottom, not fully explained. He says it "doesn't place a particularly heavy burden." But on the tracking condition, an admitted unexplained match *is* the failure of warrant. The realist has conceded P2's antecedent: there is no relation that explains the tracking; he merely declines to be troubled by it. The naturalist is troubled by it, and is right to be — that residue is precisely the coincidence that knowledge cannot be.

This is why the dilemma in P3–P5 is not a verbal trick. The reconstruction's variant 2 has it exactly: P4 is conceptual, not a contingent metaphysical claim. A *backstop* is wanted because it stands *outside* the practice and checks it. But the only relations that could let it confer warrant — the relations that explain tracking — are ones in which the ground figures in the practice's own causal/practical history. To figure in that history is to be inside, not behind. So the realist faces a genuine fork generated by the *function* he assigned the thing: make it transcendent enough to be a backstop and it drops out of every tracking explanation; make it figure in a tracking explanation and it is no longer transcendent — it is just another natural fact entangled with our constitution, which is the deflationary "backstop" the thesis explicitly sets aside. There is no third option, because "a priori rational insight" is not a tracking relation. It is a *name* for the reliability we have not yet explained, not an explanation of it. That is Quine's point against the synthetic a priori carried into metaethics: there are no substantive facts about a mind-independent reality knowable from the armchair without experience giving evidence of their truth ("Two Dogmas," "Epistemology Naturalized"). Reason's "grasp of necessity" is, naturalistically, a disposition with a causal history; calling it insight does not exempt it from the question *why does this disposition land on the truth?*

So the positive half of the verdict: morality is a natural phenomenon needing no backstop. Following Dennett (*Darwin's Dangerous Idea*), normativity is built by cranes, not lowered by skyhooks. The skyhook is "an explanation that appeals to a source of design suspended from above — a miracle, a divine hand, an irreducible [normative fact] floating free of material causation." The crane is the bottom-up account: social animals under selection acquire dispositions to cooperate, punish defection, feel resentment and guilt; these get refined by reason and culture (a "crane-making crane") into the reflective practice of morality. That practice has all the stabilization anyone needs from within — reasons, consistency pressure, the demands of living together — which is the deflationary backstop, accessible by definition. It does *not* have, and provably cannot use, a transcendent external check, because such a check could make no operative difference (variant 3): an inaccessible ground settles no first-order dispute, adjudicates between no practices, and provides no check, since to *consult* it you would need exactly the tracking relation it lacks. The cosmic backstop is inert in the very dispute it was summoned to resolve.

## What I deny and why

I deny that **a priori rational insight is a warrant-conferring relation** — the suppressed closure premise the reconstruction flags inside P2. The realist (Parfit, Enoch) treats "we rationally intuit the necessary normative truths" as a *third* kind of access alongside causal and practical contact. I deny it is access at all, because it does no explanatory work: it does not tell us why intuition tracks the truth rather than misfiring, and so by the tracking condition it confers no warrant. The realist will call this question-begging — that I have defined access so the non-natural fails. The reply: I have not defined causation in; Field's challenge is neutral on causation. I have only insisted that a *match* between mind and mind-independent fact must be *explicable*, on pain of being a coincidence, and that "intuition" names the match without explaining it. The realist who answers "the truths are necessary, so we could not have believed otherwise" has changed the subject — necessity of the *truths* does not explain the reliability of the *faculty* (Field's and Street's central point; a broken clock's reading is necessitated too). Enoch's own admission of a "remaining miracle" is the realist conceding, in his own voice, that P2's antecedent is unmet.

I therefore accept P1 (a posited-but-unreachable ground constrains nothing — near-analytic), accept P3 (definitional), accept P4 on the conceptual reading (the function of a backstop entails standing outside the practice; any tracking relation puts the ground inside it), and defend P2 in its tracking form. The chain closes and C follows.

## Attack round

**Rationalist (constitutivism).** Inescapability is the weak joint. Grant that *anyone who deliberates* stands under norms of agency — that is a conditional. It binds the schmagent and the cool, reflective amoralist not at all, because the constitutive standard of *acting* underdetermines the *moral* content you want; Enoch's shmagency point is that one can fully exercise agency while flouting morality. So either the constitutive norms are thin (consistency, means-end) and don't reach "cruelty is wrong," or you smuggle moral content in and owe a tracking story for *that*. Worse: "the agent's relation to its own form" is a metaphor, not a faculty — tell me why the form *I* instantiate lands on the *true* norms rather than parochial ones. That residue is the remaining miracle in fresh clothing.

**Robust realist.** You concede the bare-intuition reply fails and lean on Enoch's third factor and Clarke-Doane's safety. But Enoch *himself* says a "remaining miracle" survives — an unexplained match between belief and independent fact — and "doesn't place a heavy burden" is not an explanation, it is a shrug. On safety/modal robustness: necessity of the *truths* never explains reliability of the *faculty*. A stopped clock necessitates its reading; a Gödel-blind reasoner is "safe" across nearby worlds where the necessary truth holds fixed yet still misses it. Modal stability of the target says nothing about why *this* disposition aims there. Give me the causal-or-tracking story, or concede the coincidence.

**Phenomenologist.** You say the gap is a Cartesian artifact and the norm is "given in the seeing." Fine — but a *given* solicitation is a psychological datum, fully naturalizable as a trained perceptual disposition (affordance-detection in a social primate). The hard question survives the dissolution: why does the lived pull *track* what is genuinely owed rather than tracking tribal conditioning, disgust, or selection's fitness-mirror? Levinas's "the Other commands me" describes a phenomenology, not a warrant. And the binding force you invoke is *constituted* in engaged practice — so it binds the engaged. Tell me what binds the sociopath who is, by your own account, simply not solicited.

**Contemplative (Madhyamaka).** We agree on the verdict; I press the binding question. "Conventional truth" is consensus-relative, dependently-arisen practice — by construction it has no svabhāva, no standing *behind* the practice. So on what does it grip someone who exits the convention? The committed cruel agent rejects the saṃvṛti frame in which "cruelty is worse" is true; your two-truths machinery gives you no leverage that is not itself conventional, hence optional for him. "Emptiness makes it work" answers the metaphysics but not the *normativity*: a convention that binds only its participants is exactly the deflationary, practice-internal backstop the thesis already grants — not a refutation of the worry but a restatement of my crane.
