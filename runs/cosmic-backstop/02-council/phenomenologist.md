# Phenomenologist — blind draft

VERDICT: The thesis is ill-posed, because P4 (and the P1/P2 "access" picture feeding it) presupposes a knower-here / ground-there gap that lived moral experience never contains — moral norms are constituted in our engaged being-in-the-world, neither a mind-independent backstop we "make contact" with nor a projection we throw outward, so the whole "contact-or-nothing" frame is a Cartesian artifact rather than a real fork.

## Case

Start where the thesis refuses to start: with how a moral norm actually shows up before any theory of it. I do not first perceive a neutral situation — a man slumped on the pavement, a child reaching for a flame — and then, in a second step, *consult* a ground and *check* whether my judgment makes "contact" with it. The pull to help is already there *in* the seeing. The situation shows up as soliciting, demanding, to-be-responded-to. The norm is not an object I reach across a gap to grasp; it is a feature of how the world is disclosed to an engaged being who is already in it. This is the datum the thesis has to explain away, and its vocabulary cannot.

The reason it cannot is that P1–P2 and especially P4 are built on a stage set Heidegger spent *Being and Time* dismantling: a subject on one side, a ground or object on the other, and "access" as a relation that must be established *across* the gap between them. Heidegger's claim is that this picture inverts the real order. Our primary way of being is not a detached subject staring at present-at-hand objects and wondering how to contact them; it is *being-in-the-world* — already absorbed, already coping, already in the midst of a meaningful whole. The carpenter using the hammer has "no awareness of himself as a subject over and against a world of objects"; his circumspective dealing is, in Heidegger's phrase, "non-subject-object in form" (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, "Martin Heidegger"). The detached, contemplative stance that asks "do I have *contact* with the thing?" is not the bedrock. It is a *deficient*, *founded* mode — it arises only when smooth engagement breaks down and the world goes "present-at-hand." The thesis takes this derivative, breakdown stance and treats it as the universal form of all access to normative reality. That is the smuggled metaphysics: it has already decided that to relate to a norm is to be a spectator-subject reaching for an occurrent object.

Once you see that, P4 collapses as a *dichotomy*. P4 says: whatever is in contact with us is thereby part of the natural/practical order (entangled with us), and so cannot be a transcendent backstop. This trades on an exhaustive partition — *either* something stands outside our practices and is therefore unreachable, *or* it is reachable and therefore merely "ours." But that partition is exactly the inside/outside, subject/object geometry that engaged experience does not have. The norm that grips me in the face of the suffering stranger is neither "outside" my situation (a Platonic fact I must causally or rationally bridge to) nor "inside" me as a private projection I cast onto a value-neutral world. It is constituted at the meeting point — in the structure of my being-with others in a shared world. Merleau-Ponty's body-subject is the model here: motor intentionality is "not mental representation but skillful bodily responsiveness... in direct engagement with the world" (Cambridge Companion / *Phenomenology of Perception*). I do not represent the cliff-edge and then infer danger; my body already shrinks from it. Value is given *in* the perceiving, as the world's solicitation, before the subject/object split is drawn. So "is the value out there or in here?" — the very question P4 forces — is a question the phenomenon does not answer because it was never posed in those terms by the lived case.

Levinas drives the point home for the specifically *moral* case, which is what this run restricts itself to. The normative is not something I access; it is what *accesses me*. In the face-to-face encounter the Other "speaks to me, implores or commands me," and this demand arrives "at a precognitive level," "prior to any subject-object relation" and prior to all theorizing (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, "Emmanuel Levinas"). Levinas calls ethics *first philosophy* precisely to reverse the order the thesis assumes: obligation does not wait on an epistemology that first secures contact with a ground and then licenses the judgment. The command is already binding *before* the question "but do I have warrant-conferring contact with its source?" can even be raised. Diane Perpich's gloss — "normativity without norms" — names exactly the phenomenon P1 cannot accommodate: a bindingness that is not the deliverance of a *posited ground I reach* but the very texture of being addressed. P1 says an unreachable ground "constrains nothing." Lived moral experience says the constraint is the first thing given, and the search for a reachable ground behind it is the *later*, optional, philosopher's project.

And Husserl supplies the diagnosis of *why* the thesis looks compelling anyway. The "contact" picture is what the *Crisis* calls the objectivist forgetting: Galilean science mathematized nature, came to treat its own abstraction as the real, and so produced a world of value-neutral objects between which "access" must be some causal or quasi-causal commerce (SEP / Philopedia, "The Crisis of European Sciences"). On that picture, norms become an embarrassment — they are not among the contactable objects — so they must be either reduced to natural facts (the naturalist's "causal story") or exiled as projections. But this is precisely the "loss of meaning" Husserl diagnoses: the forgetting of the *lifeworld* (*Lebenswelt*), the pre-reflective horizon of lived, meaning-laden experience that "precedes and underpins all abstract theorizing." The thesis's P4 dilemma is a symptom of that forgetting. It mistakes a *derived* theoretical attitude (everything is an object to be contacted) for the *originary* one, and then is puzzled that norms won't fit. Return to the lifeworld and the puzzle dissolves: the morally salient world was never value-neutral waiting to be contacted; it showed up as meaningful and demanding from the start.

So I am not pleading for a tie or a fog. I am defending a positive, contentful claim: **moral norms are constituted in engaged being-in-the-world.** They are real (Levinas's command is not optional, Merleau-Ponty's solicitation is not invented) and they are not mind-independent in the thesis's sense (they require a being situated, embodied, with others — they are not free-floating occurrent facts). That third option is not a dodge; it is the position the dichotomy was built to render invisible. The thesis is valid given P1–P4, as the reconstruction grants — but P4 is not a conceptual truth about "backstop," it is a Cartesian picture wearing a conceptual mask, and once the mask is off the deduction has nothing to stand on.

## What the thesis presupposes

The thesis presupposes that *to be related to a normative ground at all is to be a subject standing across a gap from an object, such that the only live question is whether and how the gap is bridged ("contact").* Every premise inherits this. P1 ("a merely posited ground constrains nothing") assumes constraint must flow from a *reached* object. P2 packages the bridge into two modes, causal or practical, and treats them as the *only* warrant-conferring relations. P4 then partitions reality cleanly into "contacted, hence inside/natural" and "transcendent, hence unreachable" — a partition that is *coherent only if* the inside/outside, subject/object geometry is already in force. The word "access" itself is the tell: it names a crossing, and a crossing presupposes two pre-given sides. Strip that presupposition — attend to how a norm is *actually* lived, as the soliciting structure of an engaged situation rather than as an object on the far side of an epistemic gap — and there are no two sides to bridge, P4's dichotomy is not exhaustive, P5 does not follow, and the conclusion never closes. The naturalist and the realist, I will press in the attack round, are *quarreling over which side of this gap the norm sits on or how to cross it* — and in so quarreling they have already agreed to the one premise that is false.

## Attack round

Having now read the other four, my blind diagnosis is confirmed: three of them are not opponents but co-defendants. Each fills the same empty slot.

**Naturalist.** You have built the most rigorous possible bridge and then noticed no traffic crosses it — Field's "explain the correlation," Street's two horns, Enoch's "remaining miracle." But a *correlation* between belief-here and fact-there only needs explaining if there are two relata held apart to begin with. Your "tracking" presupposes a knower whose states might *match or miss* a separately-standing order. That is the spectator stance Heidegger showed is a *founded, deficient* mode, derivative of engaged coping. You have not proven norms lack a ground; you have proven the *grounding-from-behind* picture is empty — and then read the emptiness as a verdict on morality rather than on the picture. You fill the slot with "nature" and call the slot's failure a result.

**Rationalist.** You come closer — constitutivism's "relation a thing bears to its own form, not contact across a divide" is nearly my point. But you re-theorize the lived datum into a *structure of rational agency*, an architecture of self-legislation and inescapable jurisdiction. The pull I feel toward the slumped stranger is not first the form of my volition; it is the stranger *soliciting* me, prior to any reflexive turn on agency's constitutive standards. You relocate the ground into the rational subject — still a subject, still the inner pole of the old split — rather than dissolving the pole. You fill the slot with "agency."

**Realist.** You are the purest case. "There is a third door, and reason has always walked through it" — but a *door* is exactly the gap geometry: a knower on one side, a causally-inert necessary fact on the far side, "rational insight" the crossing. Parfit's queerness-shrug ("no ontological implications") does not escape the subject/object frame; it just declares the object weightless while keeping the architecture intact. Safety, modal robustness, companions-in-guilt — all are answers to "how does the inner reach the outer?" You fill the slot with "non-natural fact" and never ask whether the slot was real.

**The shared, unexamined premise.** All three quarrel over *what occupies the ground-slot* and *how the gap is crossed*. None asks whether there is a slot or a gap. The word "access" is the shared tell — it names a crossing, and a crossing needs two pre-given sides. Strip the geometry and the dispute that organizes their disagreement evaporates.

**Contemplative — ally, but over-theorized.** We arrive at the same place by different roads: you deny the gap ("no knower-over-here standing in a contact-relation to a ground-over-there"), I deny it. Your reading of P4 as "good news, badly worded" is exactly mine. But where I return to the *lived* phenomenon — the norm given in the seeing, before reflection — you replace one metaphysical thesis with another: a global doctrine that *everything* lacks svabhāva, the two-truths apparatus, the emptiness-of-emptiness. That is a *theory* of why there is no ground, and it generalizes far past the moral case the run restricts us to. My objection is lighter: I need no claim about tables, fire, or nirvāṇa. I need only that we attend to how a demand actually shows up. You are right that the craving for a backstop is the knot — but you untie it with a system, where description suffices.
