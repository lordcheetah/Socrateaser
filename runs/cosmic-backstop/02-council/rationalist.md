# Cosmic Backstop for Morality — Rationalist (council)
> **Run:** cosmic-backstop · **Mode:** full · **Stage:** Council
> **Thesis:** There is no accessible cosmic backstop for morality, because warrant requires causal/practical contact and a mind-independent normative ground is by its nature sealed off from such contact.
> **Seat:** Rationalist — Kantian/rationalist
> **Defending:** VERDICT: The thesis is FALSE, because its decisive premise (P2) smuggles in a closure claim — that causal or practical "contact" are the only warrant-conferring relations — and the authority of moral norms is in fact constitutive of rational agency itself, so we have access to the backstop not by reaching out to touch it but by being the kind of agents we already are.

VERDICT: The thesis is FALSE, because its decisive premise (P2) smuggles in a closure claim — that causal or practical "contact" are the only warrant-conferring relations — and the authority of moral norms is in fact constitutive of rational agency itself, so we have access to the backstop not by reaching out to touch it but by being the kind of agents we already are.

## Case

The thesis is engineered so that everything turns on P2: *access requires contact, where contact is either causal or practical/constitutive.* The reconstruction concedes this openly — "P2 carries the weight," and P2 "quietly carries a closure claim — 'contact (in these two modes) is the *only* way access can be had' — whose exhaustiveness is asserted, not derived" (`01-reconstruction.md`). I attack exactly there. But I do not attack it the way the abstract a priori realist (Parfit/Enoch) does, by positing free-standing non-natural facts that reason "intuits" across a gap. That route leaves the gap intact and then claims a mysterious faculty for leaping it — which is precisely the target P2 was built to hit. My route denies that there is a gap to leap at all.

**The constitutivist move.** On the Kantian picture, the authority of the moral law is not the authority of an *object* we must somehow get into contact with. It is the authority of a *law we ourselves give*. Kant's striking doctrine is that "a rational will must be regarded as autonomous, or free in the sense of being the author of the law that binds it," and that "the fundamental principle of morality — the CI — is none other than the law of an autonomous will" (Johnson & Cureton, *Kant's Moral Philosophy*, SEP). Autonomy here is not lawlessness; it is *self-legislation* — being "bound only by laws of one's own making." The categorical imperative "binds rational agents unconditionally, regardless of their desires or ends" (SEP, *Categorical Imperative* entry / Wikipedia summary), and in the second *Critique* Kant grounds this in the "fact of reason" (*Factum der Vernunft*): "it is simply a 'fact of reason' that our wills are bound by the CI, and he uses this to argue that our wills are autonomous" (SEP, *Kant's Moral Philosophy*).

Notice what this does to the thesis's dilemma. P3 defines a backstop as a ground "whose authority does not depend on us, our practices, or our constitution," and P4 says anything we *contact* is thereby "part of the natural/practical order" — entangled with us. The dilemma assumes the binding ground must be *either* an external object out of reach *or* an internal artifact of our contingent practices. The Kantian denies the dichotomy. The moral law is mind-*involving* without being mind-*dependent* in the deflationary sense the thesis needs. Its authority does not depend on what any of us happens to want, decide, or practice — it is not a "form of life" we could have legislated otherwise — yet it is not sealed off in a Platonic heaven either, because it is the very form of rational volition. It is necessary and unconditional (satisfying the spirit of P3's transcendence), and it is accessed by the only route that could matter for a *practical* ground: by being a rational agent and deliberating at all.

**This is not "practical contact" in P2's sense.** P2's "practical/constitutive" disjunct construes practical access as a ground "showing up in what we do and how we are situated" — i.e., the ground is one more item in the order of our situated activity. But the categorical imperative does not "show up" in our activity as a feature of it that we could observe or fail to observe; it is the *constitutive standard of the activity itself*. Korsgaard makes this precise: practical principles are "internal to the very activity they govern: they thus play a constitutive role," and the principles governing action are "constitutive standards of agency, standards that arise from the nature of agency itself" (Korsgaard, *Self-Constitution*; *The Sources of Normativity*, Cambridge UP 1996). The relation between an agent and the constitutive standards of agency is not a *contact* relation across a divide — it is the relation a thing bears to its own form. A move counts as a chess move only by answering to the constitutive rules of chess; one does not first locate chess and then make "causal or practical contact" with it. Likewise an exercise of practical reason counts as such only by standing under the law of practical reason. So P2's exhaustive disjunction — causal OR situated-practical — leaves out the warrant-conferring relation that actually obtains: the *constitutive* relation between an agent and the norms without which there is no agency to speak of.

**Why this is genuine access, not posited and inert.** P1 demands that a ground "actually constrain belief or judgment, rather than merely being posited." The constitutivist meets P1 head-on, and more cleanly than the realist does. The realist must explain why our intuitions *track* an independent realm — the Benacerraf/Field "access problem" that variant 1 of the reconstruction sharpens into its best weapon. That challenge has bite against free-standing normative facts: absent a tracking story, it looks like coincidence that our intuitions land on the truths. But the constitutivist owes *no* tracking story, because there is nothing external to track. The norm is not a target our judgment might hit or miss by luck; it is the standard internal to judging-well as such. The question "but how do you know your faculty is reliably correlated with the normative facts?" presupposes the two-place picture — faculty here, facts there — that constitutivism rejects. Ferrero's defense of the move turns on the *inescapability of agency*: agency is "an inescapable enterprise that has the largest jurisdiction and is closed under rational assessment," such that "a systematic failure to be guided by the norms of rationality and morality would amount to a loss or lack of agency, and since we cannot but be agents, we cannot but be unconditionally guided by these norms" (Ferrero, *Constitutivism and the Inescapability of Agency*, Oxford Studies in Metaethics). That inescapability is exactly the unconditional, non-optional, practice-independent authority a *backstop* was wanted for — and it is, by hypothesis, the most accessible thing there is.

**The self-undermining test.** The thesis asserts P1–P4 and means for us to *believe* the conclusion *because the argument is valid and its premises true*. That demand — believe what the reasons support — is itself a categorical norm of theoretical reason, and the proponent's act of asserting it is an exercise of rational agency that already stands under constitutive norms it did not contract into and cannot causally inspect. If the thesis were right that no unconditional norm is accessible without contact, the proponent's own claim on our assent would be inaccessible to *us* by the same standard, and the argument could exert no rational pull. The thesis can be *stated* only from inside the space of binding reasons whose accessibility it denies. That is not a rhetorical flourish; it is the transcendental point. The bindingness of "you ought to believe what your evidence supports" is a precondition of advancing P1–P4 at all, and it is binding in a way no causal or merely-situated "contact" delivers.

So the dilemma never closes. There is a third warrant-conferring relation — the constitutive one — that is neither Humean causal commerce nor the contingent showing-up of a ground "in what we do." The reconstruction admits this is fatal if such a relation exists: "If such a third relation exists and confers warrant, P2 is false" (`01-reconstruction.md`). It exists. P2 is false. C does not follow.

## What I deny and why

I deny **P2**, and with it the closure assumption the reconstruction flags as suppressed.

- P2 asserts that access is *exhausted* by causal contact or practical/constitutive contact, where both are read as relations to a ground standing over against the knower. This exhaustiveness is the disputed conclusion dressed as a premise. It "imports a broadly naturalist epistemology ('show me the causal/practical story') and then convicts the non-natural ground of failing a test it was never going to pass" (`01-reconstruction.md`). The constitutivist refuses the test, not by pleading a special faculty, but by denying that warrant for the supreme norm is a matter of a knower contacting a separate ground.

- I therefore also deny the dichotomy carried by **P4** — that whatever binds us is *either* transcendent-and-uncontacted *or* contacted-and-thereby-merely-ours. The moral law is unconditional and not up to us (so not a deflationary, optional practice), yet not external to rational volition (so not sealed off). P4's clean partition assumes that mind-involvement entails practice-dependence; the autonomy doctrine is precisely the denial of that entailment. Self-legislation yields a law that is *ours* in origin and *unconditional* in authority at once.

I do **not** deny P1: an inaccessible ground constrains nothing. I grant it gladly, because the constitutive ground is the most accessible ground imaginable — we cannot deliberate, choose, or even assert the thesis without already standing under it.

## Attack round

**Naturalist.** Your case is self-undermining. You demand that we *believe the thesis because the reasons support it* — but "believe what your evidence supports" is itself an unconditional epistemic norm whose authority over us you cannot ground in your own coin. On the tracking condition, your demand for *our* assent is warranted only if there is a tracking story explaining why we land on the truth; the norm of evidence-responsiveness, being no natural fact you can causally inspect, fails your test. So either the norm binds (and P2's closure is false — a non-contact warrant exists) or it does not (and your argument exerts no rational pull, since we have no obligation to follow where it leads). You cannot state your conclusion except from inside a space of binding reasons your conclusion declares inaccessible. The "remaining miracle" you press on the realist is your own.

**Robust realist.** We share the verdict; I deny your route, and the access problem is *yours*, not mine. You posit a free-standing, causally inert normative fact and then owe Field/Street a story for why intuition *tracks* it — Enoch's "remaining miracle," which you smuggle back via third-factor harmony. My constitutivism owes no such story: there is no separate fact "over there" for judgment to hit or miss by luck, so the two-place tracking picture never gets a grip. The norm is the constitutive standard of judging-well, not a target. You bought transcendence at the price of a gap; I get unconditional authority (self-legislation, inescapable agency) with no gap to bridge. Drop the free-standing fact and the queerness and coincidence worries simply vanish.

**Phenomenologist.** You charge me with relocating the ground "into the rational subject" — still an inner pole. But constitutive standards are not *in* a pre-given subject; the subject is constituted *by* standing under them. There is no agent prior to the norms who then internalizes a ground. So your "still a pole" objection misfires: I deny the pre-given pole exactly as you deny the gap. The deeper trouble is yours: lived "solicitation" is a datum, and the naturalist will naturalize it as trained affordance-detection. Description cannot tell the genuinely binding pull from tribal conditioning — only a *constitutive* norm of practical reason discriminates the demand that *ought* to move me from the one that merely *does*. You need my normativity to make your phenomenology more than psychology.

**Contemplative.** You read my agency as reified svabhāva via MMK 8 (agent and act mutually dependent). But constitutivism already grants agent and act are co-constituted — that is the doctrine, not a counterexample. Inescapability is not a metaphysical self-existing entity; it is a *transcendental* claim: any move that assesses, including your assertion that all is empty, already stands under norms of rational assessment. Here is the bite: your verdict asks for our rational assent, and "emptiness wrongly grasped is like a snake" is offered as *correct*. Correctness is a binding norm you cannot cash as mere convention without making your own teaching optional for whoever exits the frame. Conventional-only authority is exactly the deflationary backstop — it cannot underwrite the categorical "ought" your own corrective presupposes.
