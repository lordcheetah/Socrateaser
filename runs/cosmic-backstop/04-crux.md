# Cosmic Backstop for Morality — Crux map
> **Run:** cosmic-backstop · **Mode:** full · **Stage:** Synthesis
> **Thesis:** There is no accessible cosmic backstop for morality, because warrant requires causal/practical contact and a mind-independent normative ground is by its nature sealed off from such contact.

---

## The crux

Once "contact" is disambiguated, the whole dispute collapses onto one premise: **does warrant for a belief about a mind-independent normative domain require an *explanation of why the belief tracks the truth* (and is bare a priori rational insight, by itself, such an explanation or merely a name for an unexplained match?)** Everyone who reaches the thesis on its own (epistemic) terms agrees the original "access requires causal/practical *contact*" reading is dead; the live fork is whether the realist's tracking-explanation is owed and unmet (thesis holds in its restricted form) or not owed because there is nothing external to track (thesis fails).

## Resolving the P2 disambiguation (the distinction-watchdog's load-bearing flag)

The watchdog is right that the council fought two different P2s under one name, and this *moves* the crux rather than decorating it:

- **P2-contact** (the premise `01-reconstruction.md` certified valid): access requires the ground figure in the *causal/etiological or practical* commerce that produces our judgments. This is the reading the robust realist correctly convicts of question-begging — it imports a broadly naturalist epistemology and then notes the non-natural fails it. The counterexample report independently kills this reading: as a *general* access principle it convicts mathematics and logic of unwarrant (Cases 1–2), which is incredible and, for logic, self-undercutting (the thesis's own deduction runs on non-contact warrant for modus tollens).
- **P2-tracking** (the premise the naturalist actually defends, and the reconstruction's "charitable variant 1"): a relation confers warrant only if standing in it *explains why our judgments track the facts rather than miss them*. This drops the causal theory (Field's reformulation of Benacerraf — verified accurate by the attribution-checker on both the naturalist and realist drafts) and is *not* refuted by the math/logic companions, because those domains arguably have non-causal tracking stories (structuralism, indispensability) that ethics may lack.

**Does the real crux survive disambiguation? Yes — but only in the P2-tracking form, and the naturalist and realist are *not* merely fighting two versions.** They converge on rejecting P2-contact and both accept the tracking/correlation burden. Their genuine disagreement is sharp and survives: the naturalist says a priori insight "is a *name* for the reliability we have not yet explained, not an explanation of it" and presses Street's Darwinian dilemma plus Enoch's conceded "remaining miracle"; the realist says the tracking burden is *meetable without contact* (Enoch's third factor, Clarke-Doane's safety/necessity reply) and that for *necessary* truths the coincidence worry has no purchase. That is one real crux on one shared premise, not a pseudo-dispute. The thesis's defensible residue is therefore narrower than the bald conclusion: **"morality, unlike math/logic, has no non-contact tracking story"** — a live, unsettled disanalogy, not a closed result.

## Position map

- **Naturalist (Benacerraf–Field–Street):** Thesis TRUE, on the P2-tracking reading. One commitment that forces it: **an unexplained match between belief and a mind-independent domain is a coincidence, and knowledge cannot be coincidence** (HP5). To change his mind he would have to grant that a priori rational insight into *necessary* truths is itself a tracking-explanation (or that necessary truths need none) — i.e., concede the realist's safety reply discharges the burden. He cannot retreat to P2-contact without the math/logic companions sinking him.
- **Robust realist (Parfit/Enoch specialist):** Thesis FALSE. One commitment: **there is a third warrant-conferring relation — a priori insight into causally inert, necessary truths — and the reliability burden is dischargeable without contact** (denies HP1, HP5's bite for necessary truths). To change his mind he would have to admit that necessity of the *truths* does not explain reliability of the *faculty* (the naturalist's broken-clock point) and that his third-factor reply bootstraps (Dyke) — i.e., that Enoch's own "remaining miracle" *is* the failure of warrant rather than a tolerable residue.
- **Rationalist (Korsgaard/Ferrero constitutivist):** Thesis FALSE. One commitment: **the supreme norm is the constitutive standard of agency, so there is no separate fact to track and no gap to bridge** (denies HP1 *and* the two-place picture in HP3/HP4). To change his mind he would have to grant Enoch's shmagency point — that agency is optional, so its constitutive norms bind only opt-ins and never reach P3-transcendence — or admit (per the distinction-watchdog, #5) that he wins only by *collapsing* the authority-question into the access-question, a register-crossing he must defend.
- **Phenomenologist (Heidegger/Levinas/Merleau-Ponty/Husserl):** Thesis ILL-POSED. One commitment: **the subject/object gap that "access" presupposes (HP3) is a derivative, breakdown mode, not the form of all relation to a norm; the norm is given *in* engaged being-in-the-world.** To change his mind he would have to concede the naturalist's challenge — that "lived solicitation" is a naturalizable trained disposition that cannot, by description alone, distinguish the demand that *ought* to move me from the one that merely *does*. He does not meet the thesis on its epistemic terms; he rejects the terms.
- **Contemplative (Madhyamaka):** Thesis's CONCLUSION correct (affirms P4 outright), its FRAMING confused. One commitment: **nothing has svabhāva, so a "ground behind the practice" was never available to lose; emptiness is what makes the conventional work.** To change his mind he would have to grant that the conventional/practice-internal footing he offers just *is* the deflationary backstop the thesis already sets aside — i.e., that he has restated the naturalist's crane in the normative-therapeutic register, not refuted the worry.

## Convergence

More is shared than the verdicts suggest, and naming it bounds the real dispute:

1. **Everyone rejects P2-contact.** No serious voice defends "warrant requires causal/practical commerce" as a general principle. The naturalist disowns the "Humean billiard-ball" caricature; the realist, rationalist, phenomenologist, and contemplative all reject it from their own directions. The premise the reconstruction certified valid is the premise nobody will hold.
2. **Everyone agrees the deflationary backstop exists and is accessible** (reasons, coherence, a form of life, conventional truth). The distinction-watchdog confirms no one smuggles this back in as an answer to the robust thesis. The fight is entirely about the *robust* backstop.
3. **The naturalist and both realists agree the tracking/correlation-explanation burden is the real test** (HP5). They split only on whether it is met. This is the genuine crux, and it is narrow.
4. **The phenomenologist and contemplative converge against the gap** (HP3) — both reject "access" as presupposing two pre-given sides. They are co-defendants, not opponents, differing only in method (lived description vs. a global metaphysics of emptiness).

So the council splits cleanly into two camps that *talk past each other by design*: those who fight *inside* the access/tracking frame (naturalist vs. realists — a real, settleable-in-principle dispute over the disanalogy) and those who *reject the frame* (phenomenologist, contemplative — a dispute about whether the question is well-posed at all).

## What the functional roles changed

- **Hidden premises** relocated the surface crux. The stated crux is P2, but its force rides on **HP1** (contact-relations are the *only* warrant-conferring relations — asserted, not derived) and, in the charitable form, **HP5** (the tracking/non-coincidence demand). Surfacing HP1 is what exposes the question-begging charge; HP5 is what survives it. **HP4** (univocity of "warrant," hiding in the shared word) is the quiet enabler of the P1→P2 chain and is exactly what the realist denies when he calls normative warrant *sui generis*.
- **Counterexamples** forced the retreat. Math and logic (Cases 1–2) make P2-contact untenable as a general principle and push the thesis to its narrower, defensible residue. The rational-insight datum (Case 3) and the constitutivist case (Case 4) show the residue is *contested, not settled*, and that **P4** (not only P2) carries weight once practical-contact access is taken seriously. Net effect: the thesis is not shown false in its strongest form, but its universal contact-reading does not survive, and the crux relocates onto the disanalogy.
- **Distinctions** is the pivot of this whole map: it established that **P2-contact ≠ P2-tracking**, that validity was certified on the former while defensibility lives on the latter, and that the rationalist (#5, authority→access) and contemplative (#6, metaphysical-absence→normative-sufficiency) each win their verdict only by *crossing register*. Their "P2 is false" / "framing is confused" verdicts therefore should not be tallied as refutations *on the thesis's own epistemic terms* — a crucial weighting instruction I have followed.
- **Self-application:** SURVIVES, and this *sharpens* the crux. The thesis is meta-level (P2 quantifies over normative grounds, not over itself), so no self-refutation. But the price is exactly the one the brief flags: to escape self-defeat the thesis must *concede that non-contact rational reflection confers warrant on some claims* (its own modal/closure premise P2). It therefore cannot assert "no non-contact warrant ever" — the blanket-naturalist form would saw off its own branch. **Self-application forces the thesis onto the restricted Benacerraf/tracking (variant-1) form**, and the asymmetry it must then defend ("a priori reflection reaches modal facts about warrant but not first-order normative facts") *is* the realist's whole opening. This independently corroborates the distinction report's verdict: only P2-tracking is defensible.
- **Attribution:** Three flags, one bearing on the map. (i) The naturalist↔realist clash over Enoch's "remaining miracle" is *accurately quoted by both* — it is a legitimate interpretive disagreement (concession of failure vs. tolerable residue), not a misattribution, and it sits exactly on the crux. (ii) The Parfit view-name and the Field p.26 string are minor/terminological. (iii) **The Nāgārjuna 24.14 misattribution** — the contemplative presents a "possible/possible" rendering as verbatim Garfield when Garfield reads "clear/clear" (the wording is from the Kalupahana/Siderits-Katsura lineage). See the next paragraph for its effect on weight.

### Effect of the Nāgārjuna misattribution on the contemplative's position

Minimal, and it should be stated plainly so the map is not distorted in either direction. The error is a *misattributed translation*, not a fabricated verse or a reversed conclusion: the verse number is correct and the philosophical point (emptiness as the enabling condition of the conventional) is faithfully Nāgārjuna's and is independently carried by 24.18 and 24.20, which the checker verified verbatim/accurate. So the contemplative's *argument* does not lose a load-bearing citation. What it costs is a sliver of the draft's authority-by-precision — it claimed to have "verified against Garfield" and had not — which matters only insofar as the contemplative's force already depends on a framework premise (two-truths) that an anxious interlocutor rejects. The distinction report had *already* discounted the contemplative's verdict as a register-crossing (metaphysical-absence → normative-sufficiency) rather than a refutation on the thesis's epistemic terms. The misattribution does not lower the contemplative's weight further on the *crux* (it was never weighing on the epistemic crux); it is a credibility ding on a position already mapped as "answers a different question well."

## What it would take to settle it

This bottoms out in **a basic commitment, not a fact or a proof** — but it is worth saying precisely *which* commitment, because the dispute is unusually well-localized.

- **The frame-internal fork (naturalist vs. realist)** is settleable *in principle* by argument, and it has a determinate shape: it turns on whether **modal robustness (necessity of the truths) explains reliability of the faculty.** This is not a matter of empirical fact — both sides have the same biology and the same mathematics. It is the open question of whether "the truths could not have been otherwise, so we could not easily have believed falsely" *answers* the reliability challenge or merely *sidesteps* it (the naturalist's broken-clock objection: a necessitated reading is still not a tracking one). No experiment decides this; it is adjudicable only by whether one accepts that safety-without-explanation is enough for warrant. Reasonable people who grant every shared premise still part here. That makes it a **standing disagreement that bottoms out in a conception of warrant** — but a *narrow, named* one, which is the genuine deliverable.
- **The frame-rejecting fork (phenomenologist/contemplative)** is not settleable on the thesis's terms at all, because they deny those terms (HP3, the gap). It is settleable only by a prior **conceptual decision** about whether "access"/"tracking" is the right grammar for our relation to norms — and nothing inside the access dispute can compel that decision either way.

So: **not empirically settleable; not provable; it bottoms out in whether you accept that an unexplained-but-modally-safe correlation can confer warrant (the frame-internal crux), or whether you accept the access/gap grammar at all (the frame-level crux).** The honest result is the crux itself, sharply located, plus the verdict that nothing in the dispute compels the commitment on which it turns.

## For the author

The author is a pragmatist with a stake, and the map places that view with unusual clarity — so I will not flatter it.

**Where the view sits:** The pragmatist's natural home is the naturalist camp *and* the reconstruction's **charitable variant 3** (the "inertness" line): even granting the realist's metaphysics for free, an inaccessible ground does no *backstop-work* — it settles no first-order dispute and adjudicates between no practices, because to consult it you would need exactly the tracking relation it lacks. This is the dialectically safest form: it concedes the a priori-access fight and still wins the operative point. It also sits comfortably beside the contemplative's "the footing was always conventional, and that is sturdy enough" — though the author should notice that taking that comfort is *agreeing with the deflationary backstop everyone already grants*, not defeating the robust one.

**The single premise the author must defend:** Stripped of the contact-reading (dead) and pushed by self-application off the blanket-naturalist form (self-defeating), the author's whole position now rests on **HP5 in its strongest form: that warrant for a belief about a mind-independent normative domain requires an *explanation* of the belief–fact correlation, and that the realist's modal/safety reply is not such an explanation but a relabeling of the unexplained match.** That is the one premise. If the realist is right that *necessity removes the coincidence* — that there is no nearby world where the truth differs, so no luck to explain away — then HP5 has no purchase in the normative case and the thesis fails even in its restricted form. The author cannot win this by demanding "a causal story" (that is the dead P2-contact, and it begs the question); they must hold the line that *safety without explanation is not warrant*. Everything the author wants stands or falls on that, and nothing in the dispute can compel the realist to grant it.

One caution the author should accept: variant 3's "inertness" payoff is real but *dialectically* won, not *epistemically* won — the realist who holds that a priori-accessed truths genuinely do settle first-order disputes will simply deny the inertness. So the inertness line relocates the fight back onto HP5; it does not escape it. The author has one premise to defend, clearly named, and it is a conception of warrant, not a fact about the world.

### Bottom line

**The crux:** With "contact" disambiguated, the dispute reduces to one premise — whether warrant for beliefs about a mind-independent normative domain requires an *explanation* of why belief tracks truth, with the live question being whether bare a priori insight (defended by modal/safety considerations) counts as such an explanation or merely renames an unexplained match.

**Settleability:** Not empirical, not provable. It is a standing disagreement that bottoms out in a conception of warrant — narrowly and namably so (does modally-safe-but-unexplained correlation confer warrant?) for the naturalist–realist fork, and in a prior conceptual decision about the access/gap grammar itself for the phenomenologist–contemplative fork. The deliverable is the located crux, not a verdict.
