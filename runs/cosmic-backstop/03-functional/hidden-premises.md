# 03 — Hidden Premises: the cosmic backstop

> Run slug: `cosmic-backstop`
> Excavated from `01-reconstruction.md` (context: `00-thesis.md`).

HIDDEN-PREMISES: 6 found

## Suppressed premises

### HP1 — The exhaustiveness (closure) premise on access-relations

- **Stated form:** Causal contact and practical/constitutive contact are the *only* warrant-conferring access-relations; there is no third relation (notably a priori rational insight) that confers warrant without being either of these.
- **Where it's needed:** P2. As written, P2 asserts "access only if contact (causal OR practical)." The biconditional-strength "only if" already encodes this closure, but the closure is *asserted*, not derived — P2 names two disjuncts and silently treats the disjunction as exhaustive of all warrant-conferring relations. Without HP1, P2 is merely "these two relations suffice for access," which does not yield "no non-contact relation gives access," and the modus tollens to C1 (P1+P2+P5) fails: a backstop could be reached by a third route.
- **How contestable:** **hidden crux candidate.** This is the load-bearing suppressed premise. The reconstruction itself flags P2's exhaustiveness as "asserted, not derived." HP1 is exactly the claim the robust realist denies, and it is more contestable than P2's surface content (which lists relations everyone agrees are *sufficient*).
- **Who would deny it:** Robust non-naturalist moral realists (Parfit/Enoch) and Kantian/rationalist a priori traditions — they assert a priori rational insight as precisely the third, non-contact, warrant-conferring relation.

### HP2 — The naturalist gloss on "contact" (no contact-relation reaches the transcendent)

- **Stated form:** Any relation rich enough to confer warrant on our judgments is *ipso facto* a relation that makes its relatum part of the natural/practical order; no warrant-conferring relation can hold to something that remains fully mind-independent and transcendent.
- **Where it's needed:** P4 → P5, and the joint of P4 with P2. P4 says "whatever is in contact is part of the natural/practical order." For the dilemma in P5 to be airtight, contact must *exhaustively* drag its object into the order — there can be no warrant-relation to something that stays transcendent. If a relation could both confer warrant *and* leave its object transcendent, P4 would not exclude that object from being a backstop, and P5 collapses.
- **How contestable:** **live/disputed.** This is the conceptual hinge the charitable variant 2 tries to convert into a definitional truth about "backstop." Whether it is conceptual or smuggled naturalism is itself the fight.
- **Who would deny it:** Theists / classical natural-law theorists (access via participation, conscience, revelation — a relation they take to reach a transcendent ground without naturalizing it); also realists who hold the abstract/necessary can be grasped without being naturalized.

### HP3 — The no-gap / subject-object presupposition behind "access"

- **Stated form:** "Access" and "contact" name a relation across a real gap between a knower (us, our practices) and a ground distinct from us; knowing or being warranted is a relation between two relata standing apart.
- **Where it's needed:** P1, P2, and P4 jointly. The whole apparatus of "access requires standing in a relation *to* a ground," and P4's clean partition of "contacted (hence inside the order)" vs. "transcendent (hence outside)," presupposes there is a gap to be bridged at all. If knower and ground are not two things across a gap, the partition in P4 and the relational reading of P1/P2 do not get a grip, and P5's dichotomy is a false one.
- **How contestable:** **live/disputed** (the deeper, secondary route the reconstruction names as the phenomenologist/contemplative target).
- **Who would deny it:** Phenomenologists and contemplatives — they reject the subject/object split as a smuggled confusion, so the gap "access" presupposes does not exist.

### HP4 — Univocity of "warrant" / the justificatory monism premise

- **Stated form:** There is a single, univocal notion of warrant in play, such that the warrant a ground "confers on our moral judgments" (P1) is the same kind of thing as the warrant supplied by causal or practical contact (P2); justification is not relation-relative in a way that would let necessary normative truths be warranted on different terms than empirical ones.
- **Where it's needed:** The hypothetical syllogism from P1 through P2 to C1. P1 says warrant requires access; P2 says access requires contact. Chaining them ("warrant ⊃ access ⊃ contact") requires that the "access/warrant" of P1 is the very same relation governed by P2. If "warrant" equivocates — if normative/a priori warrant is a different species not subject to the contact condition — the chain breaks at the join.
- **How contestable:** **live/disputed.** Often invisible because both premises use the word "warrant," masking that the realist holds normative warrant to be sui generis.
- **Who would deny it:** Rationalists and non-naturalist realists who model normative/mathematical knowledge as a distinct, non-causal species of warrant (the "known the way mathematical truths are known" line).

### HP5 — The tracking/non-coincidence premise (latent in the strongest variant)

- **Stated form:** A ground confers warrant only if our standing in the relation *explains why our judgments track it rather than miss it* — mere correlation or a coincidence between our intuitions and the truths does not amount to warrant.
- **Where it's needed:** It is the engine of the charitable variant 1 (the Benacerraf/Field reliability reframing of P1/P2). Under that variant, the work P2 does is carried by this tracking demand: a priori insight is excluded not because it isn't "contact" but because, absent a tracking explanation, our landing on the truths is coincidental. Without HP5 the reliability variant has no force and reduces to bare P2.
- **How contestable:** **live/disputed**, and the reconstruction names it the author's best line. Contestable because the realist may deny that warrant requires a *tracking/explanatory* relation at all (e.g., for necessary truths there is nothing to "track" that could have been otherwise).
- **Who would deny it:** Non-naturalist realists who hold that for necessary normative truths the modal-tracking demand is misconceived (no possible world where the truths differ, so no coincidence to explain away).

### HP6 — The pinned (robust, non-deflationary) sense of "backstop" holds throughout

- **Stated form:** Throughout the argument "cosmic backstop" / "normative ground" refers to the robust, mind-independent, transcendent guarantor (P3's sense), and never silently shifts to the deflationary sense (reasons, practices, a form of life that stabilizes judgment from within).
- **Where it's needed:** Stability of the term across P1–P5 and C. P3 fixes the robust sense, but P1 ("a normative ground confers warrant...") and C ("no accessible cosmic backstop for morality") must not let the deflationary sense slip in. If the term drifts, the conclusion either becomes trivially false (deflationary grounds are accessible by definition) or equivocates between the two senses.
- **How contestable:** **granted, but load-bearing.** The thesis doc explicitly pins this and treats the equivocation as a pseudo-dispute to clear. Marked here because the validity and non-triviality of the whole argument silently depend on it; it is the premise whose violation produces the talk-past-each-other failure the run is built to avoid.
- **Who would deny it:** No serious party *denies* the distinction; the risk is unnoticed slippage. Deflationists/pragmatists who reject the robust sense as intelligible at all would say there is no stable referent for the term to keep — which is a different challenge, but one that bears on whether P3 picks anything out.

## Note for the synthesizer

The prime crux candidate is **HP1** (exhaustiveness/closure on access-relations), which sits underneath the stated crux P2: P2 *reads* as the access condition, but its force depends entirely on the unstated closure claim that contact-relations are the *only* warrant-conferring ones. Stating HP1 explicitly is what exposes the question-begging charge from the framing — it is the disputed conclusion (no non-contact access) appearing as a premise. **HP2** and **HP3** are the secondary (definitional and anti-dichotomy) pressure points already mapped to the realist-vs-phenomenologist split. **HP4** (univocity of warrant) is worth surfacing because it is the quietest of the set: it hides in the shared word "warrant" and is what permits the P1→P2 chain at all.
