# 03 — Counterexamples: the cosmic backstop

> Run slug: `cosmic-backstop`
> Manufactured against `00-thesis.md` and `01-reconstruction.md`.

COUNTEREXAMPLES: 3 strong | 2 narrowing | 1 weak

## Orientation

The thesis is a universal-negative whose load is carried by **P2**: *access (warrant-conferring) requires contact, where contact is causal or practical/constitutive.* The thesis stands or falls on whether there is any **third warrant-conferring relation** — paradigmatically a priori rational insight — that is neither causal nor practical. So the counterexamples must be cases where, by our best lights, we **do** have warrant for truths about a *mind-independent, transcendent* domain (P3-satisfying: authority not depending on us, our practices, or our constitution) **without** standing in contact with it. Each such case is a witness against P2's closure claim. The richest seam is the **companions-in-guilt** literature (math, logic), because those are domains where (a) realism is widely held, (b) the entities are causally inert and mind-independent, and (c) almost nobody is prepared to say we lack warrant — yet contact is exactly as absent as in the moral case.

Cases below are ranked strongest first.

## Cases

### Case 1 — Mathematics (the Benacerraf–Field companion)

- **Case:** We have warrant for "7 + 5 = 12" and for far less trivial theorems. Mathematical objects, on the standard platonist reading, are abstract, causally inert, and mind-independent: their truth does not depend on us, our practices, or our constitution. They satisfy P3's profile exactly — a mind-independent, transcendent ground, sealed off from causal commerce. Yet the claim that we have *no warrant* for arithmetic is a non-starter; it is closer to a reductio of whatever premise generated it. (Field 1989; Clarke-Doane, *What Is the Benacerraf Problem?*)
- **What the thesis predicts:** By P2, warrant requires contact; abstracta afford no causal contact and, on the platonist picture, no practical/constitutive contact either (we do not *constitute* the numbers, nor do they "show up in what we do" in the constitutive sense P2 needs). So the thesis must say either (i) we have no warrant for mathematics, or (ii) mathematical truth is not mind-independent after all.
- **The tension:** (i) is a cost almost no one will pay — denying warrant for arithmetic is more incredible than any premise of the argument. (ii) forces the proponent into anti-realism or fictionalism about mathematics on pain of inconsistency, which is a substantive, contested metaphysical commitment smuggled in to save an epistemic thesis. Either branch is expensive.
- **Force:** **decisive** against P2 *as a fully general access principle*, and at minimum **strongly narrowing** for the moral case: it shows the access condition cannot be a blanket truth about all mind-independent abstract domains, so the thesis owes a principled reason why morality is unlike mathematics.
- **Likely repair:** The defender splits the companion (the literature's standard move): mathematics has a *structuralist / logical* reconstruction — knowledge of mathematical structure reduces to logical knowledge, or is underwritten by indispensability to empirical science — that gives a tracking story morality lacks (Berry, *(Probably) Not Companions in Guilt*). **Cost:** this concedes that contact is *not* required for warrant in at least one transcendent domain, which is the death of P2 as stated; the thesis survives only as the narrower claim "morality specifically lacks a non-contact tracking story." That is a real retreat, and it relocates the whole fight onto whether such a story exists for ethics (Enoch's deliberative-indispensability argument is precisely a bid to supply the moral analogue of the math case).

### Case 2 — Logic itself (the self-undercutting companion)

- **Case:** The thesis's own argument is a deductive chain (P1–P5 → C), valid by hypothetical syllogism and modus tollens. To run it, the proponent relies on warrant for the laws of logic — non-contradiction, the validity of modus tollens. Logical facts are the paradigm of mind-independent, transcendent, causally inert truths: their authority does not depend on us or our practices (that is what makes them *laws* and not conventions). We are not in causal contact with the law of non-contradiction, nor do we constitute it.
- **What the thesis predicts:** By P2, the proponent has no warrant for the very logical principles the argument deploys, since there is no contact with them.
- **The tension:** The argument then saws off the branch it sits on. If P2 is true, the proponent cannot be warranted in the inference from P1–P5 to C — so the conclusion is unwarranted by its own lights. This is the classic self-defeat structure (cf. the law-of-non-contradiction "any proof must use it" point; Cowie's overgeneralization framing of companions-in-guilt).
- **Force:** **decisive** if logical realism is granted — a universal access principle that forbids non-contact warrant for transcendent truths cannot be *used* without exception for the logic it runs on. At worst for the attacker it is **strongly narrowing**: P2 must be restricted so it does not range over logic.
- **Likely repair:** (a) Go anti-realist/conventionalist about logic — but then the argument's *validity* is merely a feature of our practice, and the thesis loses its claim to track anything the realist must accept; the conclusion becomes "within-our-practice" in just the deflationary way the thesis was built to exclude. (b) Claim logic is a special case where a priori insight *does* confer warrant. **Cost:** (b) is an outright admission that a priori insight is a genuine third warrant-conferring relation — exactly what P2 denies — and now the realist asks why ethics cannot ride along. The defender's only principled exit is to argue logic's a priori warrant is *disanalogous* to ethics' (e.g., constitutive of thought itself), which again narrows the thesis and shifts it to a disanalogy burden.

### Case 3 — The rational-insight datum (perceptual-analogue intuitions)

- **Case:** Take a self-evident normative proposition of the kind realists lead with: "gratuitous cruelty for amusement is wrong," or "if A is better than B and B better than C, A is better than C." Competent reflectors converge on these the way they converge on simple logical or arithmetical truths, with the same phenomenology of *seeing that it must be so*. The realist (Parfit, Enoch) holds these are grasped by a priori rational insight — a non-causal, non-practical relation that nonetheless discriminates: had the normative truth been otherwise, the rational appearance would differ.
- **What the thesis predicts:** P2 says this insight confers no warrant, because it is neither causal nor practical/constitutive contact. So our conviction that gratuitous cruelty is wrong is, *qua* purported tracking of a mind-independent truth, unwarranted — on a par with a baseless hunch.
- **The tension:** That verdict is hard to accept on its face, and the proponent has given no independent reason to deny that rational insight is warrant-conferring *other than* the contact requirement itself — which is the disputed point. As the reconstruction concedes, P2 here looks like the conclusion smuggled in as a premise (the question-begging charge): it imports "show me the causal/practical story" and then convicts the non-natural ground of failing a test it was never going to pass.
- **Force:** **narrowing**, not decisive. It does not *refute* P2; it exposes that P2 is contested rather than analytic, and shifts the burden onto the proponent to motivate the contact requirement without assuming it. Its force depends on the realist's phenomenological datum being granted, which the attacker can resist — so it is weaker than Cases 1–2, which exploit domains the proponent is already committed to.
- **Likely repair:** Deploy the **charitable variant 1** from the reconstruction (the Benacerraf/Field "discriminating warrant" version): do not assume causation, simply *demand a tracking story*. "We just intuit them" supplies no explanation of why intuition lands on the truths rather than missing, so absent a tracking relation the convergence is a coincidence that undercuts warrant. **Cost:** modest, and this is the defender's best line — but it converts the thesis from "access requires contact" into "warrant requires a tracking-explanation," which is a *different and weaker* thesis: the realist can now try to supply a non-causal tracking story (third-factor / pre-established-harmony accounts) without ever conceding contact. The crux survives but in relocated, more defensible form.

### Case 4 — Constitutive/practical access to agency's norms (the Kantian narrowing)

- **Case:** The Korsgaard-style constitutivist holds that the authority of the basic norms of practical reason is *given in the structure of agency itself*: to act at all is to be committed to the constitutive principles of action. This is the very "practical/constitutive contact" P2 lists as a legitimate access route — yet the constitutivist claims the norms so accessed are categorically binding, not merely "ours."
- **What the thesis predicts:** This is the awkward case for P2's *own disjunction*. If practical/constitutive contact is a genuine access route (P2 grants it), and if the constitutivist is right that such contact reaches *categorical, non-optional* norms, then we have warranted access to a ground whose authority does not depend on our contingent practices — threatening to satisfy P3 *via* a route P2 itself licenses.
- **The tension:** The thesis must hold that practical/constitutive contact only ever reaches grounds that are *dependent on us* (P4), so anything reached this way fails P3's transcendence. But the constitutivist's whole point is that the structure of agency is not optional — it is inescapable — so norms grounded in it are not "up to us" in the relevant sense.
- **Force:** **narrowing.** It pressures P4 (the "contacted ⇒ part of the practical order ⇒ not transcendent" inference) rather than P2's content. If practical contact can reach inescapable norms, P4's clean partition fails for at least one route.
- **Likely repair:** Invoke the **shmagency objection** (Enoch): nothing forces one to *be* an agent, so the norms constitutive of agency bind only those who opt in — hence they remain practice-dependent and fail P3's transcendence. **Cost:** low; this repair is well-developed and lets the thesis keep P4. But it commits the proponent to the contestable claim that agency is escapable, and it concedes that *if* constitutivism's inescapability claim held, P4 would fail — so the thesis is now hostage to a live first-order debate it does not settle.

### Case 5 — Theistic participation / conscience (weak)

- **Case:** Classical natural-law / theistic traditions claim an accessible transcendent ground reached through participation, conscience, or revelation — not Humean causation. Here is an alleged transcendent ground (P3-satisfying) with an alleged access route.
- **What the thesis predicts:** Either the access route *is* a form of contact (then by P4 the ground is entangled with us and not transcendent), or it is not contact (then by P2 it confers no warrant). Either way no *accessible backstop*.
- **The tension:** The proponent must say conscience/revelation either fails to warrant or fails to reach something transcendent.
- **Force:** **weak.** The case relies on contested theological assumptions (that such participation occurs, that it warrants) that the proponent can decline without embarrassment; it does not exploit a commitment the proponent already shares, unlike the math and logic companions. It also arguably gets *absorbed* cleanly by the thesis's central dilemma rather than straining it.
- **Likely repair:** Run the dilemma directly (above). **Cost:** essentially none — this is the case the thesis was *designed* to handle, so it tests scope hardly at all. Logged for completeness, not as a threat.

## Where this leaves the thesis

The decisive pressure is **companions in guilt** (Cases 1–2): if P2 is a general truth about access to mind-independent transcendent domains, it convicts mathematics and logic of unwarrant, which is incredible and (for logic) self-undermining. The proponent's only viable response is to **abandon the fully general P2** and defend the narrower disanalogy claim — *morality, unlike math and logic, lacks a non-contact tracking story*. That move is available (it is the literature's mainstream realist-vs-debunker battleground), but it is a genuine retreat: it converts the thesis from "access requires contact, full stop" into "warrant requires a discriminating tracking-explanation, which ethics has not supplied" — i.e., the reconstruction's **charitable variant 1**. Cases 3–4 then show that even this narrower form is contested rather than settled, and that P4 (not just P2) carries hidden weight once practical-contact access is taken seriously. None of the cases shows the thesis *false in its strongest charitable form*; what they show is that its **universal, contact-based reading (P2 as stated) does not survive**, and the defensible residue is narrower and relocates the crux onto a live, unsettled disanalogy.
