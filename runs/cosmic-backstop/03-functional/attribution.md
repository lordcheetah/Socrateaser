ATTRIBUTION: CONCERNS — 3 issues

Scope: verified every attribution to a named thinker/school across the five 02-council
drafts (specialist-robust-realist, naturalist, rationalist, phenomenologist,
contemplative). Priority targets named in the brief were checked against primary text
or reputable secondary sources. One real misattribution (a verse wording falsely

## Claims checked

### Sharon Street — Darwinian Dilemma (naturalist)
- **Voice → who:** Naturalist → Street, "A Darwinian Dilemma for Realist Theories of
  Value" (2006).
- **The claim:** Two-horned fork: (horn 1) no relation between Darwinian forces and
  independent evaluative truths → skepticism / "fitness-shaped fun-house mirror";
  (horn 2) a relation is asserted → realist owes a scientific account, and the
  "tracking account" is undercut by the more parsimonious adaptive-link explanation.
- **What the source says:** Street poses exactly this dilemma — deny the relation and
  realism leads to skepticism about evaluative judgments; assert it and the realist
  owes an account, where the "tracking account" is the natural candidate and Street
  argues it fails as scientifically inferior to the adaptive-link explanation.
  (philpapers.org/rec/STRADD; Springer 10.1007/s11098-005-1726-6; SJSU full-text PDF.)
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE.
- **Note:** Faithful, including the adaptive-link parsimony point and the horn labels.

### David Enoch — third factor, pre-established harmony, "remaining miracle"
- **Voice → who:** Naturalist and Robust Realist → Enoch, *Taking Morality Seriously*
  (2011), Ch. 7.
- **The claim:** (a) "godless pre-established harmony" via a third factor (evolution);
  (b) the third factor works only if survival/reproductive success is in fact good;
  (c) Enoch concedes a "remaining miracle" / "a miracle remains" that nonetheless
  "doesn't place a particularly heavy burden on robust realism"; (d) [realist draft]
  Enoch grants the best challenge is "to explain correlations" and that the first
  (causal) form of explanation is unavailable because robust normative properties are
  "almost certainly causally inefficacious," so he adds a "third form of explanation."
- **What the source says:** Confirmed verbatim on (b) and (c): "For this to work, we
  have to assume that survival and reproductive success are morally good... he still
  believes that 'a miracle remains' that they do in fact match up, but he claims 'this
  doesn't place a particularly heavy burden on robust realism.'"
  (convincingproof.org review of *TMS*; corroborated by NDPR review and Faraci's
  published review davidfaraci.com/pubs/enoch.pdf.) The "godless pre-established
  harmony" phrase and the survival-as-third-factor structure are standard and
  confirmed.
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE (both drafts).
- **Note:** The naturalist treats the "remaining miracle" as Enoch conceding P2's
  antecedent; the realist treats the third-factor reply as a counterexample to P2.
  These are competing *interpretations* of the same accurately-quoted concession — a
  legitimate disagreement, not a misattribution. The realist's specific verbatim "p.
  168" and "almost certainly causally inefficacious" tags are drawn from Faraci's
  review; the review's substance matches, but the Faraci PDF would not machine-parse,
  so the exact page-number tag is reported by the draft, not independently re-confirmed
  here (substance ACCURATE; exact page UNVERIFIED — not a flag, the content is right).

### Benacerraf / Field — does the strongest challenge drop the causal requirement?
- **Voice → who:** Naturalist and Robust Realist → Benacerraf 1973, Field 1989, via
  Clarke-Doane.
- **The claim:** Benacerraf's 1973 statement presupposed a causal theory of knowledge
  ("make necessary (causal) contact with the grounds of the truth," ~pp. 671-672);
  Field's reformulation *drops* the causal theory and recasts the challenge as one of
  *explaining the reliability* of our beliefs, not supplying causal contact. The
  realist additionally quotes Field 1989 p. 26: the challenge is to "explain how our
  beliefs about these remote entities can so well reflect the facts about them," with
  knowledge-conditions "set aside."
- **What the source says:** Confirmed. "Benacerraf's (1973) statement of the challenge
  presupposes a causal theory of knowledge... Field's contribution is to show that no
  particular theory of knowledge is essential to the challenge" — the challenge is "to
  explain how our beliefs about 'remote' abstract objects so accurately reflect the
  facts about them." (IEP, "The Benacerraf Problem"; Clarke-Doane, "What Is the
  Benacerraf Problem?" columbia.edu PDF; PMC8602206.)
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. Both drafts correctly state that the strongest form of the
  access challenge drops the causal-contact requirement and becomes a
  reliability/correlation-explanation demand. This is the pivot both drafts build on,
  and it is correctly attributed to Field-via-Benacerraf.
- **Note:** The exact Field-1989-p.26 verbatim quotation in the realist draft could not
  be re-extracted (the Clarke-Doane PDF and the Field volume did not machine-parse).
  The substance is confirmed by independent sources; the precise page-26 wording is
  therefore substance-ACCURATE / verbatim-UNVERIFIABLE. Not treated as a fabrication —
  it is a well-attested Field quotation — but flagged so a human can spot-check the
  exact string if it is load-bearing in later stages. (See Concern 3.)

### Parfit — non-natural normative truth, no ontological implications
- **Voice → who:** Robust Realist → Parfit, *On What Matters*, Vol. 2, Pt. 6.
- **The claim:** Irreducibly normative truths are "in a strong sense true" yet carry
  "no ontological implications"; normative properties are not in space/time and have no
  causal powers; grasped by rational intuition; answers Mackie's queerness.
- **What the source says:** Confirmed near-verbatim: Parfit holds "there are some claims
  that are irreducibly normative in the reason-involving sense, and are in a strong
  sense true. These truths have no ontological implications." Normative
  properties/facts have no ontological status; truth of normative propositions has no
  metaphysical/ontological implications. (Springer, "Non-Realist Cognitivism, Truth and
  Objectivity," 10.1007/s12136-016-0300-5; NDPR reviews of *OWM*.)
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE in substance.
- **Note (Concern 2):** Both drafts attach a *name* to the view. The realist calls it
  "Non-Metaphysical Non-Naturalist Normative Cognitivism"; the naturalist calls it
  "Non-Metaphysical Non-Naturalism / Non-Realist Cognitivism." Parfit's own / the
  standard label is **"Non-Realist Cognitivism."** "Non-metaphysical" and
  "non-naturalist" are accurate descriptors of the view, but the realist draft's
  compound coinage is not a label Parfit uses verbatim. Minor — descriptively correct,
  terminologically loose.

### Korsgaard / Ferrero — constitutivism (rationalist)
- **Voice → who:** Rationalist → Korsgaard (*Self-Constitution*; *Sources of
  Normativity*) and Ferrero ("Constitutivism and the Inescapability of Agency").
- **The claim:** Practical principles are "constitutive standards of agency... internal
  to the very activity they govern," arising "from the nature of agency itself"
  (Korsgaard); agency is "an inescapable enterprise that has the largest jurisdiction
  and is closed under rational assessment," such that "a systematic failure to be
  guided by the norms of rationality and morality would amount to a loss or lack of
  agency," grounding unconditional authority and answering Enoch's shmagency objection
  (Ferrero).
- **What the source says:** Ferrero confirmed essentially verbatim — "inescapable"
  enterprise, "largest jurisdiction," "closed under rational assessment," the
  loss-of-agency formulation, and the explicit reply to Enoch's shmagency objection.
  (philarchive.org/rec/FERCAT; researchgate 228778900.) Korsgaard's view confirmed as
  represented: constitutive principles "identify the conditions upon which doing x
  counts as an activity of a certain kind" and supply its standards of correctness;
  hypothetical and categorical imperatives are constitutive of rational agency.
  (Harvard Philosophy; UVM Cuneo/Harp review.)
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE (Ferrero quotes verbatim-confirmed; Korsgaard substance
  confirmed).
- **Note:** The Korsgaard strings "internal to the very activity they govern" /
  "constitutive standards of agency" were not located word-for-word in the secondary
  sources reached, but they accurately render her *Self-Constitution* position; no
  distortion. The rationalist's gloss of the Kantian "fact of reason" and autonomy as
  self-legislation is standard and matches the SEP "Kant's Moral Philosophy" entry it
  cites.

### Enoch — "Agency, Shmagency" (rationalist citation + naturalist attack round)
- **Voice → who:** Rationalist (as the challenge constitutivism must answer) and
  Naturalist (attack round: "one can fully exercise agency while flouting morality").
- **What the source says:** Enoch's shmagency objection holds that participation in
  agency is optional, so what is constitutive of action cannot yield categorical
  normativity — confirmed as the live objection Ferrero answers. (Ferrero source above;
  Enoch, *Philosophical Review*.)
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. Correctly framed as the objection, not as Enoch's positive view.

### Heidegger / Levinas / Merleau-Ponty / Husserl (phenomenologist)
- **Voice → who:** Phenomenologist → SEP "Heidegger," SEP "Levinas," Merleau-Ponty
  *Phenomenology of Perception*, Husserl *Crisis*.
- **The claim:** Heidegger — being-in-the-world, ready-to-hand vs present-at-hand,
  circumspective coping "non-subject-object in form," theoretical knowing a *deficient/
  founded* mode arising on breakdown. Levinas — ethics as first philosophy, the face
  "implores or commands," demand "at a precognitive level," "prior to any subject-object
  relation"; Perpich's "normativity without norms." Husserl — Galilean mathematization,
  objectivist forgetting, the *Lebenswelt* as forgotten meaning-fundament.
- **What the source says:** These are standard, accurate renderings of the cited SEP
  entries and texts. The quoted phrases ("non-subject-object in form," face that
  "implores or commands," "prior to any subject-object relation") match the SEP
  Heidegger and Levinas entries. Perpich's "normativity without norms" is a correctly
  attributed gloss.
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. No checkable misquotation found; these are mainstream
  expository attributions to encyclopedia sources, used as such.

### Nāgārjuna — MMK verses against Garfield (contemplative) — PRIORITY
- **Voice → who:** Contemplative → Nāgārjuna, MMK Ch. 24, "verified against Garfield's
  translation."
- **What the source says (Garfield, *Fundamental Wisdom of the Middle Way*, OUP 1995,
  canonical numbering, verified against the full archive.org text and Wikipedia's
  Garfield quotations):**
  - **24.18** — Draft: "Whatever is dependently co-arisen, that is explained to be
    emptiness; that, being a dependent designation, is itself the middle way."
    Garfield 24.18 is **word-for-word identical.** ACCURATE.
  - **24.8-9** — Draft fuses the two-truths line and the "do not understand the
    distinction" line and labels it "24.8-9." Garfield: 24.8 = the two-truths line,
    24.9 = the "do not understand" line. Content correct; presented as a fused
    paraphrase, not Garfield's exact lineation. ACCURATE (content) / lightly recombined.
  - **24.10** — Draft: "Without relying on convention, the ultimate is not taught;
    without reaching the ultimate, nirvāṇa is not attained." Matches Garfield 24.10 in
    sense. ACCURATE.
  - **24.20** — Draft: "If all this were non-empty, there would be no arising and
    ceasing." Matches Garfield 24.20 ("If all this were nonempty... There would be no
    arising and ceasing..."), lightly trimmed. ACCURATE.
  - **24.36** — Draft: "Whoever undermines emptiness... also undermines all the
    conventions of the world." Garfield 24.36: "If dependent arising is denied. /
    Emptiness itself is rejected. / This would contradict / All of the worldly
    conventions." Reasonable paraphrase of the import; OVERSTATED-as-verbatim but
    accurate in sense.
  - **24.14 — THE PROBLEM.** Draft presents as a "Verbatim rendering" credited to
    Garfield: **"For whom emptiness is possible, everything is possible. For whom
    emptiness is not possible, nothing is possible."** Garfield's actual **24.14**
    reads: **"For him to whom emptiness is clear, / Everything becomes clear. / For him
    to whom emptiness is not clear, / Nothing becomes clear."** The draft's
    "possible/possible" wording is **not Garfield's** — it is the rendering found in
    other translators (e.g., the Kalupahana / Siderits-Katsura lineage), not in the
    edition the draft explicitly names and claims to have verified against.
- **Verdict on 24.14:** DISTORTED (verbatim claim false). The verse number is correct
  and the *philosophical point* (emptiness as the enabling condition) is faithfully
  Nāgārjuna's, so this is not a fabricated verse — but it is a **misattributed
  quotation**: a different translator's words presented as a "verbatim rendering...
  verified against Jay L. Garfield's translation," which is exactly the kind of
  confident sourcing error this check exists to catch.
- **Two-truths secondary framing (Garfield "Madhyamaka Is Not Nihilism"; SEP "Theory of
  Two Truths in India"; the snake-wrongly-grasped image at 24.11; opponent's objection
  at 24.1-6):** all ACCURATE. 24.1-6 does contain the opponent's charge that emptiness
  destroys the Four Noble Truths (confirmed in the Garfield text), 24.11 is the
  snake/spell image (confirmed), and Garfield's "to see something as empty is to see it
  as conventional/dependent, not unreal" is correctly attributed.

## Summary of flagged issues
1. **(DISTORTED) Nāgārjuna MMK 24.14, contemplative draft.** A "possible/possible"
   rendering is presented as Garfield's verbatim text and "verified against Garfield's
   translation," but Garfield's 24.14 reads "clear/clear." Correct verse number,
   correct philosophical point, wrong translator's words falsely credited to Garfield.
   Fix: either requote Garfield ("For him to whom emptiness is clear, everything becomes
   clear...") or change the attribution to the actual source of the "possible" wording.
2. **(MINOR / terminological) Parfit's view-name, realist draft.** "Non-Metaphysical
   Non-Naturalist Normative Cognitivism" is a loose coinage; Parfit's standard label is
   "Non-Realist Cognitivism." Descriptors are accurate; the compound name is not
   Parfit's verbatim.
3. **(MINOR / unverifiable-exact) Field 1989 p. 26 verbatim quote, realist draft.** The
   substance (Field recasts Benacerraf as a reliability challenge, sets aside
   knowledge-conditions, "remote entities... reflect the facts") is independently
   confirmed and is a genuine Field quotation; the exact page-26 string could not be
   machine-extracted from the source PDFs, so the precise wording is reported by the
   draft rather than re-verified here. Not a fabrication; flagged for human spot-check
   only if the exact string becomes load-bearing.

No reversed conclusions, no dropped qualifications that flip an argument's import, and
no invented thinkers or wholesale fabricated verses were found. Enoch's "remaining
miracle" concession — the highest-risk item — is quoted accurately by both the
naturalist (who weaponizes it) and the realist (who reframes it); the divergence
between them is interpretation, not misattribution.
