## Verdict space

The thesis is meta-theoretic: *transparent representationalism is a better model
than illusionism because it saves the appearances at lower cost.* "Better" is pinned
to one axis — minimize joint cost of (saving the first-person datum) + (theoretical
debt). The load-bearing premise is **P3** (transparency genuinely accounts for the
felt datum rather than relocating it), with its hidden **SP-A** (representational
content can *constitute/ground* felt character, not merely co-vary). Second site of
weight is **P7 / SP-D** (the all-else-equal clause and the unstated axis-weighting
that licenses "wins on conservatism" → "better model simpliciter").

The serious answers:

1. **TRUE (defend P3 in strong identity form).** Transparency does save the
   phenomena: the felt character of redness just *is* the experience's directedness
   at redness-out-there, no residue left over, and introspection of that content is
   veridical. SP-A holds. Illusionism alone pays the conservatism penalty.
   → *thesis TRUE.*

2. **FALSE — P3/SP-A fails (relocation).** Identifying character with content does
   not explain felt character; it relabels it. The explanatory gap survives the
   identity claim. Neither theory saves the phenomena; transparency merely *loses
   less candidly*. (Chalmers/Goff realist flank.) → *thesis FALSE.*

3. **FALSE — P7/SP-D fails (wrong axis).** Grant transparency wins conservatism.
   "Better model" still doesn't follow: illusionism is leaner ontologically (zero
   phenomenal properties) and dissolves the hard problem rather than answering it.
   The conservatism axis is not decisive; on the axes that matter for a science of
   mind, illusionism wins. → *thesis FALSE* (on the all-things-considered reading the
   word "better" smuggles in).

4. **REJECTS-FRAMING — P5/P6 invert (conservatism is a vice).** "Saving the
   appearances" is not a virtue here; it is preserving the very illusion that
   manufactures the hard problem. The conservatism penalty the thesis charges to
   illusionism is illusionism's whole point. (Frankish.) → *thesis FALSE by
   re-valuing its evaluative premise.*

5. **ILL-POSED — SP-C fails (no theory-neutral explanandum).** There is no fixed
   "manifest datum" prior to theory to be saved or declared illusory; "how it seems"
   is itself a theory-laden construct. P1/P2 have no stable referent, so the
   cost-accounting never gets off the ground. (Dennett heterophenomenology — and a
   distinct contemplative variant.) → *thesis ILL-POSED.*

## Fixed-seat assignments

### Seat: naturalist
- **Assigned verdict:** FALSE
- **Premise tied to:** **P7 + SP-D** (the all-else-equal clause and the suppressed
  axis-weighting premise). Concedes P3/P4 arguendo; attacks the move from
  axis-superiority to "better model."
- **One-line brief:** "You hold the thesis is FALSE because P7 is false and SP-D is
  unearned: even granting transparency saves the appearances more conservatively,
  illusionism is the better *scientific* model — it is ontologically leaner (it
  posits no phenomenal properties for a physics to find a home for) and it dissolves
  the hard problem instead of inheriting it, so the conservatism axis the thesis
  privileges is not the decisive theory-choice axis in a mature science of mind."
- **Collapse risk:** **flag — re-aimed.** The naturalist's *natural* verdict here is
  straight strong illusionism, which would duplicate the Frankish specialist. Re-aimed
  off the first-order "introspection misrepresents" claim (P4's content) and onto the
  *theory-choice / axis-weighting* attack (P7, SP-D). The naturalist never argues
  introspection lies; it argues that even if transparency is the more conservative
  theory, conservatism loses to parsimony + hard-problem-dissolution under naturalist
  scoring. Distinct move from the illusionist specialist (who fights at P4/P5) and
  from the rationalist (who fights at P3/SP-A).

### Seat: rationalist
- **Assigned verdict:** FALSE
- **Premise tied to:** **P3 + SP-A** (the anti-relocation gap). This is the
  load-bearing premise.
- **One-line brief:** "You hold the thesis is FALSE because P3 fails on SP-A: the
  identity of phenomenal character with representational content is not a priori
  intelligible — one can conceive the full representational-content facts holding
  with the felt character absent or inverted, so content can at most co-vary with
  character, not constitute it; transparency therefore does not *account for* the
  datum, it relocates and relabels it, leaving the explanatory gap exactly where
  illusionism leaves it — meaning neither theory saves the phenomena and the
  comparative advantage in P3 is illusory."
- **Collapse risk:** **none** vs. the other fixed seats. Shares the FALSE verdict
  with the naturalist but reaches it by attacking a *different* premise (P3/SP-A, the
  reduction itself) from a *different* commitment (a priori conceivability / the
  explanatory gap), not P7/SP-D. *Overlap watch with the Chalmers/Goff specialist:*
  see specialist notes — the rationalist runs the conceivability/intelligibility
  engine, the specialist runs the first-person phenomenal-realist datum; if they
  converge, re-aim the rationalist to press the *self-application* edge (does the
  thesis's own appeal to "saving appearances" covertly assume the very intrinsic
  character it claims to reduce). Held distinct as written.

### Seat: phenomenologist
- **Assigned verdict:** TRUE
- **Premise tied to:** **P3** in its strong/identity form (Charitable Variant 1) —
  defends SP-A by denying there is any felt residue to relocate.
- **One-line brief:** "You hold the thesis is TRUE because P3 is correct as a report
  of what disciplined attention actually finds: when you introspect an experience of
  red, you find only red-as-presented — the world's properties, not an extra inner
  qualitative paint — so the diaphanousness of experience is a *phenomenological
  datum*, not a theoretical posit; this means introspection is broadly *veridical*
  about what it delivers (worldly directedness), and transparency saves that datum at
  face value, whereas illusionism must convict the very faculty that delivers the
  datum of systematic error it cannot motivate from within experience."
- **Collapse risk:** **flag — guarded.** Two dangers. (1) The phenomenologist's
  *natural* instinct is to defend an irreducible felt given, which would duplicate the
  Chalmers/Goff realist. Re-aimed: this seat is cast to take Harman's transparency
  observation as the *phenomenological* finding — attention discloses no intrinsic
  qualia — which its tradition can hold honestly (the diaphanousness thesis is itself
  a phenomenological claim). (2) The near-ally trap: denying intrinsic qualia is
  exactly where transparency and illusionism *agree*, so this seat could collapse into
  the illusionist. Guarded by anchoring the brief to the **veridicality of
  introspection** — the one axis on which the two views have opposite truth-conditions.
  The phenomenologist says introspection *succeeds* (delivers worldly directedness
  truly); the illusionist says it *fails* (misattributes phenomenality). Same denial of
  thick qualia, opposite verdict on introspective reliability. That is the non-verbal
  residue the pseudo-dispute check isolated, and it keeps this seat from echoing the
  illusionist.

### Seat: contemplative
- **Assigned verdict:** ILL-POSED (rejects-framing)
- **Premise tied to:** **SP-C / P1-P2** (the explanandum-stability premise).
- **One-line brief:** "You hold the thesis is ILL-POSED because SP-C is false: there
  is no fixed, theory-neutral 'manifest datum of introspective reports of qualitative
  character' standing still to be either saved or declared illusory — sustained
  contemplative attention shows that the apparent qualitative character and even the
  reporting subject are themselves constructed, fluctuating, and partly produced by
  the act of looking, so both transparency and illusionism share a false premise (a
  stable explanandum + a stable introspector), and the comparative cost-accounting in
  P2/P5 never acquires a determinate target — the question of which model 'saves the
  appearances at lower cost' has no fact of the matter."
- **Collapse risk:** **flag — re-aimed away from Dennett.** The ill-posed verdict via
  SP-C is *also* Dennett's move, so this seat risks duplicating the optional
  heterophenomenology specialist. Re-aimed: Dennett dissolves the datum from the
  *third-person operationalist* side (only verbal/behavioral reports are data). The
  contemplative dissolves it from the *first-person disciplined-attention* side (the
  introspected datum and the introspector are impermanent constructions, observable as
  such under training). Same SP-C target, opposite epistemic route — and the
  contemplative route is one the heterophenomenologist explicitly cannot take (it
  trusts a refined first-person practice Dennett rejects in principle). This makes the
  Dennett specialist *optional*, not redundant: see notes.

## Span check

- **Seats defending thesis TRUE:** phenomenologist — **count = 1.** Satisfies ≥1. ✓
- **Seats opposing the thesis:** naturalist (FALSE), rationalist (FALSE),
  contemplative (ILL-POSED) — **count = 3.** Satisfies ≥2. ✓
- **Duplicate-move check:** **PASS.**
  - naturalist FALSE @ P7/SP-D (axis-weighting) ≠ rationalist FALSE @ P3/SP-A
    (the reduction itself). Different premise, different engine.
  - rationalist FALSE @ P3/SP-A (conceivability) vs. phenomenologist TRUE @ P3
    (identity, no residue): same premise, *opposite verdict* — direct contest on the
    load-bearing premise, exactly what we want. Not a duplicate; a head-to-head.
  - contemplative ILL-POSED @ SP-C ≠ all three above (attacks P1/P2's target stability
    rather than P3/P4/P7).
  - Re-aims applied to all three flagged seats (naturalist off illusionism→P7;
    phenomenologist off realism→transparency-as-datum; contemplative off
    Dennett→first-person construction). No two fixed seats make the same move.

Verdict space coverage: TRUE (1), FALSE-via-P3 (2), FALSE-via-P7 (1), ILL-POSED-via-SP-C
(4) are all seated. The remaining serious answer — **reject-framing via P5/P6
re-valuation (conservatism is a vice, Frankish)** — is *not* covered by a fixed seat
and is the strongest direct opponent of the thesis. It must be summoned. See below.

## Specialists to summon

### 1. Frankish-style strong illusionist — **ESSENTIAL, summon.**
- **Exact stance:** Introspection systematically misrepresents functional states as
  phenomenally charactered (defends P4 as true *and as a virtue*), and re-values
  **P5/P6**: conservatism about the appearances is the bug, not the feature, because
  the "appearance" being saved is the illusion that generates the hard problem.
  Replacing the hard problem with the illusion problem is a net theoretical *gain*.
- **Premise targeted:** P5/P6 (inverts the evaluative weight) and defends P4.
- **Why the fixed four can't voice it:** The naturalist was deliberately re-aimed
  *off* this move to P7/SP-D to avoid duplication, so no fixed seat argues that global
  introspective error is true-and-good. This is the thesis's named direct opponent and
  the council is incomplete without it. It occupies verdict-space slot 4, which no
  fixed seat holds.

### 2. Chalmers/Goff-style phenomenal realist — **SUMMON.**
- **Exact stance:** Neither theory saves the phenomena; transparency *relocates* the
  felt character into represented content that patently lacks intrinsic qualitative
  character. P3 is false because SP-A is false — but argued from the *positive reality*
  of phenomenal character (the felt given is the most certain datum we have), not from
  conceivability alone.
- **Premise targeted:** P3 / SP-A, from the realist commitment.
- **Why the fixed four can't voice it:** The rationalist attacks P3/SP-A too, but via
  a priori conceivability/intelligibility — a *negative* engine (you cannot make the
  identity intelligible). The specialist runs the *positive* engine (phenomenal
  character is a present reality that the reduction omits). Distinct move on the same
  premise; together they pincer P3. **Caution to orchestrator:** monitor for
  rationalist↔specialist convergence; if they collapse, push the rationalist to the
  self-application edge flagged in its seat note.

### 3. Dennett-style heterophenomenologist — **OPTIONAL, do not summon by default.**
- **Exact stance:** No theory-neutral appearance exists to be saved; only verbal and
  behavioral data are admissible, and "how it seems" is a theory-laden report, not a
  datum. Attacks SP-C from third-person operationalism.
- **Premise targeted:** SP-C / P1.
- **Why it is optional:** The contemplative seat already holds the ILL-POSED/SP-C
  corner, re-aimed to a first-person route that is genuinely distinct from Dennett's
  third-person route. Summoning Dennett adds a *second* attacker on SP-C from the
  opposite epistemic direction, which has value only if the council's SP-C corner
  looks thin or if the orchestrator wants the third-person-vs-first-person contrast on
  the dissolution move made explicit. **Recommendation: hold in reserve; promote only
  if the contemplative's SP-C attack reads as mystical/idiosyncratic rather than as a
  serious dissolution the thesis must answer.** Do not summon merely for coverage —
  SP-C is already seated.

## Casting notes for the orchestrator

- **The hottest exchange is at P3.** Rationalist (FALSE, conceivability) and
  Chalmers/Goff specialist (FALSE, positive realism) on one side; phenomenologist
  (TRUE, transparency-as-datum, no residue) on the other — all three at the
  load-bearing premise. This is where the crux will likely localize. Make sure the
  attack round (full mode) routes these three against each other.

- **Guard the near-ally collapse explicitly in seat prompts.** When you paste the
  phenomenologist and the illusionist briefs, remind each that they *agree* on denying
  thick/intrinsic qualia and *split only on whether introspection is veridical or
  systematically erroneous*. If they start arguing about whether qualia exist, the
  distinction-watchdog flag (and the framer's pseudo-dispute warning) has fired — pull
  them back to the veridicality axis.

- **The naturalist re-aim is load-bearing for span.** If the naturalist drifts back
  into "introspection misrepresents" (its gravitational pull), it duplicates the
  Frankish specialist and the council loses its P7/SP-D coverage — leaving the
  axis-weighting flank (verdict-space slot 3) unmanned. Hold it to P7/SP-D.

- **One verdict-space slot is covered only by a specialist:** the P5/P6 re-valuation
  (conservatism-as-vice) lives entirely with the Frankish illusionist. That is why it
  is marked ESSENTIAL rather than optional. If for some reason only one specialist can
  be summoned, summon the illusionist, not the realist — the realist's premise (P3) is
  already double-covered by fixed seats, whereas the illusionist's (P5/P6 inversion) is
  not covered at all.

- **No pragmatist seated.** The author's own pragmatism (treat "which model is better"
  as a question of explanatory bookkeeping) is the *frame* of the thesis itself; it is
  not given a defending chair. If the council needs the deflationary "the dispute is
  merely verbal / pick the more useful tool" reply, summon it as a specialist for the
  council to attack — do not seat it.
