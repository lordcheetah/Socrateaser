# Representationalism vs Illusionism — Naturalist (council)
> **Run:** representationalism-vs-illusionism · **Mode:** full · **Stage:** Council
> **Thesis:** Transparent representationalism is a better model of phenomenal consciousness than illusionism because it saves the appearances at lower explanatory cost — without convicting introspection of systematic error.
> **Seat:** Naturalist — hard naturalist, Quine–Dennett register
> **Defending:** VERDICT: FALSE — the thesis wins only by smuggling in SP-D, the unstated premise that conservatism-about-introspection is the *decisive* axis of theory choice; on the naturalist's actual ledger of theoretical virtues, that axis is one weight among several and not the heaviest, so illusionism's leaner ontology and dissolution of the hard problem outscore transparency's conservatism advantage.

VERDICT: FALSE — the thesis wins only by smuggling in SP-D, the unstated premise that conservatism-about-introspection is the *decisive* axis of theory choice; on the naturalist's actual ledger of theoretical virtues, that axis is one weight among several and not the heaviest, so illusionism's leaner ontology and dissolution of the hard problem outscore transparency's conservatism advantage.

## Case

I concede the comparative bookkeeping the thesis wants me to concede. Grant P3: transparent representationalism does account for the explanandum without convicting introspection of global error. Grant P4: strong illusionism discharges the same explanandum only by positing a global introspective misrepresentation. So on the single dimension the thesis names — *not declaring the manifest datum illusory* — transparency is the more conservative theory. Fine. I am not going to fight P3 or P4; that is the Chalmers/Goff seat's job and the Frankish seat's job respectively.

My move is upstream of all of that. The thesis is comparative — "better *model*" — and the reconstruction itself flags (SP-D, P7) that getting from "wins on the conservatism axis" to "is the better model" requires an unstated premise: that conservatism is the *decisive* axis. **That premise is false, and it is false for reasons internal to naturalist theory-choice.** The conservatism of introspection is not a privileged tribunal. It is one virtue on a list, and when it collides with the others it does not automatically win.

Start with the canonical naturalist statement of how theories are chosen. In *The Web of Belief*, Quine and Ullian enumerate the virtues of hypotheses as a *plurality* held in tension: conservatism, modesty (positing fewer further commitments), simplicity, generality, and refutability ([Quine & Ullian, *The Web of Belief*](https://www.goodreads.com/en/book/show/931301); summarized in [Stanford Encyclopedia on underdetermination](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/scientific-underdetermination/)). Conservatism appears on that list — "the fewer existing beliefs a new belief would interfere with, the better" — but it appears *as one entry*, explicitly weighed against simplicity, modesty, and scope. There is no Quinean ranking on which conservatism is lexically prior. Quine's deeper point in "Two Dogmas" and after is that the web faces the tribunal of experience *as a whole*, and revision can fall anywhere — including on the beliefs nearest the periphery of report. Nothing is immune to revision in principle, and that explicitly includes the deliverances of introspection. To treat "don't impugn introspection" as the decisive criterion is to exempt one class of beliefs from the holistic trade-off Quine insists no belief enjoys. That is not conservatism functioning as a virtue; it is conservatism inflated into a first philosophy about the mind, exactly the move the naturalist denies anyone.

Now weigh the axes the thesis sets aside under P7's "all else equal." They are not equal. On **ontological economy** — Quine's modesty and simplicity — illusionism posits strictly fewer kinds of thing. It quantifies over functional/representational states and the brain's misrepresentation of them; it posits *no* phenomenal properties at all. Transparency, even in its leanest Tye-style form, must keep "represented worldly properties that introspection veridically delivers as felt character" doing real explanatory labor. Illusionism's domain of quantification is a proper subset. By the very modesty criterion Quine names, the leaner theory is to be preferred when it covers the data. And it does cover the data: the illusionist owes an account of why introspection *says* there is felt character, which is the same report-explanandum transparency must explain. Both explain the reports; only one of them does so without a phenomenal-property posit.

On **fecundity / hard-problem dissolution**, the asymmetry is sharper still. Dennett's argument — which I am *not* defending wholesale, but invoking only to show what the conservatism axis is being traded against — is that illusionism is "the obvious default theory of consciousness," and that consciousness is "a user-illusion brilliantly designed by evolution to fit the needs of its users" ([Dennett, as reported in Nagel's NYRB review](https://www.nybooks.com/articles/2017/03/09/is-consciousness-an-illusion-dennett-evolution/); cf. [Dennett, "Illusionism as the Obvious Default Theory of Consciousness," *JCS* 2016](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11229-020-02548-9)). The payoff is theoretical fecundity: by relocating the explanandum from "why is there felt character" to "why does the brain *represent* there as being felt character," illusionism converts the hard problem into the meta-problem — a tractable, third-person, mechanistic question. That is what a fertile research program looks like: it hands working scientists a problem they can actually attack with the prediction-error / global-workspace tools they already have ([Synthese, "Fame in the predictive brain"](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11229-020-02548-9)). Transparency, by contrast, leaves the hard problem standing and merely insists introspection was right all along. *Saving the appearances is not a research program.* It is a constraint that, taken as decisive, freezes inquiry at the manifest image.

And that is the heart of the naturalist objection to the *axis*: the thesis treats convicting introspection of error as a cost to be minimized. The history of naturalistic inquiry treats convicting a faculty of systematic error as a *standard and successful explanatory move*. The Churchlands' point about folk psychology generalizes precisely here: introspection is theory-laden, and "what we introspect may be largely determined by our folk psychological framework" — "introspecting" felt qualia may be just like people "seeing" demonic spirits or celestial spheres ([Stanford Encyclopedia on Eliminative Materialism](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/materialism-eliminative/)). Empirical work on introspective unreliability bolsters this. A naturalist does not regard "your introspective report is systematically misleading" as a debt the theory must apologize for; she regards it as the kind of debunking explanation that paid off for vital spirits, phlogiston, and absolute simultaneity. The thesis's whole accounting treats a *virtue* of illusionism — its willingness to subordinate first-person seeming to the best total theory — as a *vice*. That inversion is only compelling if you have already decided introspection gets a special pass. The naturalist hasn't, and shouldn't: there is no privileged armchair access to how the mind must be, and "it really seems that way" is precisely the datum a science of mind is supposed to *explain*, not to ratify.

So even granting P3 and P4 in full, the inference to "better model" fails. The thesis is valid only on the restricted conservatism axis (the reconstruction's own qualification at P7/SP-D). Off that axis — on modesty, simplicity, and fecundity, the other named Quinean virtues — illusionism wins. And there is no neutral, naturalistically respectable reason to make conservatism-about-introspection the tiebreaker. The appearance that we *need* to save the introspective appearances is itself an artifact of taking the manifest image as bedrock, which is the one thing a naturalist will not grant.

## What I deny and why

I deny **SP-D** (the axis-weighting premise) and therefore **P7** ("all else relevantly equal").

- **SP-D is false.** The move from "transparency wins on conservatism" to "transparency is the better model" requires that conservatism-about-introspection be the decisive theory-choice axis. No naturalist account of theory choice ranks it so. Quine's own list (conservatism, modesty, simplicity, generality, refutability) holds these in tension with no lexical priority for conservatism, and his holism explicitly denies any belief — including introspective report — immunity from revision. Making "save the appearances" decisive *just is* exempting one class of beliefs from the web's trade-offs, which is the privileged armchair access naturalism rejects.

- **P7 is false.** Other things are not equal. Illusionism is strictly more modest ontologically (no phenomenal properties posited) and strictly more fecund (it converts the intractable hard problem into the tractable meta-problem, feeding an active research program). The thesis can only call these a tie by treating ontological economy and fruitfulness as off-axis — which is exactly to assume SP-D.

I do *not* deny P3 or P4. My quarrel is not "qualia don't exist" (first-order, the Frankish seat) nor "transparency relocates the explanandum" (the Goff seat). It is meta-theoretic: **the thesis names the wrong scorecard.** Scored on the naturalist's actual virtues, the leaner, more fertile theory is the better model even if it is the less conservative one — and being less conservative about an unreliable faculty is no mark against a scientific theory at all.

## Attack round

**vs. Phenomenologist (TRUE).** Your whole case rests on the diaphanousness datum: "go attend, and you find a world, no inner screen." But that is an introspective report, and introspective reports have no special authority over a science of mind — they are *data to be explained*, not verdicts about what is there. Schwitzgebel's work on the unreliability of introspection (and the Churchlands' theory-ladenness point) shows that "I attend and find only the world" is exactly the kind of report a naturalized account predicts a self-monitoring system would issue whether or not it is true. You treat "careful description" as a tribunal that overrules theory; that is phenomenology smuggled in as first philosophy. Husserlian "essential structure" is armchair access to how mind *must* be — the move I deny anyone.

**vs. Rationalist (FALSE, gap unsecured).** Your engine is the explanatory gap: SP-A is "not a priori secured" because "why does *this* content feel like *that*?" stays intelligible. Grant the intelligibility — it shows nothing metaphysical. The gap is an *epistemic fact about us*, our concepts and modes of presentation, not a gap in nature. Levine, whom you cite, says exactly this: it "doesn't demonstrate a gap in nature, but a gap in our understanding." A premise can be true without being a priori securable or analytically transparent; your demand that constitution be *secured by reason* before it counts is the rationalist criterion, not a fact about the world. Conceivability tracks our concepts, not modal reality (the a-posteriori-necessity literature you cite cuts against you).

**vs. Realist (FALSE, content can't constitute feel).** Same diagnosis, sharper. Mary and Inverted Earth are conceivability/intuition pumps; they license an anti-physicalist *metaphysics* only if intuited dissociability entails real dissociability — the very inference a naturalist rejects. "Acquaintance with the determinate phenomenal given" is just a confident introspective report wearing certainty as a badge; certainty of seeming is not authority about nature. Your candor point cuts the other way: a theory that posits irreducible qualities with *no causal story of how we contact them* is not honest, it is a placeholder for ignorance. Demand the causal story for "acquaintance" and the realist ontology evaporates.

**vs. Illusionist (FALSE).** We are allies on the verdict, and your hard-problem-as-motivation point is strong. But you over-weight error-debunking *for its own sake*: the live virtue is not "introspection is wrong" but *fruitfulness* — converting the hard problem into the tractable meta-problem and feeding real research. "Conservatism is a vice" overstates it; conservatism is a genuine Quinean virtue, just not decisive here, outweighed by modesty and fecundity. Frame the win as the leaner, more fertile theory, not as the thrill of catching introspection in a lie. And drop "quasi-phenomenal properties" if it tempts you toward a new posit; the cleaner naturalist line is functional states plus a model of why they get *reported* as phenomenal.

**vs. Contemplative (ILL-POSED).** Your dissolution is the most interesting threat to my verdict, because if there is no stable explanandum my scorecard has nothing to score. But the move proves too much. The reports are stable enough to be measured, modeled, and predicted — that is all my naturalist scorecard needs; I quantify over the *reporting*, not a reified "felt given." Your Abhidharma/Madhyamaka claim that attention "dissolves" the datum is itself a trained introspective report, and so falls under the same suspicion I press on the phenomenologist: no special authority. "Ultimate vs. conventional" is doing the work a naturalist does with "manifest vs. scientific image" — minus the empirical accountability. I keep the conventional level, naturalize it, and your ultimate level is an unneeded posit.
