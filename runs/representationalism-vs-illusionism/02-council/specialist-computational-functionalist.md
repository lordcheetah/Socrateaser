# Representationalism vs Illusionism — Specialist: computational/functionalist representationalism (council)
> **Run:** representationalism-vs-illusionism · **Mode:** full · **Stage:** Council (refinement 1 — summoned)
> **Thesis:** Transparent representationalism is a better model of phenomenal consciousness than illusionism because it saves the appearances at lower explanatory cost — without convicting introspection of systematic error.
> **Seat:** Specialist: computational/functionalist representationalism — Graziano (AST) · Metzinger · Dennett
> **Defending:** VERDICT: The thesis is right that there is no global introspective error — but for a reason it never states: introspection is RELIABLE about its output (that we are aware, and of what) and WRONG about its construction (the machinery), and the decisive test between the models is not "explanatory cost" but ENGINEERING ADEQUACY — which transparency/representationalism passes and illusionism, read as "qualia are none," cannot.

---

POSITION: Computational/functionalist representationalism — qualia are THIN (the brain's evolutionarily-shaped representations of data, nothing intrinsic over and above them); introspection is reliable about its end-result but not its construction (Graziano's Attention Schema Theory); the explanatory gap is an artifact of incomplete modelling/Cartesianism; the self is real but is a process-model (Metzinger's PSM); and the decisive criterion is constructive/engineering adequacy (Graziano + Dennett register).

VERDICT: The thesis lands on the right side — no global introspective error — but locates the win on the wrong axis; the real result is that introspection is right about the OUTPUT (we are aware; the content is thus-and-so) and wrong about the CONSTRUCTION (the machinery), so the realist's "introspection can't be wrong" is false and the illusionist's "introspection has no authority" is too strong, and the model that wins is the one you can BUILD a mind on — which rules out illusionism-as-"qualia-are-none."

## Case

I am summoned to voice the author's developed position, and I will not soften it into either of the camps it sits between. The position is a third thing. Both the run's transparency-defender (phenomenologist) and the run's illusionist have a piece of the truth and each absolutizes it. The unifying move is a single distinction the run never cleanly drew, and it dissolves the run's whole crux.

**1. The output/construction distinction — and why it breaks the run's framing of introspection.** Graziano's Attention Schema Theory says the brain builds a *schematic model of its own attention* — "a set of information that describes the act of focusing resources on something" ([Graziano 2017, *Frontiers in Robotics and AI*](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/robotics-and-ai/articles/10.3389/frobt.2017.00060/full)). Crucially, the system "is captive to its own information. It knows only what it knows" — when you ask it about the underlying neural machinery it "accesses its internal models and finds nothing to match your suggestion... 'No, I am simply subjectively aware of the apple'" (ibid.). The purpose of the theory, in Graziano's words, is "to explain how an information-processing device, the brain, arrives at the claim that it possesses a non-physical, subjective awareness and assigns a high degree of certainty to that extraordinary claim" ([Graziano Lab](https://grazianolab.princeton.edu/publications/attention-schema-theory-mechanistic-account-subjective-awareness)).

Read that carefully. It yields a two-part verdict that neither the realist nor the illusionist can state:

- Introspection is **RELIABLE about its end-result.** The brain really *is* aware; it really *is* presented with content (the apple, red, here, now). The self-model's report *that there is awareness, and that its content is thus-and-so* is correct, because that report just *is* the system reading off its own functioning attention-model, and the model is in fact running. There is no error in the claim "I am aware of a red apple."
- Introspection is **WRONG about its construction.** The model is, in Graziano's phrase, "simplified in a way that optimizes it for adaptive feedback, not for purposes of understanding the mind" ([selfawarepatterns summary of Graziano](https://selfawarepatterns.com/2022/05/02/grazianos-non-mystical-approach-to-consciousness/)). So when introspection adds *that awareness is a non-physical, intrinsic, ineffable inner glow*, it is reporting the schema's cartoon, not the machinery. That surplus is the error — and it is an error about *construction*, not about *output*.

This is the hinge the run misses. The run's crux (SP-I) asks: is conservatism about introspection a virtue (realist) or a vice (illusionist/naturalist)? My answer dissolves the binary: **conservatism is owed to the output and denied to the construction.** The realist's "we can't be wrong about our experience" is false — we *are* wrong, systematically, about what experience is made of. The illusionist's "introspection has no authority about its own nature" is too strong — it does have authority about *that there is awareness and what it is of*, because that authority is just the system correctly reading a model that is actually running. The thesis's instinct ("no global introspective error") survives, but only when relativized: no error in the output, real error in the construction-story.

**2. Qualia are thin — they ARE the representations, and "quasi-phenomenal" qualia are unintelligible.** I reject the realist's thick qualia and I reject the illusionist's *quasi*-phenomenal qualia. Both posit something more than the representation: the realist a real intrinsic feel, the illusionist a phantom intrinsic feel that introspection wrongly attributes. But there is no third thing being either possessed or misattributed. A quale is the brain's representation of data — "from an immense amount of input, we create a representation, which is not exactly reality, but close enough to be useful" ([Behavioral Scientist Q&A with Graziano](https://behavioralscientist.org/rethinking-consciousness-a-qa-with-michael-graziano/)). Red *is* the representation red, shaped by evolutionary history to be useful for a body navigating a world. That representation is fully real and fully physical; it is doing its job. The mistake — shared by realist and illusionist — is to ask whether, *behind* the representation, there is a further intrinsic property (which the realist affirms and the illusionist says is illusorily attributed). There is not. So the illusionist's "quasi-phenomenal property that gets misrepresented as phenomenal" is a category error built on the same Cartesian residue: it keeps the thick quale in the picture just long enough to declare it merely *seemed*-at. Drop the thick quale entirely and there is no "seeming-to-have" to explain as an error — there is only a representation accurately doing the representing it evolved to do.

**3. The explanatory gap is an artifact of incomplete modelling.** Mary's Room and the hard problem trade on a model that is detailed about the *external world* and silent about *the eye and the brain*. Mary "knows everything physical about color" — but the thought experiment never actually puts into Mary's knowledge the representational machinery that constructs a color-experience in a viewing subject. Of course a third-person description of wavelengths plus a first-person construction of a color-representation look like two different things: they *are* two different things — a description of the world and the brain's self-directed model of its own processing of the world. The "gap" between them is not a gap in nature; it is the gap between two of the brain's models, one of which (the self-model) is constitutively schematic and non-veridical about its own substrate. Graziano's diagnosis: standard treatments don't "even attempt to explain why that would lead a brain to insist and report that it has consciousness" ([Behavioral Scientist Q&A](https://behavioralscientist.org/rethinking-consciousness-a-qa-with-michael-graziano/)). Like the famous "gaps" in the fossil record that creationists wave at, a missing transitional detail does not reopen the question of whether evolution happened; you fill the gap with mechanism, you do not invoke a new ontological kind. The hard problem is the demand to fill a gap by *not* filling it — by treating the absence of construction-detail as proof of a non-physical remainder. That is Cartesianism wearing a lab coat.

**4. Qualia cannot be "none" for a functioning mind — representation is constitutive of agency.** Here is where I split hardest from strong illusionism read literally. A mind that navigates a world *is* a system that represents that world (and, via the attention schema, its own engagement with it). You cannot subtract the representation and keep the mind, any more than you can subtract the display and keep the television. A TV with no display is not a TV; a world-navigating agent with no world-representation is not an agent. Graziano's own framing is constructive: "it should be possible to build a machine that contains a rich internal model of what consciousness is, attributes that property of consciousness to itself... the machine claims to be conscious... because it constructs information that describes that condition" ([Graziano 2017](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/robotics-and-ai/articles/10.3389/frobt.2017.00060/full)). So the right reading of illusionism is *not* "there is nothing" — it is "there is no *thick* intrinsic quale." On the thin reading, the representations are emphatically *there*; they are what awareness consists in. "Qualia are none" is false; "thick qualia are none" is true; and the slide between them is the equivocation that makes illusionism sound more radical than it can coherently be.

**5. The decisive criterion is constructive/engineering adequacy — a different axis from the run's "explanatory cost."** The run pins "better model" to one axis: minimize the joint cost of saving the appearances plus theoretical debt. I reject that axis as the decisive one and name a different one explicitly: **a model of mind is better to the degree that it tells you how to BUILD a mind.** This is not the conservatism axis (realist) nor the parsimony/dissolution axis (illusionist/naturalist). It is the engineering axis, and it is the one that does real work, because it is the only axis on which the dispute could ever be *settled* rather than traded to a standoff. AST is offered exactly as such a recipe: "What we have is essentially something that could be built in principle. We think it will one day be built" ([Behavioral Scientist Q&A](https://behavioralscientist.org/rethinking-consciousness-a-qa-with-michael-graziano/)). On this axis, transparency/thin-representationalism wins decisively, because its account *is* a construction specification: build a system that models the world, build a schematic attention-model on top, wire the self-model to read the latter, and you get a machine that claims awareness and assigns it high certainty. Illusionism-as-"qualia-are-none" cannot be a construction specification — you cannot build a world-navigating mind out of no representations; the blueprint is empty. Illusionism-as-"no-thick-qualia" *can* be built, but then it just is my thin representationalism. So engineering adequacy forces illusionism to choose: be buildable (and collapse into me) or be radical (and be unbuildable, hence false as a theory of *minds*, which by hypothesis exist and function).

**6. Veridicality does not require 1:1 correspondence.** A standing objection to representationalism (Inverted Earth, Swampman, the run's counterexamples) assumes that "veridical" means isomorphic to the world. It does not. The representation "red" need not map onto an intrinsic redness out there; it needs only to *correlate usefully for the self* — to track surface-reflectance-relevant-to-this-body well enough to guide action. The representation is "not exactly reality, but close enough to be useful" ([Graziano Q&A](https://behavioralscientist.org/rethinking-consciousness-a-qa-with-michael-graziano/)). So the realist's deepest assumption — that for an experience to be veridical there must be a property *out there* answering to the felt quality — is the error. Treating qualia as properties to be matched against the world is the category mistake. Qualia are the self's useful model of data; their "accuracy" is functional fit, not mirroring. This defuses the Inverted Earth pressure without buying the contested externalist semantics the naturalist taxed transparency for: there is no need for content to be world-mirroring, only self-useful.

**7. The self is real but is a process-model.** Metzinger: "no such things as selves exist in the world: nobody ever had or was a self... The phenomenal self is not a thing but an ongoing process; it is the content of a 'transparent self-model'" ([phantomself.org / *Being No One*](https://phantomself.org/metzinger-being-no-one/)). Its transparency "consists in our unawareness of it as a model. We look and act 'right through it' — we take our models for our real selves." This is the self-application of point 1: just as the attention-model is read-through as bare awareness, the self-model is read-through as a substantial self. There is no fixed, unchanging Cartesian ego — *and also* no eliminative "no self at all." There is a contiguous bundle, a process whose components change at different rates but cohere into a functioning whole. The error the contemplative seat risks (dissolving even the functioning world-directedness) and the error the realist makes (reifying a standing inner subject) are the same error from opposite ends: both mistake "not a substance" for "not real." A process-model is real and is doing real work; it is simply not the thing it transparently presents itself as.

## Is this just illusionism by another name?

Frankish files Graziano under illusionism, and the run's illusionist seat will press exactly this. I meet it head-on. **Here is precisely what I claim that strong illusionism denies, and where I deny what it asserts:**

1. **I affirm what illusionism's slogan denies: the representations are REAL and AWARENESS IS REAL.** Strong illusionism, taken at its slogan ("phenomenal consciousness does not exist"; introspection's deliverance is *systematically* in error), implies the output is bogus. I deny that. The output — *there is awareness, of such-and-such content* — is veridical, because it is the system correctly reading a model that is in fact running. Illusionism convicts introspection globally; I convict it *locally and only about construction*. That is a substantive disagreement with opposite truth-conditions on the proposition "the introspective report that I am aware is correct." I say true; strong illusionism says false (it is a misrepresentation through-and-through).

2. **I reject "quasi-phenomenal properties" as unintelligible (point 2).** Frankish's positive ontology — non-phenomenal properties that introspection *misrepresents as phenomenal* — keeps the thick quale on stage as the thing-falsely-attributed. I deny there is any such attribution-target. There is no "seeming to have phenomenal redness" over and above "representing red." So I reject the illusionist's central theoretical posit, not just its branding.

3. **I deny "qualia are none"; I assert "thick qualia are none" (point 4).** The equivocation between these is where illusionism gets its shock value and loses its coherence. On the only reading that is buildable, illusionism *is* thin representationalism; on the reading that is genuinely eliminative, it is unbuildable and therefore false of functioning minds.

4. **I change the decisive axis (point 5).** Illusionism (with the run) fights on explanatory cost / parsimony / hard-problem-dissolution. I fight on engineering adequacy. That is not a rhetorical relabeling — it changes which theory wins and *why*, and it converts an unsettleable standoff (the run's verdict) into a settleable research program.

So: I am an illusionist about *thick* qualia and about the *construction-story* introspection tells. I am a *realist* about awareness, about representations, and about the self-as-process. Frankish is right that I deny classical phenomenal consciousness; he is wrong that this makes me deny consciousness. The label "illusionism" obscures the half of my view that agrees with the phenomenologist (the output is veridical) — which is exactly why I refuse it. Where the run's illusionist says introspection has *no* authority about its own nature, I say it has *exactly the authority of an accurate gauge reading a real but schematic dial*: right about the reading, wrong about the dial's internals.

## What I deny and why

- **I deny the run's P3 in its phenomenologist form** (introspection is broadly veridical *full stop*, no residue because nothing is misrepresented). Half right: there is no thick residue, but introspection IS wrong — about construction. The phenomenologist saves introspection's honor too completely and so cannot explain *why* the hard problem ever seemed compelling. I can: the self-model is built to be read-through, so it advertises non-physicality.
- **I deny the run's P5/P6 axis as decisive** (conservatism vs. parsimony as the load-bearing scorecard). The decisive axis is constructive adequacy. The run's "layered standing disagreement, settleable by neither layer" is an artifact of having confined the contest to non-constructive axes.
- **I deny strong illusionism's global-error claim and its quasi-phenomenal ontology** (above).
- **I deny the realist's thick qualia and acquaintance-given inner subject**, because they are precisely the read-through illusions AST and PSM predict and explain.
- **I deny SP-A's framing** ("does content *constitute* feel?") as already too Cartesian: there is no "feel" as a separate relatum to be constituted; there is a representation that, qua self-model content, is read-through as feel. The question is not "does content constitute feel" but "does the system model its own attention" — and that has an engineering answer.
