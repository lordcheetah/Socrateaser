## Verdict space

The dispute over personal identity has a standard, century-spanning taxonomy. The serious answers to *this* thesis (psychological continuity is necessary and sufficient for personal identity, against biological continuity):

1. **Thesis TRUE (neo-Lockean, kind-relative — Variant B).** Persons are a distinct kind whose persistence is constituted by overlapping psychological connections. P3/P4 hold *for the kind person*; the human animal is a coincident thing with separate (biological) persistence conditions. This is the strongest defensible form of the thesis (the reconstruction names it the version the council should attack). — *thesis TRUE*

2. **FALSE — animalism (necessity-attack).** We are essentially human animals. The early fetus existed before any psychology and did not cease to exist when a mind switched on, so psychological continuity is not *necessary*; and the animal here thinks all my thoughts, so a psychologically-defined person would be a second thinker ("too many thinkers"). Attacks P6 and the necessity of P7. — *thesis FALSE*

3. **FALSE / ill-posed — Parfitian reductionism (sufficiency-attack from inside).** Psychological continuity is real and is what matters in survival, but fission shows it can hold one-to-many, so it cannot constitute *numerical identity* (which is one-to-one and transitive). Identity is not what matters; the "only because identity" framing is the error. Attacks P4 and the framing's assumption that identity is the prize. — *thesis FALSE / partly rejects-framing*

4. **REJECTS-FRAMING — the lived self is not a person-stage between which an identity relation holds.** P1–P2 reify the self into discrete time-slices and then ask what relation re-links them; this third-person stage ontology is an artifact of the puzzle-cases, not a description of how a self is given. The question "what relation makes two stages one person?" mistakes its own object. Attacks P1/P2. — *rejects-framing*

5. **FALSE — substance/soul criterion.** A persisting immaterial self is the identity-bearer; neither psychology nor biology constitutes identity, both merely track it imperfectly. This denies the suppressed premise SP (that only psychology or biology are candidate relations). — *thesis FALSE*

## Fixed-seat assignments

### Naturalist
- **Seat:** naturalist
- **Assigned verdict:** FALSE
- **Premise tied to:** P6 (and the necessity role of P7). Denies that biological-organism continuity fails to constitute identity — it *is* what constitutes our persistence.
- **One-line brief:** You hold the thesis is FALSE because we are essentially human animals: the early fetus that you once were had no psychology yet did not cease to exist when a mind switched on, so psychological continuity is not necessary for your persistence (P6 is false), and the living animal sitting here is already a thinker, so positing a second, psychologically-defined person produces an absurd "too many thinkers" — the organism's biological continuity is the identity-maker.
- **Collapse risk:** none. This seat carries the Olson/van Inwagen animalist line, which is why no standalone Olson specialist is summoned (see notes).

### Rationalist
- **Seat:** rationalist
- **Assigned verdict:** TRUE
- **Premise tied to:** P3/P4, defended in the kind-relative Variant B form. The self is essentially a rational, self-conscious unity individuated by the continuity of its thought, intention, and character.
- **One-line brief:** You hold the thesis is TRUE in its strongest form: a person is essentially a self-conscious rational thing, and what makes a later thinker one and the same as an earlier one is the overlapping continuity of memory, intention, and character (P3) with no biological remainder required (P4) — the human animal is a numerically distinct coincident thing with merely biological persistence conditions, so animalism's "too many thinkers" worry is answered, not refuted.
- **Collapse risk:** none. This is the sole TRUE seat; it must defend the biconditional, not retreat to "psychology usually tracks identity."

### Phenomenologist
- **Seat:** phenomenologist
- **Assigned verdict:** rejects-framing
- **Premise tied to:** P1/P2 — the constitution of identity as a relation between person-stages.
- **One-line brief:** You hold the thesis is ill-posed because P1–P2 already smuggle in the picture they pretend to investigate: they slice the self into discrete time-stages and then hunt for the relation that re-binds them, but the self is not given as a series of slices awaiting reconnection — it is lived as a single ongoing temporal flow with retention and protention built in, so the very question "which relation makes two stages the same person?" misdescribes its object, and *both* the psychological and biological answers inherit that error.
- **Collapse risk:** flag — re-aimed. Natural temptation is to deny the persisting self outright, which would duplicate the contemplative. Re-aimed to attack the *stage-relation framing* (P1/P2), not the persistence of the subject: the phenomenologist affirms a continuous lived self while rejecting the council's third-person apparatus for individuating it.

### Contemplative
- **Seat:** contemplative
- **Assigned verdict:** FALSE
- **Premise tied to:** P7 (the uniqueness/identity-bearer role) — attacked from a *different* premise than the naturalist.
- **One-line brief:** You hold the thesis is FALSE because even granting that something persists, psychological continuity is the worst possible candidate for the identity-maker: memory, intention, and character are precisely the most impermanent, constructed, and clung-to contents of mind — the chain the thesis elevates to identity-bearer is the very flux that contemplative practice reveals as not-self, so P7's claim that *this* relation uniquely constitutes identity inverts the truth, mistaking the most changeable layer for the abiding one.
- **Collapse risk:** flag — re-aimed. Natural contemplative move (anatta / "no self") would either duplicate the phenomenologist's framing-attack or the Parfitian's "identity isn't what matters." Re-aimed to a *premise-specific* attack on P7: it concedes the framing's question and concedes a self may persist, but denies that the psychological chain is what does the persisting — distinct from the naturalist (who names biology) and from the Parfitian (who denies one-to-one identity).

## Specialists to summon

1. **Parfitian reductionist.** Stance: psychological continuity is real and is *what matters*, but fission shows the relation holds one-to-many, so it cannot constitute numerical identity — identity is not what matters, and the thesis's "only because identity" framing is the mistake. Targets **P4** (sufficiency) and the framing's assumption that identity is the prize. Why the fixed four can't voice it: this is an attack from *inside* the psychological tradition the thesis leans on — the rationalist is committed to defending strict identity, the contemplative attacks the wrong-relation rather than the one-to-many structure, and neither will concede "psychology matters but identity doesn't." Essential summon.

2. **Substance/soul theorist (Swinburne-style).** Stance: the identity-bearer is a persisting immaterial self; psychology and biology both merely track identity imperfectly, and no thought-experiment can settle facts about the soul. Targets the suppressed premise **SP** (that only psychology or biology are candidate identity-relations) and P7's eliminative structure. Why the fixed four can't voice it: every fixed seat works within a broadly naturalistic or analytic frame and none defends a non-physical substance as the bearer — this corner of the verdict space (answer 5) is otherwise empty. Summon to keep the eliminative argument honest about SP.

3. *(Not summoned)* **Standalone Olson animalist** — deliberately omitted. The framer suggested it, but the naturalist seat carries the full animalist case (fetus + too-many-thinkers), so a separate Olson specialist would duplicate a fixed seat's assigned move. Folded into the naturalist rather than summoned.
