DISTINCTION: CONCERNS — 7 issues

## Equivocation

- **Term:** "person" — sense in P1/P2/P3: a kind-neutral placeholder for "whatever
  entity has personal identity" (a formal role-filler); sense in the conclusion C
  and in the rationalist/neo-Lockean defense: a substantive natural kind, a
  self-conscious rational subject with psychological-continuity persistence
  conditions; sense in the animalist denial and in the framer's "pseudo-dispute
  check": a phase sortal or functional predicate that applies to an organism for a
  while without fixing the organism's persistence conditions.
  → The argument does not survive clean disambiguation. P1 sets up "person" as a
  formal role-filler ("whatever entity…"), but P3/P4 and the conclusion then treat
  it as the name of a substance-kind with psychological persistence conditions. The
  animalist can accept every premise that uses "person" in the phase-sortal sense
  while denying the conclusion, because the conclusion silently promotes "person"
  from a phase predicate to an essential kind. The framer pre-flagged this as the
  "person-stage identity / strong thesis" slide; the council does not consistently
  resolve it. The rationalist handles it by stipulating Variant B (kind-relative
  reading) but the reconstruction's P1–P2 are written before that stipulation is
  in force, so the reconstruction equivocates where the rationalist merely
  disambiguates.

- **Term:** "identity" — sense in P1, P2, P5, and the conjunction C: strict
  numerical identity, the one-one transitive relation; sense in P3 as the
  Parfitian specialist deploys it (and as it is tacitly used in some council
  passages): "Relation R," the relation of psychological connectedness-and-
  continuity that is what matters in survival and that can hold one-to-many.
  → This is the equivocation the framer called "the Parfitian slide" and it is
  live in the council. The Parfitian specialist correctly isolates it: the
  reconstruction builds the thesis on strict numerical identity (P2) but then
  populates the identity-making role with Relation R (P3), and R can branch while
  numerical identity cannot. The phenomenologist and the contemplative do not
  distinguish the two senses; they treat both as equally suspect and attack what is
  effectively R while speaking of "identity." This does not invalidate their
  objections but it means their arguments hit a somewhat different target than they
  name. Any premise that says psychological continuity "constitutes" identity must
  specify which sense it intends; the reconstruction does not.

- **Term:** "continuity" — sense in P3 and in Parfit-inflected passages: overlapping
  chains of direct psychological connections, admitting of degrees, not requiring
  a single substrate; sense in P6's biological foil: the persistence of one self-
  maintaining metabolic process (a yes/no condition on organism survival); sense as
  the phenomenologist uses it: the primitive retention-protention flow of lived
  time-consciousness, which is neither a chain of connected states nor a biological
  process but a formal structure of experience.
  → Three different things are called "continuity." The argument's sufficiency
  direction compares psychological continuity (degree-admitting, relation-between-
  stages) with biological continuity (organism-level, all-or-nothing), as if these
  are two candidates for the same role. The phenomenologist's "continuity" is not
  a third candidate for that role; it is a phenomenological datum that challenges
  whether the role-description ("a relation between stages") is apt at all. The
  council does not always keep these separate: the naturalist and rationalist treat
  biological and psychological continuity as rivals in the same game; the
  phenomenologist is playing a different game; and the Parfitian further
  distinguishes psychological connectedness (direct links) from psychological
  continuity (overlapping chains of connectedness). These three or four senses
  appear undifferentiated in the conclusion's "linked by continuity of psychology."

- **Term:** "self" — sense in the rationalist (Kantian) passages: the transcendental
  subject, the formal unity of apperception, not an object among others; sense in
  the contemplative passages: the stream of five aggregates, a dependently-arisen
  process with no svabhāva; sense in the phenomenologist: the temporal flow of
  retention-protention, Merleau-Ponty's "time is someone"; sense in the thesis
  proper: the persisting entity whose persistence conditions are at issue.
  → Each council seat uses "self" in a sense appropriate to its tradition. The
  rationalist's Kantian self (transcendental condition) cannot be identified with
  the thesis's persisting entity (empirical subject with numerical identity), but
  the rationalist moves between them: "the unity of apperception" grounds
  "psychological persistence conditions" via a bridge ("so whatever thinks must have
  psychological persistence conditions") that is not argued — it is asserted as a
  consequence of Shoemaker's functionalism, which is a different theoretical
  framework than the Kantian transcendental argument. Using Kant to establish that
  the self is a rational-psychological unity (phenomenological/transcendental) and
  then treating that as a result in metaphysics of persistence (numerical identity
  conditions) is a category crossing within a single council draft.


## Category bleed

- **Crossing:** The rationalist's transcendental argument (Kant, B131) — "the *I
  think* must be able to accompany all my representations" — is established as a
  phenomenological/transcendental claim (a condition on the possibility of unified
  experience) and then used as a metaphysical claim about numerical persistence
  conditions (what it takes for the self to be one and the same thing over time).
  → The standard of evidence it was given: transcendental reflection on the
  structure of experience — what must be true for experience to be possible at all.
  The standard it now needs: a metaphysical argument about persistence conditions
  for a concrete entity, which requires showing that the transcendental unity of
  apperception picks out the same thing as the "person" whose identity is in
  dispute, and that its conditions are numerically individuating. These are not the
  same requirement. Kant explicitly denies that the "I think" is a substance or a
  persisting object (the Paralogisms critique); the rationalist's move from
  "apperceptive unity" to "psychological persistence conditions for numerical
  identity" is precisely the inference Kant blocks. The council draft does not
  bridge this; it proceeds as if "the self is a rational-psychological unity"
  (transcendental) entails "the self persists by psychological continuity"
  (metaphysical).

- **Crossing:** The phenomenologist's Husserlian analysis of retention-protention —
  the stream has "an absolute flowing identity in a manifold" — is established as a
  phenomenological claim (how temporal experience is given, from the first-person
  standpoint) and then deployed to reject P1–P2 as false metaphysics.
  → The standard of evidence it was given: description of how the lived self shows
  up in experience; phenomenological analysis of the structure of time-consciousness.
  The standard it now needs: to establish that the metaphysical framework of stages-
  and-relations is not merely phenomenologically unmotivated but actually false as
  an account of what persists. The phenomenological result is that the self is not
  *experienced* as stages bound by a relation. This does not by itself establish
  that stages-and-relations is wrong as a metaphysical account — metaphysical
  persistence conditions need not map onto phenomenological appearance. The
  phenomenologist does not bridge this; the move from "not given as stages" to
  "there are no stages" is an unargued crossing from phenomenological to
  metaphysical claim. (This does not refute the phenomenologist's point, which may
  be correct, but the crossing needs an explicit bridge — e.g. a claim that
  phenomenological structure is evidentially relevant to metaphysical structure, or
  a methodological premise about priority.)

- **Crossing:** Several council seats (especially the Parfitian and, partially, the
  contemplative) move between two normative/practical claims and the metaphysical
  thesis. Parfit's "identity is not what matters" is an argument that the practical
  stakes attach to Relation R, not to numerical identity — this is a normative/
  practical claim (what we have reason to care about, what prudential concern
  tracks). But it is regularly deployed in the council as if it settles the
  metaphysical question (whether psychological continuity constitutes strict
  numerical identity). The Parfitian specialist is careful to distinguish these
  ("my objection is to the form the thesis wraps around that truth") but the
  conflation recurs in the reconstruction itself: Variant A ("psychological
  continuity is the relation that matters for prudential concern") is described as
  "a genuine variant, not a rescue of the same thesis" — which is exactly right —
  but then treated as if Parfit's practical conclusion somehow bears on the
  metaphysical thesis's truth-value. The practical finding that R is what matters
  leaves the metaphysical question (what constitutes identity) entirely open; it
  does not refute the strong thesis, it changes the subject.
  → Standard given: practical/normative argument from the fission case about what
  survives that is worth caring about. Standard needed: metaphysical argument about
  constitution. The fission case is a genuine pressure on the metaphysical thesis
  (it threatens sufficiency), but the "identity is not what matters" conclusion is
  a normative output, not the metaphysical argument itself.

- **Crossing (minor):** The contemplative's Assutavā Sutta comparison ("the body
  endures longer than the mind") is established as an empirical-phenomenological
  ranking of temporal endurance and then used to make a metaphysical point about
  which is the better identity-bearer. Durational stability (empirical/
  phenomenological) and suitability as a metaphysical identity-criterion are
  different things: a thing can be highly stable yet not the right locus of identity
  (e.g. a person's skeleton). The contemplative is clear that neither criterion
  wins the metaphysical role, so this crossing does not infect the verdict, but the
  argument from "endures longer" to "is the better metaphysical candidate" within
  the P7-attack segment is not bridged.


## Clean joints

- **"Psychological continuity" vs. "psychological connectedness":** The Parfitian
  specialist correctly maintains the Parfit distinction (connectedness = direct
  overlapping links; continuity = sufficiently overlapping chains of connectedness).
  The reconstruction's P3 uses "overlapping chains of direct psychological
  connections" in a way that conflates the two but does so consistently; no
  equivocation within the reconstruction itself, and the Parfitian flag is an
  external one about Parfit's technical vocabulary, not a within-document shift.

- **"Because, and only because":** Verified stable throughout. Every council seat
  reads this as asserting a strict biconditional (both necessary and sufficient)
  and attacks it as such. No one silently weakens it to a ceteris-paribus claim
  without flagging that the thesis would thereby change. The load-bearing modal
  commitment is consistently recognized.

- **P6 (biological continuity not sufficient):** All seats that address P6 use
  "biological-organism continuity" in the same sense (persistence of the self-
  maintaining metabolic process as a whole, not mere brain-persistence). The
  brain-criterion / somatic-vs-cerebral split noted in the framing does not appear
  in the council; no equivocation detected here.

- **"Numerical identity":** Where the term appears in the reconstruction and in the
  rationalist and Parfitian drafts, it consistently means the strict one-one
  transitive relation — not qualitative similarity, not "what matters," not
  continuity of kind. The two-sense problem with "identity" (flagged above as
  equivocation) is a cross-draft issue; within any single draft the term is used
  consistently once that draft chooses its reading.

- **"Person-stage":** Used in a consistent technical sense in the reconstruction
  (temporal part or time-slice of a person for purposes of stating the identity
  relation). The phenomenologist's objection is to the concept's legitimacy, not
  to its sense — a clean philosophical dispute about the framework, not an
  equivocation within it.

- **"Persistence conditions":** Stable in the metaphysical direction throughout all
  six drafts — referring to the conditions that must obtain for an entity of some
  kind to survive from one time to another. No draft uses this in a merely epistemic
  or evidential sense (what tells us whether someone survived) except the soul
  theorist, who explicitly distinguishes "evidence of identity" from "constitution
  of identity" and labels the distinction clearly. That explicit separation is a
  clean joint, not a confusion.
