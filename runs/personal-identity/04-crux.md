# Personal Identity Over Time — Crux map
> **Run:** personal-identity · **Mode:** light · **Stage:** Synthesis
> **Thesis:** A person persists over time because, and only because, of psychological continuity (overlapping memory, intention, character) — not biological continuity.

---

## The crux

All six positions agree that biological-organism continuity does not *constitute*
personal identity (P6) and that psychological continuity is, at minimum, deeply
involved in what we care about — and then they fork on **one question: is strict
numerical identity (a one-one, transitive, all-or-nothing, fully determinate
relation) the thing that personal persistence consists in at all?** Everything
downstream — whether fission refutes the thesis, whether the fetus refutes it,
whether a soul or a lived flow does the work psychology can't — follows from the
answer to that single question, because the thesis stakes its whole content on
the words "because, and *only because* [identity]."

There is a second, partly-verbal fork nested inside the first — **are we
essentially persons (psychologically individuated) or essentially human animals
(biologically individuated)?** — but the distinction-watchdog's analysis shows
this one is downstream: it is genuine only once you have already answered the
identity question in a way that licenses two coincident things. The real,
non-verbal residue collapses into the first fork.

## Position map

- **Rationalist (TRUE, kind-relative Variant B):** Verdict — psychology is
  necessary and sufficient for the strict numerical identity *of persons*, with the
  human animal a distinct coincident thing. Forcing commitment: **persons are an
  essential kind whose identity conditions follow a priori from the concept of a
  self-conscious rational unity** (Locke's "consider itself as itself," Kant's
  apperceptive unity, Shoemaker's functionalism). To change its mind it would have
  to give up that there are two coincident things here — the person and the animal —
  i.e., abandon coincidence ontology and concede the animalist's "one thinker."
  *(Attribution/distinction note: the move from Kant's B131 to a diachronic
  persistence condition is an interpretive extension Kant's Paralogisms arguably
  block — the watchdog flags this as a transcendental→metaphysical category
  crossing, and the attribution-checker marks the inference, not the quotation, as
  contested. The Kantian leg of this seat's authority is softened; the Lockean and
  Shoemaker legs stand clean.)*

- **Naturalist / animalism (FALSE):** Verdict — psychology is neither necessary
  (the early fetus persisted without it) nor sufficient (PVS, fission), because we
  *are* the human animal and its persistence is biological. Forcing commitment:
  **there is exactly one thinking thing in the chair, the organism, and no causal
  or ontological slot for a second "person" object** (van Inwagen's organicism:
  only lives compose). To change its mind it would have to admit a coincident
  non-organism thinker — the very thing it deflates as an artifact of first-person
  access. Note: this seat *agrees* identity is strict numerical identity; it just
  says biology, not psychology, fills the role.

- **Contemplative (FALSE, attacking P7):** Verdict — even granting that something
  persists and that there is a unique identity-maker, psychology is the *worst*
  candidate, since memory/intention/character are the most impermanent, most
  clung-to aggregates. Forcing commitment: **the more volatile a content, the less
  fit it is to bear identity, and the eliminative step "not biology, therefore
  psychology" is both invalid and substantively backwards** (Assutavā Sutta's
  ranking). To change its mind it would have to hold that impermanence is no bar to
  being an identity-bearer. *(Distinction note: the seat's own deeper view denies
  the role is occupied at all — the "two truths" move — but its assigned, committed
  attack concedes the framing and loses psychology on the merits inside it. The
  watchdog flags a minor empirical-endurance→metaphysical-suitability crossing that
  does not infect the verdict.)*

- **Phenomenologist (REJECTS-FRAMING, attacking P1/P2):** Verdict — the thesis is
  ill-posed: it slices the self into person-stages and hunts for a re-binding
  relation, but the lived self is given as one retention-protention flow whose
  holding-together is prior to any relation between parts. Forcing commitment:
  **the stage-and-relation ontology is a theoretical re-coding that destroys the
  phenomenon it claims to describe; both psychology and biology are rival glues for
  a vase that was never broken.** To change its mind it would have to grant that
  metaphysical persistence conditions can legitimately diverge from how the self is
  given in experience. *(Distinction note: the watchdog flags the move from "not
  given as stages" to "there are no stages" as an unbridged phenomenological→
  metaphysical crossing — the objection may be right but needs an explicit
  priority premise it does not supply.)*

- **Parfitian reductionist (FALSE / rejects-framing, attacking P4 + "only because
  identity"):** Verdict — psychology (Relation R) is exactly what matters in
  survival, but fission shows R can hold one-to-many while identity is one-one, so
  R *cannot be* identity; "personal identity is not what matters." Forcing
  commitment: **there is no further deep fact of identity over and above the
  particular continuities, so in branching cases the identity question is empty.**
  To change its mind it would have to reinstate a determinate one-one further fact
  that fission leaves undefined. This is the seat that names the crux most
  precisely: it accepts the thesis's content and rejects its *form*.
  *(Attribution note: the precise Reasons and Persons section numbers are
  UNVERIFIABLE from open sources — substance confirmed, "double success" quote
  confirmed at p. 256; precision softened, position not in doubt.)*

- **Soul theorist / Swinburne (FALSE, denying SP):** Verdict — neither psychology
  nor biology *constitutes* identity; both are mere evidence. The duplication
  argument that sinks the body sinks the memory-chain too, leaving only a
  non-duplicable immaterial substance. Forcing commitment: **identity is a further,
  unanalysable fact about a particular indivisible soul, epistemically inaccessible
  precisely because it is not constituted by the evidence.** To change its mind it
  would have to accept that "we cannot tell who survived" means "there is no fact
  about who survived" — the reductionist reading it explicitly refuses. This seat
  exists to attack the suppressed premise SP (no third-kind criterion is a
  candidate), and it does so directly.

## Convergence

The shared ground is wider than the dispute first suggests:

1. **Everyone rejects the bare bodily criterion.** Naturalist, rationalist,
   Parfitian, and soul theorist all affirm P6 in some form: the persistence of the
   metabolic process *as such* does not constitute personal identity. (The
   naturalist relocates the work to the organism's *life* rather than mere body;
   the others deny biology any constitutive role at all.) The thesis's negative
   half — "not biology" — is nearly unanimous. The fight is entirely over the
   positive half.

2. **Psychological continuity is conceded to be doing *something* real by almost
   everyone.** The rationalist makes it constitutive; the Parfitian makes it what
   matters; the soul theorist makes it evidence; even the contemplative grants it
   "does real work" conventionally. Only the phenomenologist declines to grant it a
   role, and only because it declines the whole role-structure.

3. **Three of six accept the strict-identity framing and fight inside it**
   (rationalist: psychology fills it; naturalist: biology fills it; soul theorist:
   a soul fills it). Three challenge the framing itself (Parfitian: identity isn't
   what matters; phenomenologist: no stages to relate; contemplative's deeper view:
   the role is unoccupied). This 3-3 split *is* the crux made visible.

## What the functional roles changed

- **Self-application (N/A):** Correctly inert here. The thesis is a first-order
  metaphysical criterion, not a claim ranging over all claims, so no
  self-refutation loop exists. The one residue the tester recorded — that the
  theorist's memory-based access to their own reasoning is not fully independent of
  what the thesis says memory constitutes — is an epistemic-grounding note, not a
  logical defeater, and it does not move the crux. Noted, not forced.

- **Distinction-watchdog (CONCERNS, 7 issues) — this reshapes the map the most.**
  It resolves the "person vs. human animal" question: the equivocation is *real in
  the reconstruction* (P1 sets "person" as a formal role-filler; the conclusion
  silently promotes it to an essential kind), which means the naive thesis does not
  survive disambiguation — the animalist can accept every premise on the
  phase-sortal reading and still deny the conclusion. But once the rationalist
  *stipulates* Variant B, the verbal layer is gone and a genuine metaphysical
  disagreement remains (coincidence ontology vs. one-thing organicism). **So the
  person/animal fork is partly verbal at the level of the unreconstructed thesis
  and fully genuine at the level of Variant B — and even genuine, it is downstream
  of the identity-framing fork.** On the Parfitian slide, the watchdog confirms the
  reconstruction itself equivocates on "identity": P2 is built on strict numerical
  identity, P3 populates the role with Relation R, and R can branch while identity
  cannot. **This is not a fixable wording slip; it is the crux. The thesis's "only
  because identity" clause is load-bearing and the watchdog certifies that the term
  it rests on is used in two incompatible senses across the very premises that
  state it.** The watchdog also flags that the phenomenologist and contemplative
  attack "R" while naming "identity," so their objections land slightly off their
  stated target — true, but it sharpens rather than dissolves the map.

- **Attribution-checker (CONCERNS, 3 issues):** Mostly clean — 15 of 18 claims
  ACCURATE against fetched sources. Three softenings, none fatal: (1) Olson's
  primary PDF was unreadable, so the animalist's textual authority rests on strong
  secondary attestation (position not in doubt); (2) the Parfitian's *Reasons and
  Persons* section numbers are UNVERIFIABLE though their content is confirmed — so
  the Parfitian's *precision* is softened, its *substance* stands; (3) the
  rationalist's use of Kant B131 as a diachronic persistence condition is an
  interpretive extension the text does not state and the Paralogisms arguably
  forbid. **Effect on the map:** the rationalist (the sole TRUE seat) loses one of
  its three supporting legs to the Kant flag, leaning its weight onto Locke and
  Shoemaker; this does not refute the seat but means its strongest *transcendental*
  argument is contested at the source.

## What it would take to settle it

This **bottoms out in a conceptual decision, not an empirical fact and not a
compelling argument.** Every position agrees on every physical fact about fetuses,
brains, and fission cases; what they disagree about is *what relation gets to count
as "what we are/what persists,"* and whether the prize being contested (strict,
determinate, one-one identity) is even the right prize. The decisive move is the
treatment of the fission case, and fission does not yield a forced verdict — it
yields a **trilemma** that each position resolves by paying a different price it
was independently willing to pay:

- Keep sufficiency, give up one-one identity → "identity isn't what matters"
  (Parfitian).
- Keep one-one identity, give up sufficiency → biology decides which animal is
  which (naturalist).
- Keep both, relocate the bearer to something non-duplicable → the soul (Swinburne).
- Deny the case is well-formed → the flow was never sliced (phenomenologist).

No fact and no argument in the dispute can compel the choice among these, because
the choice *is* the choice of what "personal identity" is going to mean. This is a
**standing disagreement that bottoms out in whether you grant that strict numerical
identity is the thing to be explained.** Grant it, and the fight is the genuine
(settleable-in-principle, fact-constrained) one between rationalist, naturalist,
and soul theorist. Decline it, and the thesis was malformed from P2. Nothing in the
council can force the grant.

## For the author

The author is a pragmatist, and the pragmatist sits in a specific, exposed place on
this map: **closest to the Parfitian seat, and nowhere near the thesis as stated.**
A pragmatist's instinct is that "what we are the same person *because of*" should be
cashed out by what the identity-talk is *for* — accountability, anticipation,
prudential concern, promise-keeping — which is precisely Relation R, what-matters,
Variant A. That instinct is congenial to the thesis's *spirit* (psychology, not
biology, is what we care about) but lethal to its *letter* (the "only because
*identity*" clause), because the pragmatic payoff attaches to R, and R can branch.
The watchdog's verdict applies to the author directly: the practical finding "R is
what matters" leaves the metaphysical question open; it does not vindicate the
strong thesis, it *changes the subject*.

So the single premise the pragmatist author would have to defend to keep the thesis
**as written** is the one the pragmatist is least equipped to defend: that strict
numerical identity — a determinate, one-one, all-or-nothing further fact — is the
genuine explanandum, and that psychology constitutes *it* rather than merely
servicing the practical purposes identity-talk exists for. A pragmatist who takes
that on has stopped being a pragmatist about identity. The honest pragmatist move
is to retreat to Variant A and concede, with the Parfitian, that the thesis is true
in spirit and false in form — which is a real result, not a defeat, but it is not
the thesis the author walked in with.
