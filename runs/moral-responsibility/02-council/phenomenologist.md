# Basic-Desert Moral Responsibility — Phenomenologist (council)
> **Run:** moral-responsibility · **Mode:** full · **Stage:** Council
> **Thesis:** No one is ever morally responsible in the basic-desert sense, because basic desert requires being the ultimate source of one's action, and no one is ever the ultimate source of what they do.
> **Seat:** Phenomenologist — continental/phenomenological, Husserl–Merleau-Ponty
> **Defending:** VERDICT: The thesis is FALSE because P1 is false — desert is not a debt that ultimate sourcehood pays off but a fittingness given inside the lived second-personal stance of resentment, gratitude, and address; the "ultimate source" requirement is a theory imposed on a phenomenon that never asked for it.

---

VERDICT: The thesis is FALSE because P1 is false — desert is not a debt that ultimate sourcehood pays off but a fittingness given inside the lived second-personal stance of resentment, gratitude, and address; the "ultimate source" requirement is a theory imposed on a phenomenon that never asked for it.

## Case

Start where the dispute actually lives — not with a regress diagram but with the moment it is meant to be about. Someone, out of plain contempt, breaks a promise to you and watches it land. You feel the heat of resentment. Now describe that feeling honestly, before any theory touches it. What is it a response *to*? It is not a response to a verdict you have reached about whether this person, traced back through their genes and upbringing and the Big Bang, was the buck-stopping originator of their contempt. You did not run that inquiry and you did not need to. You are responding to the contempt itself — to the **quality of will** the person showed you, to the regard or disregard for you that their action expressed.

This is Strawson's central observation in "Freedom and Resentment," and it is a *descriptive* claim about the phenomenon, not a metaphysical concession. The reactive attitudes — resentment, gratitude, indignation, hurt feelings, forgiveness — are responses to "the quality of others' wills towards us, as manifested in their behaviour: to their good or ill will or indifference or lack of concern." Resentment is the felt registering of *ill will addressed to me*; gratitude is the felt registering of *good will addressed to me*. Strawson's diagnosis of the free-will dispute is that both sides "over-intellectualize the facts" — the consequentialist by reducing the practice to calculated deterrence, the libertarian by demanding a contra-causal metaphysical license the practice never rested on. The demand for ultimate sourcehood is precisely such an over-intellectualization: a theoretical artifact read back into a structure that was complete without it.

So here is the phenomenological case against P1, in three steps.

**First: desert shows up *within* the reactive stance, not behind it.** When you resent the contemptuous promise-breaker, the sense that he *has it coming* — that the resentment is *fitting*, owed, called for — is not a separate judgment you add after consulting the metaphysics. It is internal to the attitude itself. To resent is already to take the other as someone to whom the resentment is *fittingly addressed*. Desert is not the hidden premise that licenses the reaction from outside; it is the shape the reaction already has from inside. This is the route I am defending, and it is stronger than the rationalist's: I am not arguing that desert needs a *weaker* sourcehood that survives conceptual analysis. I am arguing it needs *no* sourcehood at all, because the work P1 assigns to sourcehood — explaining why the attitude is fitting — is already done by the second-personal structure of the attitude itself.

**Second: the structure is second-personal — *responsible-for* is grounded in *responsible-to*.** Resentment and gratitude are not first-person verdicts about a third-person object; they are second-person *address*. Darwall makes this precise: the moral sense of "responsible for" is conceptually tied to "responsible *to*," and to hold someone responsible is to address a claim to them as a fellow participant who can be expected to acknowledge it (*The Second-Person Standpoint*, ch. IV). This is the standpoint Strawson calls the **participant** stance, opposed to the **objective** stance in which we treat a person as something to be managed, cured, or handled rather than addressed. Crucially, you cannot derive the participant stance from the objective one — you cannot get the *you* of address out of a third-person inventory of causes. The lived structure of "I resent *what you did to me*" has a you-and-I in it that the sourcehood question, posed entirely in the third person ("is this object the ultimate cause of that event?"), has already abolished. The phenomenon Levinas tracks under the face of the Other — the way another person confronts me as a claim and not a thing — is the same structure: address precedes and grounds the question of causal origination, it does not wait on its answer.

**Third: therefore P1 inverts the order of grounding.** P1 says: *first* the agent must be an ultimate source, and *then*, in virtue of that, blame can be deserved. But the phenomenon runs the other way. We find ourselves already inside the participant stance, already addressing and being addressed, already finding resentment fitting or unfitting — and the concept of desert is *abstracted from* that lived fittingness, not the reverse. Husserl's term for what P1 commits is the *Galilean* substitution: mistaking the idealized theoretical construct (the buck-stopping originator) for the concrete *Lebenswelt* — the lived world of interpersonal address — that the construct was supposed to be *about* (*Crisis of the European Sciences*, §9). P1 takes a model built to answer a cosmological question and treats it as the precondition of a practice that was never answering that question. That is the false picture. Desert is not found *behind* the reactive attitudes in the metaphysics of causation, and it is not merely *projected* by us onto an indifferent world either — that "objective backstop or mere projection" dichotomy is itself the Cartesian framing I reject. Desert is *constituted* in the engaged, second-personal structure of holding-to-account, which is neither a discovery about mind-independent causal facts nor a fiction we invent, but the form our being-with-others actually takes.

Note what this does *not* claim. It does not claim the manipulation and luck arguments are *false* — P2 may well be true; no one may be a regress-ending originator. The phenomenological move is to deny that this *matters* to desert, because desert never lived in that regress. When Strawson asks whether the truth of determinism could give us reason to abandon the reactive attitudes wholesale, his answer is that the question is idle: the commitment to the participant stance is not a *belief* (in contra-causal freedom) that evidence could overturn but the standing framework within which anything like a reason to blame or thank can show up at all. You cannot get an *external* warrant for the practice of address, because there is no place outside all address to stand and demand one. P1 is the demand for exactly that external warrant. It is unmeetable not because we fall short of it but because it was never a real condition on the thing it claims to condition.

## What the thesis presupposes

P1 presupposes that desert is a *debt that must be underwritten* — that the fittingness of blame is a downstream entitlement which only some upstream metaphysical fact (ultimate sourcehood) could pay for, so that if the upstream fact is missing, the entitlement is counterfeit. This is a Cartesian-cum-Galilean picture: it splits the scene into a third-person object whose causal pedigree is the real question, and a subject who is owed a *theoretical license* before the reactive attitudes are anything more than projection. Both the skeptic who defends the thesis and the libertarian who attacks P2 share this presupposition — they only disagree about whether the license can be issued. The phenomenological objection is upstream of both: the practice of holding-to-account is a *primary* mode of being-with-others, complete in its own second-personal terms, and "ultimate sourcehood" is a construct abstracted from a quite different (cosmological) inquiry and then smuggled back in as a precondition. Desert is constituted in the lived address between persons. It needs no source behind it because there is no gap behind it to fill.

## Attack round

**The shared, unexamined assumption (naturalist, manipulation specialist, libertarian).** All three play on one board: that desert is a *standing* an agent has only if some upstream metaphysical fact — buck-stopping sourcehood — *obtains* and underwrites it. They differ only on the score. The naturalist says the source-node is nowhere in the causal story ("produce the causal story; there is no such node"). The manipulation specialist says Plum lacks desert because his action "traces back, without remainder, to factors he did not produce," and the *only* feature that could have flipped the verdict is sourcehood. The libertarian agrees outright — "P1 is true... reasons-responsive ownership the agent did not ultimately author is *not* enough" — and merely insists a self-forming action supplies the missing node. That is one premise wearing three hats: *desert is a downstream entitlement awaiting an upstream warrant.* But look at the phenomenon. The four-case argument works by *manipulating your resentment* — it asks you to feel that blame stops being fitting once you learn the etiology. That is a datum *about the participant stance*, not about a causal ledger. The skeptic's own engine runs on the reactive attitudes he claims desert can do without. None of the three ever asks whether the practice of address required the warrant in the first place; they only count the change in a pocket the practice never carried.

**The naturalist** demands I locate "the contribution that is the agent's own and inherited from nothing prior." There is no such node — granted. But that is a verdict reached from the *objective* stance, the third-person inventory of causes. You cannot derive the *you* of "what you did to me" from any complete list of antecedent events, however long. Asking the practice of address to surface a node in that list is a category error: you are looking for the second person in a register that contains only third-person objects. The absence you triumphantly report is the absence the question manufactured by posing itself in the wrong dimension.

**The manipulation specialist** has built the most precise instrument against himself. The cases move only because, at each step, your *resentment of Plum* shifts — the argument is parasitic on the very fittingness it concludes is groundless. But the manipulation cases also genuinely track something real: we *do* withdraw the reactive attitudes from the manipulated. Phenomenology explains why without sourcehood — the device makes Plum's will *not his to answer for*, severs the address, turns him into something handled rather than addressed (Strawson's shift to the objective stance). That is a relational fact about whether *you-and-I* obtains, not about whether a cosmic node does. Case 4 differs from Case 1 exactly here: ordinary determinism does not abolish the second person; the neuroscientist does.

**The libertarian** concedes P1 most eagerly and so reveals the shared premise in its purest form: he agrees desert needs a buck-stopping originator and only labors to manufacture one (the self-forming action, the agent-as-substance-cause). But notice — he must build a metaphysical engine of "dual efforts" and "substance causation" to underwrite a fittingness you already feel toward the ordinary promise-breaker who never performed a Kanean torn decision in his life. The phenomenon of fitting resentment is *complete* before any of that machinery is installed. The libertarian is paying an enormous ontological bill for a warrant the practice was never invoiced for.

**The rationalist** is my near-ally and my sharpest rival, because he too denies P1 — but he relocates desert in "rational ownership of a reasons-responsive mechanism," a *normative achievement* the agent performs by adopting a self-conception "appropriately based on the evidence." That is still a theoretical overlay on the lived stance. When I resent the contemptuous promise-breaker, I am not crediting him with having rationally endorsed a mechanism; I am registering ill will *addressed to me*. Darwall's point cuts against Fischer-Ravizza here: "responsible-for" is grounded in "responsible-*to*," and the second person of address is *prior to* any reflective self-relation the agent performs. Korsgaard's self-constituting agent is a first-person rational legislator; the reactive attitudes are *second*-personal and need no such legislation to be fitting. The rationalist has the right verdict (P1 false) by building a more defensible mechanism behind the agent — but the mechanism is one more thing *behind*, when the phenomenon was already complete *between*.

**The contemplative** is an ally on the decisive move: the demand for an own-being desert-bearer "considered full-stop, independent of every relation" is exactly the *svabhāva* the lived stance never required, and her "conventionally, accountability works fine" is my "the participant stance is complete in its own terms." But she over-theorizes the dissolution by routing it through a no-self *metaphysics* — anātman, the analysis of the chariot, the claim that the bearer is "constructed." That is a metaphysical thesis about what there ultimately is, and it answers the cosmological question on the skeptic's own terms (there is no self-existent owner) rather than refusing it. I need no doctrine that the self is empty. I need only the descriptive fact that resentment addresses a *you*, and that this *you* is given in the relation, not posited behind it. She dissolves the bearer; I decline to look behind the address for one. Hers is a stronger metaphysical claim than the phenomenon requires — and so it inherits debts (the two-truths apparatus, the textual defense of niḥsvabhāva) that pure description does not.
