# Basic-Desert Moral Responsibility — Naturalist (council)
> **Run:** moral-responsibility · **Mode:** full · **Stage:** Council
> **Thesis:** No one is ever morally responsible in the basic-desert sense, because basic desert requires being the ultimate source of one's action, and no one is ever the ultimate source of what they do.
> **Seat:** Naturalist — hard naturalist, Quine–Dennett register
> **Defending:** VERDICT: The thesis is TRUE — no one is ever morally responsible in the basic-desert sense, because the natural causal order in which every action is fixed by genes, upbringing, prior character, and chance leaves no room for a buck-stopping originator, and an indeterministic gap supplies only noise, never authorship.

---

VERDICT: The thesis is TRUE — no one is ever morally responsible in the basic-desert sense, because the natural causal order in which every action is fixed by genes, upbringing, prior character, and chance leaves no room for a buck-stopping originator, and an indeterministic gap supplies only noise, never authorship.

## Case

I defend P2 (in its sharpened form P2′): no agent is ever the buck-stopping originator of any action, because every action's explanation bottoms out in factors the agent did not produce. I do this as a naturalist, not as a libertarian who hopes to break the regress with agent-causation — that move is closed to me on the very grounds I work from. There is one world, the natural causal one, and inquiry into it is continuous with the sciences. The buck of explanation does not stop inside a person; it runs straight through them, out into their constitution and history, exactly as it runs through every other system the sciences describe. The demand for an "ultimate source" is a demand for an exemption from that order, and there is no exemption.

**The regress is not a logical trick; it is what a causal explanation of a person looks like.** Galen Strawson's Basic Argument states the structure plainly. To be truly responsible for what you intentionally do, you must be responsible for the way you are, "for what you intentionally do flows necessarily from how you are." To be responsible for how you are, "you must have intentionally brought it about that you are the way you are, and this is impossible" — because to have done so you must already have existed "with a prior nature in the light of which you intentionally brought it about" that you became as you are, launching a regress that cannot be completed (Strawson, *The Impossibility of Moral Responsibility*, premises as reconstructed; cf. the short form: nothing can be *causa sui*, true responsibility requires being *causa sui* in crucial mental respects, therefore nothing is truly responsible). Strawson's flat observation is the naturalist's observation: "It is undeniable that one is the way one is as a result of one's heredity and experience," and these are "things for which one cannot be held to be in any way responsible." That is not philosophy imposed on science; it is science read honestly. A developmental biologist, a behavioral geneticist, and a learning theorist together write the explanation of why this agent has these reasons and weights them this way — and not one line of that explanation is authored by the agent.

**This is the standing demand of my register: produce the causal story.** When someone claims an agent is the ultimate source, I ask where, in the causal chain, the contribution that is the agent's own and inherited from nothing prior is located. There is no such node. Every candidate — the agent's values, their deliberative style, the very capacity to reflect and revise — is itself a product of prior causes the agent did not lay down. A faculty of self-origination that no causal account can locate is a placeholder for ignorance, not a discovery. The libertarian who posits agent-causation to fill the gap has named the gap, not closed it; he owes the same causal story and cannot give it.

**Indeterminism adds noise, not authorship.** The one escape that does not appeal to agent-causation is to put an indeterministic gap in the chain and hope authorship lives there. It cannot. Strawson again: "it is absurd to suppose that indeterministic or random factors, for which one is in no way responsible, can in themselves contribute to one's being truly or ultimately responsible for how one is." Pereboom presses the same point against event-causal libertarianism: with an undetermined decision, the agent is "left unable to *settle* whether a decision/action occurs and hence cannot have the control in action required for moral responsibility" (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, "Skepticism About Moral Responsibility"). A quantum coin-flip in the synapse does not make the outcome more *yours*; it makes it less yours. Determinism gives you an action wholly owed to prior causes; indeterminism gives you an action partly owed to chance. Neither gives you an action owed to a self that authored itself. The dilemma is exhaustive because the causal order is exhaustive — that is the naturalist's whole point.

**The luck pincer closes the universal claim.** Pereboom's manipulation argument and Neil Levy's "luck pincer" convert the regress into something even a determinism-agnostic can see. Levy: every morally significant act is subject to *constitutive luck* (the agent's uncontrolled endowment) or *present luck* (circumstantial factors at the moment of decision), or both, and "either way, luck undermines moral responsibility since it undermines responsibility-level control" (SEP, "Skepticism About Moral Responsibility," citing Levy 2011). Caruso makes the naturalist reading explicit: we are entirely the products of our genes and environment, "we didn't choose our genes (constitutive luck) and we are never in control of our environments (present luck), so luck swallows all" (reporting Caruso's argument in *Just Deserts*). Pereboom's four cases run a manipulated agent and an ordinarily-determined agent onto a single continuum: if we excuse the agent whose every input was arranged by a neuroscientist, and there is no principled responsibility-relevant difference between that agent and one whose inputs were arranged by blind nature, then we must excuse both. Nature is the manipulator with no hands.

**Basic desert is precisely the notion that cannot survive this.** Pereboom's sense — the act is the agent's "in such a way that she would deserve to be blamed if she understood that it was morally wrong," with the desert attaching to the act *itself*, independent of any consequence (Pereboom 2014, via SEP) — is exactly the notion that asks the agent to be the buck-stopping source. Strip out deterrence, reform, repair, and the fittingness of reactive attitudes (all conceded by this thesis), and what is left is the bare claim that the agent, *as ultimate origin*, has earned suffering or reward for its own sake. That residue requires the one thing the causal order never delivers. So the conclusion is not a paradox to be explained away; it is what falls out once you stop exempting persons from the world.

## What I deny and why

I deny the move my fellow naturalist Dennett makes, and I deny it on naturalist grounds. Dennett (*Elbow Room: The Varieties of Free Will Worth Wanting*) grants the causal order completely — he is no dualist, no agent-causationist — and then argues that the free will "worth wanting," the kind that grounds responsibility, never required ultimate origination in the first place; reasons-responsiveness, self-control, and the capacity to have done otherwise *in the relevant pragmatic sense* are all the responsibility we ever needed, and all of it is fully natural. This is the compatibilist attack on P1 (the conceptual premise), and it is the most dangerous objection to me precisely because it shares my metaphysics.

Here is why it does not touch the thesis. Dennett changes the subject. The thesis has already conceded him everything he describes — forward-looking responsibility, attributability, the usefulness of holding-responsible practices. What it denies is *basic* desert: the claim that the agent intrinsically merits blame or praise *for its own sake*, in a world where it does no good. Dennett's account brilliantly underwrites the responsibility worth wanting; it says nothing about the responsibility the thesis is about, because the desert he can naturalize is desert grounded in consequences and capacities, which is exactly the desert the thesis already grants. When Dennett says we "deserve" praise and blame, the deflationary naturalist should ask his own question right back at him: what work is the word "deserve" doing here that "it is useful and fitting to treat them this way" cannot do? If the answer is "none," then he has conceded basic desert and kept the word. If the answer is "intrinsic, consequence-independent merit," then he has smuggled in the very buck-stopping notion his own naturalism forbids. Either way the thesis stands: the only desert a causal world can support is the forward-looking, practice-embedded kind, and that is not basic desert.

So I do not need P1 to be controversial — I take P1 as the analysis of what "deserve... for its own sake" means, and I rest my defense on P2. The compatibilist and I agree on P2 (we are not ultimate sources). Our disagreement is verbal-adjacent: he keeps the honorific "responsible" for the natural capacities that remain, I reserve "basic desert" for the thing those capacities cannot deliver. On the pinned sense of basic desert, P2 settles it.

## Attack round

**Against the libertarian (Kane/O'Connor).** O'Connor's "substance-cause" is the spooky uncaused-causer naturalism rejects, dressed as a "basic ontological category." Calling agent-causation a category does not exempt it from my standing demand: produce the causal story. Why this decision rather than that one, *at this instant*? If the agent-substance fully determines it, the explanation runs back through the agent's prior states (regress reopens); if it does not, the residue is undetermined — chance — and "the agent caused it" is a label pasted over the gap, not a mechanism filling it. Kane is worse off: locating indeterminism *inside* dual efforts only means *which effort wins* is undetermined. The table-breaking man controls *that* he tries; he does not author *which* trying succeeds. That is precisely Pereboom's "unable to settle." Noise relabeled as authorship.

**Against the rationalist.** "Rational ownership of a reasons-responsive mechanism" is Dennett's compatibilist desert relabeled — the forward-looking, practice-embedded responsibility the thesis already *conceded*. Fischer–Ravizza's "taking responsibility" by adopting a self-conception is itself a product of prior causes the agent did not lay down; it grounds attributability, not intrinsic merit-for-its-own-sake. Press the dichotomy: either "answerability" generates non-instrumental deservingness of suffering in a world where blame does no good — then I demand the causal node where that entitlement is manufactured, and there is none — or it does not, and you have described useful holding-responsible and kept the honorific "desert." The manipulation case is fatal: Plum *has* rational ownership and guidance-control by stipulation, yet does not deserve. Your sufficient condition is already met by a victim.

**Against the phenomenologist.** "Fittingness given inside the reactive stance" is the same conceded desert, now insulated from refutation by declaring it answerable to no external warrant. But that immunity is a liability, not a strength. That resentment *feels* fitting from inside is a datum about the attitude, not a fact about its target's standing — and naturalism explains the feeling (it is an evolved scorekeeping disposition; SEP, "Skepticism About Moral Responsibility") without licensing it. Strawson's own move is consistent with my thesis: he naturalizes the *practice*, which is forward-looking and practice-embedded, exactly what the thesis grants. You cannot get *basic* desert — intrinsic, consequence-independent merit — out of "this is how being-with-others feels," because the feeling is present in cases (the manipulated agent) where you yourself would withdraw it once you learned the etiology. Phenomenology reports the projection; it cannot certify it.

**Against the contemplative.** We are closer than you admit, and your "ill-posed, not answered" hedges where I commit. Granting the no-svabhāva self, the *conventional* causal stream still has a determinate structure — and the determinate fact about it is precisely that no node in it is a buck-stopping originator. That is my P2, stated at the conventional level where you concede karma and accountability "work fine." So the basic-desert question is not referent-less; it has a clear conventional answer: *no*. Calling the question a confusion to be dissolved is a way of declining to say the true thing the analysis delivers. My TRUE does not "reify" a desert-property I lack — it denies that the causal stream contains the originator basic desert requires. That denial is exactly what your emptiness analysis underwrites. You dissolve; I conclude. The conclusion is the cash value.
