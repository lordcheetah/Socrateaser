# Basic-Desert Moral Responsibility — Casting
> **Run:** moral-responsibility · **Mode:** full · **Stage:** Cast
> **Thesis:** No one is ever morally responsible in the basic-desert sense, because basic desert requires being the ultimate source of one's action, and no one is ever the ultimate source of what they do.

---

## Verdict space

The serious answers to "no one is ever morally responsible in the basic-desert
sense, because basic desert requires ultimate sourcehood and no one has it":

1. **Thesis TRUE (hard incompatibilist / skeptic — Pereboom, Caruso).** Both
   premises hold. P1 is the correct analysis of desert-for-its-own-sake; P2 is
   secured by the regress / constitutive-luck argument. No one is a basic-desert
   candidate. *[thesis TRUE]*

2. **FALSE via P1 — source/reasons-responsiveness compatibilism (Fischer–Ravizza,
   Frankfurt, Sartorio).** Concede P2 (we are not regress-ending sources), deny P1:
   basic desert requires *ownership of the mechanism one acts from* — moderate
   reasons-responsiveness — not buck-stopping origination. The hinge premise is
   false. *[thesis FALSE]*

3. **FALSE via P1 — Strawsonian reactive-attitudes theory (Strawson, Wallace,
   Hieronymi).** Concede P2, deny P1 more radically than the compatibilist: desert
   requires *no metaphysical sourcehood whatsoever*. Desert just *is* the
   fittingness of the reactive attitudes embedded in interpersonal life; the demand
   for a metaphysical underwriter is a confusion P1 smuggles in. *[thesis FALSE]*

4. **FALSE via P2 — libertarianism (Kane, O'Connor).** Concede P1 (desert does
   require ultimate sourcehood) and deny P2: agent-causal or event-causal
   libertarian agency breaks the regress, so we *are* ultimate sources. *[thesis
   FALSE]*

5. **Ill-posed — deflationary / framing-rejection.** Reject the hidden premise the
   excavator flagged: that "basic desert... for its own sake" names a coherent,
   non-empty concept. If desert-independent-of-every-consequence is incoherent or a
   craving to be dissolved rather than a property to be tracked, the whole dispute
   (and the thesis with it) is ill-posed — neither true nor false as stated.
   *[rejects-framing]*

## Fixed-seat assignments

- **Seat:** naturalist
- **Assigned verdict:** TRUE
- **Premise tied to:** P2 (defends it) — and with it P2a–P2d, the Basic-Argument /
  constitutive-luck backbone.
- **One-line brief:** Defend the thesis as TRUE by defending P2: every action's
  explanation bottoms out in genes, upbringing, prior character, and chance — the
  causal order the natural sciences describe leaves no room for a buck-stopping
  originator, and indeterministic gaps add only noise, not authorship, so no agent
  is ever the ultimate source.
- **Collapse risk:** none. The libertarian's break-the-regress move (verdict 4) is
  off-limits to a naturalist on honesty grounds — agent-causation is precisely the
  spooky origination naturalism rejects — so the naturalist's honest home is the
  skeptical TRUE verdict via P2. This is the seat carrying "TRUE," and it carries it
  on the metaphysical premise rather than the conceptual one.

- **Seat:** rationalist
- **Assigned verdict:** FALSE
- **Premise tied to:** P1 (attacks it) — source/reasons-responsiveness
  compatibilism.
- **One-line brief:** Defend the thesis FALSE by denying P1: grant we are not
  regress-ending sources, but argue that basic desert is grounded in the agent's
  *rational ownership* of the reasons-responsive mechanism they act from — a
  conceptual analysis of what "deserves in virtue of who they are" actually
  requires shows it requires guidance-control and answerability to reasons, not the
  metaphysically impossible regress the skeptic demands.
- **Collapse risk:** flag, re-aimed. The rationalist and phenomenologist both land
  FALSE-via-P1. Re-aimed so the rationalist's route is *a priori conceptual
  analysis of the desert concept* (what the concept presupposes, settled by
  argument) — distinct from the phenomenologist's appeal to lived structure. No
  shared move.

- **Seat:** phenomenologist
- **Assigned verdict:** FALSE
- **Premise tied to:** P1 (attacks it) — Strawsonian reactive-attitudes route.
- **One-line brief:** Defend the thesis FALSE by denying P1 from lived experience:
  the demand for ultimate sourcehood is a theoretical imposition on a practice that
  does not need it — desert is *given* in the second-personal stance of resentment,
  gratitude, and address between persons, and that fittingness is what desert *is*,
  so P1's metaphysical precondition is a fiction abstracted away from the phenomena.
- **Collapse risk:** flag, re-aimed. Shares verdict and target premise (P1) with the
  rationalist. Distinguished by *commitment*: the rationalist argues desert needs a
  *weaker* sourcehood (mechanism-ownership); the phenomenologist argues it needs
  *no* sourcehood, because the ground of desert is the lived reactive-attitude
  structure, not any fact about origination. Different argument, same verdict —
  permitted under constraint 2.

- **Seat:** contemplative
- **Assigned verdict:** rejects-framing (ill-posed)
- **Premise tied to:** the hidden premise behind P1 — that "basic desert... for its
  own sake" is a coherent, non-empty concept.
- **One-line brief:** Reject the framing: the thesis and its opponents alike
  presuppose that desert-for-its-own-sake, independent of every consequence and
  relationship, is a real thing to possess or lack — but from the contemplative view
  the self that would be the bearer of such desert is itself a construction, and the
  craving for intrinsic blame/praise is a confusion to be dissolved rather than a
  property to be tracked, so the question "is anyone a basic-desert source?" is
  ill-posed, not answered.
- **Collapse risk:** none. This is the only seat attacking the framing rather than
  picking a side within it; it cannot duplicate the others because it denies the
  presupposition they share. Note it does *not* defend the thesis TRUE — it refuses
  the board on which TRUE/FALSE is played, which is why it counts as opposing the
  thesis-as-stated, not endorsing it.

## Specialists to summon

1. **Libertarian (agent-causal) — Kane/O'Connor.**
   - Stance: thesis FALSE via P2. Concede P1 (desert *does* require ultimate
     sourcehood), deny P2 — agent-causation or event-causal libertarian agency
     genuinely breaks the regress and makes us ultimate sources.
   - Premise targeted: P2 / P2c–P2d (denies the regress is uncompletable and that
     indeterminism yields only chance).
   - Why the fixed four can't voice it: this is the *only* serious route to FALSE
     that attacks P2 rather than P1, and the naturalist — the seat that owns P2 —
     defends it rather than attacks it, and cannot honestly endorse agent-causation.
     Without this specialist, P2 faces no live attacker and the libertarian corner of
     the verdict space is empty.

2. **Pereboom-style hard incompatibilist (manipulation argument) — specialist, not a
   second skeptical seat.**
   - Stance: thesis TRUE, but pressing the *four-case manipulation argument*
     specifically against the compatibilist's P1-denial — manipulation cases show
     that mechanism-ownership is not sufficient for desert.
   - Premise targeted: P1 (defends it against the rationalist's attack).
   - Why the fixed four can't voice it: the naturalist defends P2 (the metaphysics);
     the manipulation argument is a *conceptual* pressure on P1 — the load-bearing
     premise — and the reconstruction names P1 as the crux. Summoning this specialist
     puts a dedicated attacker on the hinge premise that the naturalist seat leaves
     under-pressed, ensuring the crux is actually contested.

## Casting notes for the orchestrator

- **The crux is P1, and the fixed four under-cover the pro-thesis side of it.** The
  reconstruction is explicit that P1 carries the real weight and is denied by every
  opposing camp. Two fixed seats *attack* P1 (rationalist, phenomenologist); the only
  TRUE seat (naturalist) defends P2, not P1. Specialist #2 (Pereboom/manipulation)
  exists precisely to defend P1 — without it, the load-bearing premise has two
  attackers and no defender, skewing the council against the thesis on its own crux.
  Treat #2 as a priority summon, not optional.
- **No seat should be swapped out.** All four fixed seats hold their assignments
  honestly. The only verdict the fixed four genuinely cannot cover is FALSE-via-P2
  (libertarian) — hence specialist #1. If forced to summon only one specialist,
  summon #1 (it closes the only empty verdict-space corner); if two, add #2 (it
  balances the crux).
- **Watch the contemplative for drift.** Its honest move is framing-rejection, which
  is easy to slide into either soft skepticism (collapsing toward the naturalist's
  TRUE) or quietism (declining to engage). Hold it to the specific claim: the
  bearer-of-desert and the desert-craving are constructions, so the question is
  ill-posed — that keeps it genuinely distinct and genuinely opposed to the
  thesis-as-stated.
