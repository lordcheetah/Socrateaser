# Basic-Desert Moral Responsibility — Functional — distinction watchdog
> **Run:** moral-responsibility · **Mode:** full · **Stage:** Functional pass
> **Thesis:** No one is ever morally responsible in the basic-desert sense, because basic desert requires being the ultimate source of one's action, and no one is ever the ultimate source of what they do.

---

DISTINCTION: CONCERNS — 7 issues

## Equivocation

- **Term:** "ultimate source" — sense in P1 (and throughout the thesis/reconstruction):
  the *buck-stopping, regress-ending originator* whose causal explanation does not
  bottom out in factors the agent did not produce; vs. sense imported in several council
  moves: *the reasons-responsive sourcehood compatibilists accept* (the action flows from
  the agent's own mechanism, without regress-ending origination).
  - Where the drift occurs: The rationalist's whole attack on P1 rests on this shift. He
    argues that "ultimate source" in P1, read by its functional role in the desert concept
    ("in virtue of who they are"), *just means* guidance-control / rational ownership —
    i.e., the weaker sense. He then says P1 is false because desert only requires the
    weaker sourcehood. But this is not disambiguation of P1; it is substitution of a
    different term into P1. P1 explicitly pins the buck-stopping sense, and the
    reconstruction and 00-thesis.md both flag precisely this rival sense as the thing P1
    does *not* use. The rationalist's move is dialectically legitimate as a challenge to P1
    (argue the concept does not require the strong sense), but it relies on deploying
    "source" in P1 at the weaker sense, and that makes his attack equivocate on the term
    inside P1's formulation.
  - Does the argument survive disambiguation? Yes — once both senses are kept separate,
    the dispute is real: the rationalist's genuine claim is that "basic desert requires
    ultimate sourcehood" is *false* (not that it uses a different kind of sourcehood than
    stated), because the concept of desert does not in fact require the buck-stopping sense.
    That is a clean conceptual dispute about P1's truth, not an equivocation. The
    equivocation arises only when the rationalist writes as though he is *analyzing the same
    concept of sourcehood* P1 employs, when he is substituting a different one. Flagged for
    the synthesizer to separate these two things: (a) "P1 is false because the desert
    concept does not require buck-stopping sourcehood" (a legitimate attack) vs. (b) "P1
    uses 'ultimate source' in a weaker sense than the thesis claims" (an equivocation
    attempt).

- **Term:** "desert" / "basic desert" — the thesis and reconstruction pin the Pereboom
  sense strictly: intrinsic, backward-looking, consequence-independent desert, such that
  the response is warranted *even in a world where it does no good*. Across the council
  drafts this term drifts in two directions without explicit announcement:
  - **Drift toward attributability / answerability.** The rationalist progressively shifts
    from "basic desert" to "answerability" and "rational ownership" as glosses for what
    desert requires, then concludes P1 is false. Whether this is a legitimate reconceptual-
    ization of what "basic desert" means or a substitution of attributability for basic
    desert is the crux of P1 — but the drift happens without the rationalist announcing it.
    He writes of the agent who "answers for the act and so can deserve in virtue of it" —
    but answerability (the agent owes an account) is one of the senses 00-thesis.md
    explicitly distinguishes from basic desert and says the thesis is *compatible with
    affirming*. Treating answerability as sufficient for basic desert conflates two senses
    the framer separated.
  - **Drift toward Strawsonian fittingness.** The phenomenologist argues that "desert is
    constituted in the lived second-personal structure" and that resentment's "fittingness"
    *is* the desert at issue. But Strawsonian fittingness of reactive attitudes is likewise
    explicitly separated in 00-thesis.md as sense (b), distinct from basic desert. The
    phenomenologist knows this (she acknowledges Hieronymi "does not endorse the basic
    desert / true moral responsibility framing") but still uses "desert" throughout as
    though her account discharges the thesis's explanandum. The verdict token ("thesis is
    FALSE") is issued against a thesis about basic desert using arguments that, if they
    succeed, only establish fittingness-desert. If fittingness ≠ basic desert (as 00-thesis
    pins), the verdict does not reach the thesis.
  - → These drifts do not collapse the argument but they do mean the phenomenologist's
    and rationalist's "FALSE" verdicts may be FALSE on a different thesis than the one
    stated. The synthesizer needs to adjudicate whether (i) the rationalist is arguing basic
    desert has different content than the thesis claims (a genuine P1 dispute) or (ii) he is
    arguing a different concept is the one we actually care about (a subject change).

- **Term:** "responsible" — in the manipulation specialist's draft, "morally responsible"
  is used throughout in careful alignment with Pereboom's basic-desert sense. But in the
  naturalist's attack-round response to the rationalist ("your asserting presupposes the
  very autonomous normativity that grounds rational ownership — and hence desert"), the
  rationalist uses "responsible" to cover both rational-ownership-based answerability and
  the basic-desert sense, in a single sentence that concludes "Self-undermining is fatal."
  The self-undermining argument against the naturalist is: naturalist reasoning presupposes
  normative bindingness → normative bindingness grounds rational ownership → rational
  ownership grounds desert → therefore naturalism is self-undermining. But this only goes
  through if "desert" in the last step is basic desert; if it is only answerability-desert,
  the naturalist can consistently accept it and deny the thesis. The sense of "responsible"
  and "desert" in the concluding clause of the rationalist's attack round is ambiguous
  between the two, and the force of the self-undermining argument depends entirely on which
  sense is meant.
  - → The argument is suggestive but not decisive as stated; it would need "rational
    ownership → *basic* desert" to go through, which is precisely what is in dispute.

- **Term:** "ownership" — used in three distinct senses across the drafts without always
  being flagged:
  1. *Fischer–Ravizza ownership*: the agent "takes responsibility" by adopting a
     self-conception as a fair target of reactive attitudes, based on evidence. A historical
     condition; a normative achievement (rationalist).
  2. *Frankfurt identification*: the agent acts on desires she reflectively endorses via
     second-order volitions. A synchronic condition; not historical (rationalist).
  3. *Causal-origin ownership*: the action is the agent's *own* in the sense that its full
     explanation does not trace to factors the agent did not produce (manipulation
     specialist, naturalist).
  - These three senses are run together in the rationalist's draft: "the agent makes the
    mechanism their own" is presented as though Frankfurt identification and F–R historical
    ownership pick out the same condition, when they differ on whether a historical
    criterion is required and on what it requires. The manipulation specialist exploits this:
    Plum satisfies both Frankfurt and (stipulatively) F–R ownership in Case 1, yet lacks
    desert. The rationalist's response ("Case 1 differs from Case 4 in rational ownership")
    must use sense (1) or (2), not sense (3) — but which one, and does it genuinely
    distinguish the cases? This equivocation on "ownership" is load-bearing in the
    rationalist–manipulation exchange.

## Category bleed

- **Crossing 1:** The phenomenologist establishes that desert *shows up within* the
  reactive stance (phenomenological) and deploys this as a claim that desert *requires* no
  ultimate sourcehood and *is* constituted in the second-personal structure (metaphysical /
  conceptual). The evidence given is entirely phenomenological: "You are responding to the
  contempt itself," "the sense that he has it coming is internal to the attitude itself."
  This establishes how desert *presents* in lived experience. But the conclusion reached —
  "desert is constituted in the second-personal structure, not behind it" — is a
  constitutive/metaphysical claim: it says what desert *is*, not merely how it appears. The
  standard of evidence needed to establish a constitutive claim is conceptual analysis or
  argument about the nature of desert, not description of how it feels from inside the
  reactive stance. The phenomenological evidence underdetermines the metaphysical
  conclusion: desert *appearing* self-sufficient within the stance does not entail that
  desert *is* constituted by and nothing beyond the stance. A realist about desert could
  grant every phenomenological description and insist the appearance is grounded in
  something the stance does not itself contain.
  - → The phenomenologist's move is strongest as a phenomenological claim (the sourcehood
    question does not show up within the experience of resentment) and weakest as deployed
    — a constitutive claim about what desert is. The conceptual/metaphysical conclusion
    needs a different kind of support than she provides.

- **Crossing 2:** The naturalist's attack on the rationalist ("your asserting presupposes
  normative bindingness → ... → therefore self-undermining") is a normative claim deployed
  as though it were a conceptual/metaphysical result. The naturalist's own position is a
  metaphysical thesis (no buck-stopping originator in the causal world). The rationalist's
  self-undermining charge is a normative claim: that rational inquiry *presupposes* the
  bindingness of reasons. When the naturalist anticipates and rebuts this charge — by
  implying the bindingness-of-reasons is itself part of the evolved causal order — he is
  giving an empirical/metaphysical story about the origin of our normative dispositions.
  But such a story does not close the is/ought gap it is trying to close: showing that
  norms *arose* from causes does not establish that they *bind* in the normative sense the
  self-undermining argument requires. The rationalist's charge stands unless the naturalist
  can establish a normative conclusion by a metaphysical route — which is exactly the
  category bleed the naturalist should be most sensitive to.
  - → The exchange is a genuine crossing between normative (do reasons bind?) and
    empirical/metaphysical (what is the causal story of our normative dispositions?). Neither
    draft provides the bridge.

- **Crossing 3:** The rationalist's Korsgaard move — "the authority by which an agent owns
  her action is the authority of practical reason... the is/ought gap cuts *against* the
  skeptic: no story about causal ancestry (an *is*) can dislodge the normative fact that an
  agent... *answers for* the act and so can deserve in virtue of it" — crosses from a
  normative/conceptual claim (practical reason's authority is self-grounding, not derived
  from causal history) to a metaphysical claim (therefore causal ancestry is irrelevant to
  whether desert obtains). The normative premise is established by argument about the
  structure of practical reason (conceptual/normative). But the conclusion — that causal
  ancestry cannot dislodge *basic* desert — is a metaphysical claim about what basic desert
  requires. The is/ought gap the rationalist deploys cuts both ways: if an "is" (causal
  ancestry) cannot defeat a normative fact, then equally a normative fact (practical
  reason's self-grounding authority) cannot by itself settle a metaphysical question (what
  conditions basic desert obtains under). The rationalist needs a bridge from "reasons bind
  normatively regardless of causal history" to "basic desert (the intrinsic, consequence-
  independent merit) attaches wherever reasons bind" — and that bridge is precisely what
  the manipulation argument contests.
  - → The Korsgaard argument is clean in the normative register; the crossing occurs when
    it is used to discharge a metaphysical claim about the conditions of basic desert.

- **Crossing 4:** The contemplative's diagnosis of the entire dispute as "ill-posed"
  imports a metaphysical claim — that the self has no svabhāva (intrinsic own-being), a
  claim Nāgārjuna establishes by the tetralemma and dependent-origination analysis — and
  deploys it as though it settled both the conceptual question (what basic desert requires)
  and the normative question (whether retributive impulses are afflictive fixations to be
  dissolved). The metaphysical emptiness-thesis (niḥsvabhāva) is argued on metaphysical
  grounds. But using it to close normative questions ("the retributive impulse is a
  prapañca — a fixation that generates suffering, to be dissolved") involves a crossing
  from metaphysical analysis to normative prescription. The argument that we *ought* to
  dissolve the basic-desert question rather than answer it requires a normative premise
  (something like: fixations that generate suffering ought to be dissolved) that is not
  established by the emptiness analysis. The contemplative acknowledges the soteriological
  dimension but does not present it as an additional premise — it is embedded in the
  metaphysical analysis, smuggling a normative conclusion.
  - → The contemplative's metaphysical claim (no svabhāva-bearing desert-bearer) is
    independently interesting and does not depend on the normative crossing. But the verdict
    "ill-posed, not answered" is partly normative (the question *should not* be posed in this
    way, *because* posing it perpetuates a fixation). That normative supplement needs its
    own warrant, which the emptiness argument alone does not supply.

- **Crossing 5:** The manipulation specialist establishes by thought-experiment
  (empirical/intuitive) that Case 1 Plum lacks basic desert, then generalizes to the
  conceptual claim that "the most parsimonious explanation of the shared verdict is the
  shared feature [sourcehood absent]" — P1. The move from "our intuitions track sourcehood
  across four cases" to "basic desert conceptually requires sourcehood" is a crossing from
  empirical (what our intuitions are) to conceptual (what the desert concept requires).
  Intuition-tracking can support conceptual analysis but does not settle it: a hard-liner
  can accept the intuition data and argue the concept was always sourcehood-independent
  and our intuitions have been mis-educated. The specialist acknowledges the hard-line
  reply but addresses it by questioning the intuitive price (implausibility of the verdict)
  rather than by defending the concept-from-intuitions inference directly. The argument
  is strongest as an inference-to-best-explanation about *what is doing the work* in our
  desert judgments (empirical/psychological), and weakest when presented as direct
  establishment of a conceptual necessity.
  - → The crossing does not wreck the manipulation argument; it means the conclusion
    strictly should be: "our desert judgments track sourcehood" (empirical), from which we
    can infer (defeasibly) that the concept requires sourcehood (conceptual). The
    conceptual claim needs the additional step that our desert judgments are reliable guides
    to the concept's content — a step that is contested by the hard-liner.

## Clean joints

The following terms and transitions were checked and found stable:

- **"basic desert" in the thesis and reconstruction (00-thesis, 01-reconstruction).**
  The Pereboom definition — intrinsic, backward-looking, consequence-independent —
  is pinned explicitly and held consistently across the framing documents. The "for its
  own sake" clause, the "even in a world where it does no good" test, and the explicit
  separation from senses (a)–(c) are all maintained without drift through 00-thesis and
  01-reconstruction. The framing documents are clean.

- **"ultimate source" in the thesis and reconstruction.** The buck-stopping, regress-
  ending sense is pinned once and the rival compatibilist sense is explicitly named and
  excluded. Both P1 and P2 in the reconstruction use the same sense. The reconstruction
  itself flags the univocity assumption as a hidden-premise concern. No equivocation in
  the framing layer.

- **The manipulation specialist's use of "basic desert."** Throughout the four-case
  argument, the specialist holds "basic desert" firmly to the Pereboom sense. The
  definition is quoted, the consequence-independence clause is applied, and the argument
  is targeted only at that sense. Stable.

- **The libertarian's concession of P1.** The libertarian explicitly accepts the
  buck-stopping sense of "ultimate source" and accepts P1 on that reading. His attack on
  P2 via SFAs and agent-causation uses "ultimate source" in the same buck-stopping sense
  throughout. No drift in his use of either term. The Kane UR condition as quoted also
  matches the thesis's pinned sense.

- **The naturalist's argument for P2.** The naturalist defends P2 (no buck-stopping
  origination) on causal grounds and maintains "ultimate source" in the pinned sense
  throughout. His denial of Dennett is explicitly keyed to the distinction between basic
  desert and forward-looking responsibility. The term does not shift in his main case,
  only in the attack-round exchange noted above.

- **"P1 is the conceptual premise / P2 is the metaphysical premise."** Every council
  member correctly identifies which premise they are attacking. No one conflates the
  conceptual premise (what the desert concept requires) with the metaphysical premise
  (whether any agent meets it). The debate map is structurally sound even where the
  arguments cross categories.

- **The contemplative's two-truths framework.** The distinction between the conventional
  level (karma, accountability, the chariot rolls) and the ultimate level (no svabhāva)
  is held consistently within the contemplative's own draft. She does not let the ultimate
  level dissolve conventional accountability; the two-truths apparatus functions as
  intended. The crossing flagged above (normative supplement needed) does not arise from
  equivocation but from an under-argued bridge premise.
