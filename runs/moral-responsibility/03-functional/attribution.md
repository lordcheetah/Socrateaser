### 1. Galen Strawson — Basic Argument structure and key quotes

- **Voice:** Naturalist
- **The claim:** "To be truly responsible for what you intentionally do, you must be responsible for the way you are, 'for what you intentionally do flows necessarily from how you are.'" Also: "you must have intentionally brought it about that you are the way you are, and this is impossible." Also: "It is undeniable that one is the way one is as a result of one's heredity and experience," these are "things for which one cannot be held to be in any way responsible." Also: "it is absurd to suppose that indeterministic or random factors, for which one is in no way responsible, can in themselves contribute to one's being truly or ultimately responsible for how one is."
- **What the source says:** The Sloww.co summary of Strawson's paper (drawing from the Kentucky PDF) confirms all four phrases. Exact wording: "What you intentionally do, given the circumstances in which you (believe you) find yourself, flows necessarily from how you are." "It is undeniable that one is the way one is as a result of one's heredity and experience." "These are clearly things for which one can't be held to be in any way responsible." "It's absurd to suppose that indeterministic or random factors, for which one is in no way responsible, can in themselves contribute to one's being truly or ultimately responsible for how one is." The naturalist paraphrases the second premise ("you must have intentionally brought it about that you are the way you are, and this is impossible") — not a direct quote marker in the original but an accurate characterization of the regress step Strawson describes as requiring that one "must have consciously and explicitly chosen to be the way one is." The Basic Argument structure (nothing can be *causa sui*; true responsibility requires being *causa sui*; therefore nothing is truly responsible) is confirmed by multiple sources including the SEP.
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE
- **Note:** Minor stylistic differences from the exact text ("It's absurd" vs "it is absurd"; "can't" vs "cannot") are normalized paraphrase rather than distortion. The naturalist's attribution of the *causa sui* formulation to Strawson is accurate. No dropped qualifications or reversed conclusions.

### 2. Pereboom — Four-case manipulation argument (case details)

- **Voice:** Specialist-manipulation (primary); Naturalist (secondary, citing the four cases)
- **The claim (manipulation specialist):** Case 1: "Neuroscientists, by radio-like technology, locally manipulate Plum's reasoning process moment-to-moment so that it is *strongly egoistic*, deterministically producing the decision to kill. The manipulation works through his ordinary reasons-responsive faculties." Case 2: "The same neuroscientists program Plum at the start of his life so that his reasoning is regularly, but not exceptionlessly, egoistic, and this programming causally determines the present decision." Case 3: "The 'rigorous training practices' of Plum's home and community, acting before he could resist, instill the same egoistic character." Case 4: ordinary determinism.
- **What the source says:** The Philosophical Disquisitions summary (and search-confirmed details from the 2014 version) confirm: Case 1 uses "radio-like technology" and neuroscientists "pressing a button just before he begins to reason," producing a "strongly egoistic reasoning process." Case 2: "programmed at the beginning of his life." Case 3: community training, no neuroscientists. Case 4: ordinary determinism. All four case descriptions broadly match.
- **Verdict:** OVERSTATED (Case 1 detail)
- **Note:** The manipulation specialist describes Case 1 as involving manipulation "moment-to-moment." The source describes the neuroscientists pressing a button *once* "just before he begins to reason," producing a deterministic neural state — not a continuous real-time feed. "Moment-to-moment" suggests ongoing intervention throughout deliberation; the case involves a single pre-deliberation intervention. The naturalist's attack round also uses "moment-to-moment" language, picking this up from the specialist. The distortion is small but directionally significant: the Plum cases are meant to parallel the structure of deterministic causation, and overstating the intrusiveness of Case 1 makes the contrast with Case 4 look larger than Pereboom designs it to be.

### 3. Pereboom — Basic desert definition

- **Voice:** Manipulation specialist and Naturalist
- **The claim:** "an agent is morally responsible 'in such a way that she would deserve to be blamed if she understood that it was morally wrong,' with the desert attaching to the act *itself*, independent of any consequence (Pereboom 2014, via SEP)." Also: "deserve it 'just because she has performed the action, given an understanding of its moral status,' not by way of any consequentialist or contractualist payoff."
- **What the source says:** The SEP entry on Skepticism About Moral Responsibility confirms the exact text: "For an agent to be morally responsible for an action in this sense is for it to be hers in such a way that she would deserve to be blamed if she understood that it was morally wrong, and she would deserve to be praised if she understood that it was morally exemplary." The "just because she has performed the action, given an understanding of its moral status, and not, for example, merely by virtue of consequentialist or contractualist considerations" formulation is also confirmed by multiple search results.
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE

### 4. Pereboom — "Unable to settle" (event-causal libertarianism)

- **The claim:** With an undetermined decision, the agent is "left unable to *settle* whether a decision/action occurs and hence cannot have the control in action required for moral responsibility" (attributed to Pereboom, via SEP "Skepticism About Moral Responsibility").
- **What the source says:** The SEP entry confirms this: "hard incompatibilists maintain agents are 'unable to settle whether a decision/action occurs and hence cannot have the control in action required for moral responsibility.'" The Philosophical Disquisitions entry on the Disappearing Agent Objection also confirms Pereboom's argument that "nothing settles whether the decision occurs, and so the agent does not settle whether the decision occurs."

### 5. Neil Levy — "Luck pincer"

- **Voice:** Naturalist
- **The claim:** "every morally significant act is subject to *constitutive luck* (the agent's uncontrolled endowment) or *present luck* (circumstantial factors at the moment of decision), or both, and 'either way, luck undermines moral responsibility since it undermines responsibility-level control'" (SEP, "Skepticism About Moral Responsibility," citing Levy 2011).
- **What the source says:** The SEP confirms the luck pincer framework and the distinction between constitutive luck and present luck. Search results confirm Levy's "Hard Luck" (2011) advances this argument. The general formulation — "either way, luck undermines free will and moral responsibility" — appears in the secondary literature. The specific exact phrase "since it undermines responsibility-level control" is paraphrase-level language from the SEP secondary synthesis (Caruso's defense of the luck pincer uses "responsibility-level control"), not a verbatim Levy quote being misattributed. The naturalist attributes the phrase to "SEP, citing Levy 2011," which is an accurate description of the sourcing level.
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE
- **Note:** The phrase "responsibility-level control" is a standard term in the skeptical literature (used by both Levy and Caruso); attributing it to the SEP's account of Levy is technically correct, though it risks reading like a direct Levy quotation when it is a summary formulation. No substantive distortion.

- **Verdict:** ACCURATE

### 9. Fischer and Ravizza — Guidance control, regulative control, taking responsibility

- **Voice:** Rationalist
- **The claim:** Guidance control has two components: (i) the mechanism must be the agent's own, and (ii) the mechanism must be moderately responsive to reasons. Guidance control "does not require alternative possibilities or regulative control." The ownership condition: an agent "takes responsibility" by coming to see themselves as "a fair target of the reactive attitudes" in a way "appropriately based on the evidence."
- **What the source says:** All confirmed. The PhilPapers precis and search results confirm guidance control = ownership + moderate reasons-responsiveness; that guidance control does not require regulative control/alternative possibilities; and the "taking responsibility" historical condition requiring the agent to "see themselves as a legitimate target of attitudes like gratitude and resentment, praise and blame" through a process appropriately based on evidence. The Fischer/Ravizza 1998 book (Cambridge UP) is the correct source.
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE

### 10. Harry Frankfurt — Second-order volitions, personhood, identification

- **What the source says:** The 1971 paper is confirmed as published in the *Journal of Philosophy* 68:5–20. The second-order volition structure and the person/wanton distinction are correctly reported. Frankfurt's view that the source of normative authority need not trace back to ultimate origination is an accurate characterization of his position — his compatibilist account grounds freedom in mesh/identification, not causal pedigree.
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE

### 12. Robert Kane — Ultimate Responsibility (UR), self-forming actions (SFAs), businesswoman example, dual efforts, table analogy

- **Voice:** Specialist-libertarian
- **The claim:** "an agent is ultimately responsible for an action only if the agent is responsible for anything that is a *sufficient reason, cause, or motive* for the action's occurring" (UR condition, Kane 1996). SFAs occur in moments of motivational conflict — "Kane's businesswoman, late for a meeting, who sees an assault and is torn between the moral pull to stop and the prudential pull to rush on." "Plural voluntary control via dual efforts of will." "Whichever way the choice resolves, it is the success of *something the agent was actively trying to do.*" Table-breaking analogy: "a man trying to break a glass table by pounding it succeeds in breaking it *through his own effort* even if the precise molecular path was indeterministic."
- **What the source says:** The businesswoman example is confirmed — Kane's example involves a businesswoman choosing between helping someone (stopping at an assault) or going on to a meeting; the "late for a meeting / sees an assault" description is accurate. The UR condition is confirmed per SEP. "Plural voluntary control" and "dual efforts" are confirmed. The glass table analogy: the SEP entry confirms "a man hits a glass tabletop attempting to shatter it" and Kane argues that if the man succeeds, "he may well be responsible for breaking the tabletop" even under indeterminism.
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE

### 14. Nāgārjuna — MMK Chapter 18, verses 18.1, 18.2, 18.4, 18.5

- **Voice:** Contemplative
- **The claim (verse texts):** The contemplative quotes four verses:
  - 18.1: "If the aggregates were self, it would be possessed of arising and decaying. If it were other than the aggregates, it would not have the characteristics of the aggregates."
  - 18.2: "If the self did not exist, where could what is mine exist? In order to pacify self and what is mine, grasping I and grasping mine can exist no more."
  - 18.4: "When one ceases thinking of inner and outer things as self and mine, clinging will come to a stop. Through that ceasing, birth will cease."
  - 18.5: "Through the ceasing of action and affliction, there is freedom. Action and affliction [come] from thoughts and they from fixations. Fixations are stopped by emptiness."
  The contemplative cites "Wisdomlib's edition of the MMK" for these verses.
- **What the source says:** The Wisdomlib page for MMK Chapter 18 confirms all four verses as quoted — the contemplative has reproduced the Wisdomlib text accurately. However, Garfield's 1995 Oxford translation (the standard academic reference, retrieved from Archive.org) renders these verses differently:
  - 18.1 (Garfield): "If the self were the aggregates, / It would have arising and ceasing (as properties)..."
  - 18.2 (Garfield): "If there were no self, / Where would the self's (properties) be? / From the pacification of the self and what belongs to it, / One abstains from grasping onto 'I' and 'mine.'"
  - 18.4 (Garfield): "When views of 'I' and 'mine' are extinguished, / Whether with respect to the internal or external, / The appropriator ceases. / This having ceased, birth ceases."
  - 18.5 (Garfield): "Action and misery having ceased, there is nirvana. / Action and misery come from conceptual thought. / This comes from mental fabrication. / Fabrication ceases through emptiness."
  Wisdomlib uses a different translation from Garfield. The chapter is titled "Investigation of Self and Things" in Wisdomlib, matching the contemplative's usage.
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE (as cited to Wisdomlib, not Garfield)
- **Note:** The contemplative correctly identifies the Wisdomlib source for the verse text. The translations diverge non-trivially from Garfield's: most consequentially for verse 18.5, Wisdomlib gives "freedom" where Garfield gives "nirvana," and "fixations" vs Garfield's "mental fabrication/fabrication." The contemplative's philosophical argument turns on "Fixations are stopped by emptiness" (Wisdomlib) and glosses "fixations" as *prapañca/vikalpa* — Garfield's "fabrication" (also a standard rendering of *prapañca*) would support the same gloss. No substantive distortion of the philosophical content results from using the Wisdomlib translation, but the contemplative also separately cites Garfield for the anti-nihilist commentary point (that emptiness "does not undermine phenomena's ability to do stuff, and to cause effects") — this Garfield quote could not be verified through available sources.

## Summary of concerns

1. **CONCERN 1 (Claim 2 — Pereboom Case 1):** The manipulation specialist describes Case 1 manipulation as "moment-to-moment," but the source describes a single button-press before reasoning begins. The distortion is directional: it overstates Case 1's intrusiveness relative to ordinary determination, which is the precise comparison the argument is designed to minimize. The naturalist picks up this "moment-to-moment" language in the attack round.

2. **CONCERN 2 (Claim 14 — MMK verse translations):** The contemplative's verse quotations are from the Wisdomlib translation, correctly cited. Compared against Garfield's Oxford translation (the standard academic reference), verse 18.4 reads "the appropriator ceases" vs Wisdomlib's "clinging will come to a stop," and verse 18.5 reads "nirvana" vs Wisdomlib's "freedom." The contemplative's philosophical commentary on "fixations" as *prapañca* is consistent with both translations. No misattribution of Garfield, but the two translations diverge, and readers using Garfield as the reference text will find the verses look different from what is quoted.

3. **CONCERN 3 (Claim 15 — Garfield commentary quote):** The specific Garfield quote — "does not undermine phenomena's ability to do stuff, and to cause effects" — cannot be verified against the primary text. The doctrine expressed is standard Madhyamaka and consistent with Garfield's approach, but the quotation marks elevate it beyond what available sources support.

4. **CONCERN 4 (Claim 8 — Darwall exact wording):** The phenomenologist places quotation marks around "fellow participant who can be expected to acknowledge it" when attributing to Darwall. The conceptual content is confirmed, but the exact phrasing could not be verified word-for-word from the accessible PDF (which returned binary). The attribution of the *concept* is accurate; the exact quoted phrase is potentially editorial.

5. **CONCERN 5 (Claim 6 — "luck swallows all" provenance):** The phrase "luck swallows all" is attributed to Caruso in *Just Deserts*. This is defensible — the phrase appears in that book — but search results note that Galen Strawson used "luck swallows everything" earlier. The attribution to Caruso is not fabricated, but the independent coinage may be Strawson's. Readers may want to note this provenance.
