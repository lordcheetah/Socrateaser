# Basic-Desert Moral Responsibility — Crux map
> **Run:** moral-responsibility · **Mode:** full · **Stage:** Synthesis
> **Thesis:** No one is ever morally responsible in the basic-desert sense, because basic desert requires being the ultimate source of one's action, and no one is ever the ultimate source of what they do.

---

## The crux

Every position agrees on the metaphysics — no one is a regress-ending, buck-stopping
originator (P2 holds) — and divides at a single buried premise: **HP4, the
self-authorship requirement.** To count as the "ultimate source" that basic desert
requires, must one be (at least in part) the author of the very character, reasons,
and will one acts from — or does it suffice that the action issues, here and now, from
a will that is the agent's own in some non-historical sense (reflective endorsement,
reasons-responsiveness, second-personal address)? The skeptic and libertarian build
HP4 into "ultimate source"; the rationalist and phenomenologist refuse it. **The
stated premise P1 is the visible hinge, but HP4 is the joint inside P1 on which the
whole dispute actually turns** — and beneath even that lies the question of whether
"basic desert" names something determinate enough to require anything at all (the
contemplative's and the deflationist's challenge).

A second, partly independent fork sits at HP3: even granting the skeptic's analysis,
does it reach what ordinary blame tracks, or only a philosophers' reconstruction of
desert? That fork decides whether the dispute is a discovery about practice or a
verbal one.

## Position map

- **Naturalist (thesis TRUE, via P2).** Verdict: no one is ever basic-desert
  responsible. Non-negotiable commitment: causal closure — there is one natural causal
  order and no node in it is inherited from nothing prior; indeterminism adds noise,
  not authorship. To change his mind he would have to admit either a genuinely
  uncaused-but-authored act (agent-causation) or that desert never needed sourcehood
  at all — and the latter he treats as a verbal concession, not a refutation. Note: he
  rests on P2 and treats P1/HP4 as mere definition of "for its own sake," which the
  manipulation specialist flags as leaving the load-bearing premise *asserted, not
  earned*.

- **Rationalist (thesis FALSE, via P1 — denies HP4).** Verdict: P1 is false because
  the desert concept's actual content is *rational ownership of a reasons-responsive
  mechanism*, an answerability exercised now, not inherited. Non-negotiable
  commitment: normativity is self-standing — the bindingness of one's own reasons is a
  normative fact no causal-ancestry story can touch (the is/ought gap cuts against the
  skeptic). To change his mind he would have to concede that answerability without
  authorship cannot generate *intrinsic, consequence-independent* desert — i.e., that
  Plum-in-Case-1 (who has rational ownership by stipulation) settles the matter against
  him.

- **Phenomenologist (thesis FALSE, via P1 — denies HP4 more radically).** Verdict: P1
  inverts the order of grounding; desert is *constituted in* the lived second-personal
  stance of resentment and address, needing no sourcehood whatsoever. Non-negotiable
  commitment: the participant stance is bedrock — you cannot derive the "you" of
  address from any third-person inventory of causes, and the practice "neither calls
  for nor permits" external warrant. To change her mind she would have to grant that
  the fittingness of a reactive attitude is a fact about the *attitude*, not about its
  target's standing — the naturalist's and manipulation specialist's charge that her
  own argument runs *on* the reactive attitudes it claims desert can do without.

- **Contemplative (rejects the framing — ill-posed).** Verdict: the question has no
  determinate referent; the self that would bear intrinsic desert is a construction
  (niḥsvabhāva), and the craving to settle who deserves what "for its own sake" is
  itself the confusion to dissolve. Non-negotiable commitment: the two-truths
  structure — conventionally accountability works fine, ultimately no own-being
  desert-bearer exists. To change her mind she would have to grant that "basic desert"
  picks out a coherent property that could be possessed or lacked (HP2) — which is
  exactly what she denies. She does *not* endorse the thesis: answering "no" still
  reifies the empty concept.

- **Specialist — Libertarian (thesis FALSE, via P2 — accepts HP4).** Verdict: P1 is
  true, HP4 and all; P2 is false because Kanean self-forming actions or O'Connor's
  agent-causation genuinely break the regress. Non-negotiable commitment: there is a
  third option beyond determined-and-inherited / undetermined-and-random — the agent as
  substance-cause or as the locus of dual efforts of will. To change his mind he would
  have to concede that indeterminism only relocates luck (which effort wins is settled
  by nothing the agent controls) — Pereboom's "unable to settle." He is the mirror of
  the rationalist/phenomenologist: he grants the skeptic the concept and fights the
  fact.

- **Specialist — Manipulation/Pereboom (thesis TRUE, via P1 — defends HP4).** Verdict:
  the four-case argument earns P1 by exhibiting an agent (Plum, Case 1) who meets every
  compatibilist ownership condition yet lacks desert, so sourcehood is the only feature
  that can explain the verdict. Non-negotiable commitment: the no-relevant-difference
  inference — there is no principled, desert-relevant difference between the manipulated
  agent and the ordinarily determined one. To change his mind he would have to accept
  either the hard-line reply (manipulated Plum *does* deserve) or a soft-line difference
  that does not equally indict the determined agent.

## Convergence

The shared ground is far larger than the verdicts suggest:

- **P2 is conceded by everyone except the libertarian.** Naturalist, rationalist,
  phenomenologist, contemplative, and the Pereboom specialist all agree no one is a
  regress-ending originator. The metaphysics of agency is *not* where the fixed council
  divides.
- **Everyone brackets the same three senses.** All parties accept that forward-looking,
  Strawsonian-reactive, and answerability/attributability responsibility survive. The
  dispute is sealed off to *basic* desert alone. This is what lets self-application
  SURVIVE: the skeptic is answerable for the argument without being basic-desert
  responsible for it.
- **Naturalist and contemplative nearly touch:** both say the causal stream contains no
  buck-stopping originator. They split only on whether to *answer* the question (no) or
  *dissolve* it (no referent).
- **Rationalist and phenomenologist agree on the verdict (P1 false) and the target
  (HP4), differing only on the replacement:** a *weaker* sourcehood (mechanism-ownership)
  vs. *no* sourcehood (lived address).
- **Manipulation specialist and contemplative share a method** (empty every positive
  desert-maker) and split only on whether anything remains to be empty.

So the real estate in dispute is narrow: granting all the metaphysics and all three
conceded senses, does *intrinsic, consequence-independent* desert require that the
agent authored the self it acts from?

## What the functional roles changed

- **Hidden-premise excavator relocates the crux from P1-as-stated to HP4.** This is the
  decisive structural move. P1 ("basic desert requires ultimate sourcehood") is not one
  claim but two glued together: a requirement *that* there be sourcehood, and a covert
  specification of *what* sourcehood is (HP4: self-authorship of one's character). Every
  compatibilist denial of P1 is, at bottom, a denial of HP4 — and the libertarian's
  acceptance of P1 is acceptance of HP4. The map's center of gravity is therefore HP4,
  not the surface premise. **The crux is P1 only because HP4 lives inside it.**

- **Distinction-watchdog (CONCERNS) shows part of the rationalist/phenomenologist
  attack is verbal — and isolates where it is not.** "Desert" drifts toward
  answerability (rationalist) and toward Strawsonian fittingness (phenomenologist),
  both of which the thesis *explicitly concedes* as separate senses. If their FALSE
  verdicts merely establish answerability-desert or fittingness-desert, they do not
  reach the thesis, which is about basic desert. The watchdog's resolution, which the
  map adopts: there are two readings, and only one survives disambiguation as a genuine
  dispute. (a) "P1 is false because the *basic*-desert concept does not require
  self-authorship" is a real attack on HP4 and stays on the board. (b) "The concept we
  actually care about is answerability/fittingness, not basic desert" is a subject
  change that leaves the thesis standing. The rationalist's "rational ownership →
  *basic* desert" step and the phenomenologist's "fittingness *is* desert" step are
  precisely the bridges the manipulation argument contests — so the surviving,
  non-verbal crux is exactly HP4: *can a non-historical ownership condition ground
  intrinsic desert?* The verbal residue is real and trims the apparent force of two
  FALSE verdicts, but it does not dissolve the dispute.

- **Self-application SURVIVES**, and this *protects the skeptic's two TRUE-side voices*
  from the cheapest defeat. The answerability/basic-desert distinction means the skeptic
  is not refuted by the act of arguing. This neither moves the crux nor settles it; it
  removes a distraction and confirms the four-senses framing is load-bearing, not
  decorative.

- **Counterexamples: none decisive, two dangerous, both pointing at the crux.**
  Fischer–Ravizza's Marcus (Case 2 in that report) makes P1's falsity a live conceptual
  claim, not a mere intuition clash — it forces the skeptic to *argue* HP4 rather than
  stipulate it. Eichmann (Case 3) extracts a costly bullet-bite: the skeptic must call
  the firmest moral intuition we have a misleading reactive attitude. Neither refutes
  the thesis; both raise its price and confirm P1/HP4 as the load-bearing joint. Kane's
  SFA is the only live attack on P2 and remains an unresolved metaphysical standoff.

- **Attribution (CONCERNS) shifts weight off two TRUE-side and one rejects-framing
  flourish, not off any crux-bearing claim.**
  - The **Pereboom Case 1 "moment-to-moment"** overstatement (picked up by the
    naturalist) inflates the contrast between the manipulated and the determined agent —
    exactly the contrast the four-case argument needs to *minimize*. The accurate case
    (a single pre-deliberation intervention) makes Case 1 look *more* like ordinary
    determinism, which actually *helps* the no-relevant-difference inference. So the
    overstatement is a rhetorical liability, not a logical one: corrected, the
    manipulation argument is, if anything, marginally stronger, but the specialist
    should not lean on intrusiveness. Net: the dramatic framing carries less weight; the
    structural argument carries the same.
  - The **contemplative's Wisdomlib-vs-Garfield translation divergence** (18.5
    "freedom" vs "nirvana"; "fixations" vs "fabrication") and the **unverifiable
    Garfield commentary quote** mean her *textual* authority is slightly softer than it
    reads. The philosophical content (prapañca, two-truths, niḥsvabhāva) is sound and
    survives either translation, so her framing-rejection position is undamaged; but the
    specific quoted phrase should not be treated as established text. This trims
    ornament, not argument.
  - Minor provenance caveats (Darwall exact wording, "luck swallows all" possibly
    Strawson's coinage) do not move any position.

  Net effect: the attribution concerns *cosmetically* weaken the most rhetorically
  vivid presentations on the TRUE side and the contemplative side, but they leave the
  crux untouched — because the crux rides on HP4, a conceptual commitment no
  attribution check can verify or refute.

## What it would take to settle it

This bottoms out in a **conceptual decision that argument can sharpen but not compel.**
Three layers, in order of tractability:

1. **The metaphysical layer (P2) is nearly settled by argument** and is *not* the live
   crux for the fixed council. The regress/luck argument is granted by all but the
   libertarian. The libertarian's denial via agent-causation or SFAs is a standing
   metaphysical disagreement that turns on whether indeterminism can constitute
   authorship — itself a conceptual stalemate (does "the agent caused it" name a
   mechanism or a gap?), not an empirical one. No experiment decides it.

2. **The crux layer (HP4) is a conceptual decision, not a fact.** Whether *intrinsic,
   consequence-independent* desert requires self-authorship of one's character cannot
   be read off the world or compelled by analysis, because — as the counterexample and
   distinction reports jointly show — competent speakers' intuitions diverge exactly
   here (Eichmann pulls toward HP4; Marcus pulls against it), and each side can
   redescribe the other's cases. The manipulation argument does not prove HP4; it makes
   HP4 *the best explanation of a pattern of intuitions* a hard-liner can rationally
   reject. So this fork bottoms out in which intuition one treats as fixed: that
   obvious manipulation excuses (→ HP4, thesis TRUE) or that ordinary rational agency
   suffices (→ HP4 false, thesis FALSE). Nothing in the dispute can force that choice.

3. **The deepest layer (HP2/HP3) may dissolve the question rather than answer it.** If
   "basic desert" is incoherent or empty (contemplative, deflationist), or if it is a
   philosophers' reconstruction that ordinary blame does not track (Vargas's HP3), then
   the universal thesis is either trivially true or simply beside the point for actual
   moral practice. This is not settleable by argument *within* the dispute; it is a
   prior decision about whether the dispute is well-formed.

Honest verdict: **a standing disagreement that bottoms out in a conceptual commitment
(HP4) about what intrinsic desert requires, framed by a prior decision (HP2/HP3) about
whether "basic desert" is a real and practice-relevant concept at all.** The
metaphysics is largely shared; the fight is over the meaning and reach of a concept,
and meanings of this kind are decided, not discovered.

## For the author

The author is a pragmatist — and the framing has deliberately *bracketed the
pragmatist's home ground*. Forward-looking, consequentialist, and practice-embedded
responsibility are all conceded by every voice; the thesis denies only the one sense a
pragmatist has least reason to want, *desert for its own sake in a world where it does
no good*. So the pragmatist's view does not sit at any single seat on this map — it
sits **off the board the thesis defines**, agreeing in advance with the conceded
senses and indifferent to the residue.

That is the author's exposure. The map offers two doors and the author must pick one,
because pragmatism cannot stay neutral once the crux is named:

- **Door 1 (deflationary, HP3/HP2):** Side with the contemplative's structure (minus
  its metaphysics) and Vargas's revisionism — "basic desert" is a philosophers' artifact
  that ordinary blame never tracked, so the whole dispute is idle for anything we do.
  This is the natural pragmatist move, and it makes the thesis *true-but-uninteresting*
  rather than false. The single premise the author must then defend is **HP3**: that
  conceptual analysis of consequence-independent desert does *not* reach what folk blame
  actually tracks. If HP3 fails — if ordinary blame really is committed to
  backward-looking desert (the Eichmann intuition says it might be) — the pragmatist
  cannot wave the dispute away; it reaches into the practices the pragmatist cares about.

- **Door 2 (functionalist vindication):** Side with the rationalist/phenomenologist —
  desert just *is* the answerability or fittingness embedded in our practices, and that
  is desert enough. This keeps the word and the practice. But the distinction-watchdog's
  warning lands squarely on the author here: this may be a *subject change*, not a
  refutation, salvaging the honorific "desert" by quietly redefining it as the
  forward-looking/answerability senses the thesis already granted. The single premise
  the author must defend to keep this door is the one the manipulation argument
  attacks: **that a non-historical ownership condition (answerability, reasons-
  responsiveness) can ground *intrinsic* desert — HP4-denial — and that Plum in Case 1,
  who has that ownership by stipulation and still seems to deserve nothing, does not
  refute it.**

Plainly: the author cannot keep both the pragmatist's indifference and a stake in the
outcome. Either basic desert is idle (defend HP3) or it is vindicated by function
(defend HP4-denial against the manipulation case). Pragmatism's instinct points at
Door 1; Door 1's cost is the Eichmann intuition, and the author should not flatter
themselves that it is cheap.

### One-line crux
All six positions agree the metaphysics is shared — no one authors the self they act
from (P2) — and fork on **HP4, the self-authorship requirement buried inside P1:
whether intrinsic, consequence-independent desert requires that one be the source of
one's own character, or whether a non-historical ownership of one's will suffices.**

### Settleability
**Not settleable by argument or empirical fact — a standing disagreement that bottoms
out in a conceptual decision (HP4) about what "basic desert" requires, sitting on top
of a prior decision (HP2/HP3) about whether the concept is coherent and
practice-relevant at all.** Argument can locate the fork, raise the price of each side
(Eichmann, Marcus), and clear away verbal and self-refutation distractions, but it
cannot compel the choice of which intuition to hold fixed.
